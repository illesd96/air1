# Hooks for A 32 Bussard

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Messerschmitt missed a deadline in 1930 — and Focke-Wulf built its first airliner because of it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1930, a Norwegian airline called NOBA needed planes fast. Messerschmitt had promised delivery. Messerschmitt didn't deliver. Focke-Wulf grabbed the contract, stretched their A 20 design, jammed in an engine more than twice as powerful, and shipped two Bussards in record time. They became Focke-Wulf's first commercial aircraft in airline service — and launched a designer named Heinrich Focke onto a path that ended at the Fw 200 Condor and the first practical helicopter.
