The Focke-Wulf A 33 Sperber was a scaled-down sibling of the Bussard, built for an aviation tier that doesn't exist anymore: the German air-taxi market. Three were built, one ended up at Luft Hansa as a second-hand bushplane, and then the entire category disappeared into Lufthansa's standardized fleet.
