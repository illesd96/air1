# Hooks for Airbus A310

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Airbus chopped fifteen feet off the A300, threw out the flight engineer, and accidentally invented the modern two-pilot wide-body.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

1978. The A300 was selling but it still needed three pilots in the cockpit. Airbus took the same wing, shortened the fuselage by fifteen feet, designed a fully digital glass cockpit, and certified the first wide-body operable by two pilots only. The A310 was the proof of concept for everything Airbus has done since. So why does no one remember it?
