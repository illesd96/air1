# Hooks for Airbus A340

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Airbus built it with four engines because ETOPS regulations wouldn't let twins cross oceans yet — and that decision killed it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1987, Airbus launched two widebodies at once: the twin A330 and the quadjet A340. The A340 existed for one reason — ETOPS rules in the 1980s made twin-engine ocean crossings impractical. Then ETOPS-180 happened, the 777 arrived, fuel got expensive, and the A340 became Airbus's most painful learning experience. Lufthansa retired the last passenger A340 in 2024.
