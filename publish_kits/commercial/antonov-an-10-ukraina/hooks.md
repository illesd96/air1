# Hooks for Antonov An-10 Ukraina

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Soviet airliner whose wings literally ripped off mid-flight — and Aeroflot grounded the type forever.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

On May 18, 1972, Aeroflot Flight 1491's Antonov An-10 lost both wings in cruise over Kharkov. 122 died. The cause: fatigue cracks the Soviets hadn't been inspecting. The An-10 — Antonov's first major airliner, sharing an airframe with the An-12 military transport — was retired from Aeroflot within months, its 'Ukraina' branding scrubbed from Soviet aviation.
