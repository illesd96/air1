The Antonov An-10 Ukraina was the Soviet Union's first major Antonov airliner — a high-wing four-engine turboprop sharing an airframe with the An-12 military cargo plane. The May 18, 1972 Kharkov disaster, where both wings tore off in cruise due to undetected fatigue cracks, killed 122 and forced Aeroflot to retire the entire type.
