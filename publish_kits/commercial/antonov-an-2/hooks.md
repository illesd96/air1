# Hooks for Antonov An-2

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It's a Soviet biplane with no stall speed listed in the manual — because it physically can't stall.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Antonov's An-2 first flew in 1947. Its manual famously lists the minimum airspeed as a dash, because the plane simply settles into a parachute-like descent when slow enough. It was produced continuously until 2001 — the longest production run of any aircraft type — and remains in service across former Soviet airspace as a workhorse for crop dusting, paratroop training, and Arctic resupply.
