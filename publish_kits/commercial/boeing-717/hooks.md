# Hooks for Boeing 717

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Boeing's smallest jet was actually a McDonnell Douglas plane they inherited in a merger — and they kept the Douglas DNA inside the cockpit.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When Boeing bought McDonnell Douglas in 1997, they got a half-finished airliner called the MD-95. They could have killed it. Instead, they slapped a Boeing 717 badge on the tail, kept the Douglas-style cockpit and rear-mounted engines, and sold 156 of them — the last DC-9 family member ever built. AirTran loved it. Hawaiian still flies it. So why did Boeing quietly shut the line in 2006?
