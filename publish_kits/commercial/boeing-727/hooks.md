# Hooks for Boeing 727

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

There's exactly one airliner D.B. Cooper could parachute out the back of — and that's because of how the 727 was built.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

On November 24, 1971, a man calling himself Dan Cooper hijacked Northwest Orient Flight 305, took $200,000, and parachuted out the rear ventral airstair of a Boeing 727 over the Pacific Northwest. He was never found. Boeing installed the Cooper Vane on every 727 afterward to prevent it from happening again. The 727 trijet did things no other airliner could — and that was the problem.
