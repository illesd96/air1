# Hooks for Boeing 767

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

This is the plane that ended the rule against flying twin-engine jets across oceans.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Before 1985, the FAA forbade twin-engine airliners from flying more than 60 minutes from a diversion airport. Then the 767 came along, and TWA convinced regulators to let it cross the Atlantic on two engines. ETOPS-120 was born, then 180, then 240 — and every twin-engine widebody that crosses an ocean today traces back to a 767 over the North Atlantic in 1985.
