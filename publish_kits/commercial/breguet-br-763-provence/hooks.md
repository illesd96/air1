# Hooks for Bréguet Br.763 Provence

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

France's post-war double-decker airliner had a smoking lounge on the lower deck — and Air France ran it for twenty years.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Bréguet's Deux-Ponts — the 763 Provence — was France's answer to Boeing's Stratocruiser. Four piston engines, two decks, a cargo hold that could swallow cars whole. It entered Air France service in 1953 hauling passengers and freight across the Mediterranean, and it stuck around until 1971. Not glamorous. Not famous. But weirdly successful for a French double-decker that nobody outside France remembers.
