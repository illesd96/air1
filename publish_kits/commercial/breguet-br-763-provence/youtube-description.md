The Bréguet 761/763/765 Deux-Ponts was France's post-war double-decker — a four-piston-engine workhorse that hauled passengers and freight for Air France from 1953 to 1971. It was unfashionable, unglamorous, and absolutely reliable. We walk through the engineering, the Air France routes, and why the type quietly outlasted flashier rivals.
