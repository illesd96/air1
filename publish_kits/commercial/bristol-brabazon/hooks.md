# Hooks for Bristol Brabazon

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Britain demolished an entire village to build a runway for one airliner — and that airliner only flew once with passengers.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

1949. Bristol rolls out the Brabazon — a 230-foot-wingspan, eight-engine, transatlantic luxury liner intended to fly British elites to America in unmatched comfort. To build the assembly hall, Britain bulldozed the village of Charlton. The Brabazon flew. It worked. Nobody bought it. By 1953 the only prototype was scrapped. We're left with one of the most lavish failures in aviation history — and a runway extension that's still in use today.
