The Bristol Type 167 Brabazon was the post-war airliner Britain bet its prestige on. Eight engines. 230-foot wingspan. A demolished village named Charlton to make room for the assembly hangar. It flew in 1949. Nobody bought it. The only prototype was scrapped in 1953. We tell the story of the village, the airframe, and the scrapping.
