The British Aerospace Jetstream 31 is the small pressurized turboprop that became the king of American commuter feeder routes in the 1980s and 1990s. Resurrected from a failed Handley Page design, it gave 19 passengers a properly pressurized cabin where competitors had drafty fuselages.
