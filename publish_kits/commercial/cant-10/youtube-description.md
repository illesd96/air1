The CANT 10 was a small Italian flying boat airliner produced by Cantieri Aeronautici e Navali Triestini in 1920s Trieste. A four-passenger biplane with a pusher engine mounted between the wings and an open pilot cockpit perched on top of the hull, it embodied the strange charm of early Adriatic aviation.
