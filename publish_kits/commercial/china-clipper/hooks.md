# Hooks for China Clipper

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Pan Am's flying boat flew the first commercial trans-Pacific mail route in 1935 — and crashed nine years later with everyone aboard.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

November 22, 1935. The Martin M-130 China Clipper lifts off from San Francisco bound for Manila. It's the first commercial trans-Pacific airmail flight in history. The aircraft becomes a cultural icon — books, movies, a Coca-Cola ad. Then on January 8, 1945, with a different crew, it crashes off Trinidad. Everyone dies. The China Clipper opened the Pacific to American commerce, and then quietly took its place in aviation's roll of tragic firsts.
