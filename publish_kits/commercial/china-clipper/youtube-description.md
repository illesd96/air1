The China Clipper was the first of three Martin M-130 flying boats built for Pan American Airways. On November 22, 1935, it inaugurated the world's first commercial trans-Pacific airmail service from San Francisco to Manila. It became a cultural icon. Then on January 8, 1945, it crashed off Trinidad with all hands lost. We tell the whole story.
