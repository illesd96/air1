# Hooks for Douglas DC-3

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Built in 1935, still flying paying passengers in 2026 — the only airliner that made it 90 years.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Before the DC-3, airlines lost money on every flight. American Airlines told Douglas: build us a sleeper version of the DC-2, and we'll buy enough to make a profit. The result was 21 seats of riveted aluminum that paid for itself in passenger fares — no mail subsidy, no government contracts. It made commercial aviation financially viable, then went to war as the C-47, then refused to die for nine decades.
