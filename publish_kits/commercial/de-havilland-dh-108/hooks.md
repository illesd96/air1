# Hooks for de Havilland DH 108

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Three British experimental jets were built to test swept wings — and all three killed their pilots.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

1945. De Havilland builds the DH 108 'Swallow' — a tailless, swept-wing testbed to evaluate handling characteristics for what would eventually become the Comet jetliner. Three prototypes were built. All three crashed. All three killed their pilots, including Geoffrey de Havilland Jr., the founder's son, who broke apart in flight while pushing the aircraft transonic over the Thames Estuary in 1946. The DH 108 taught Britain everything about swept wings. The lesson cost three lives.
