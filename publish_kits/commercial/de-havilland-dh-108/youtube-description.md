The de Havilland DH 108 Swallow was a tailless, swept-wing experimental aircraft built in 1945 to evaluate high-speed handling for the future Comet jetliner. Three prototypes were built. All three crashed. All three killed their pilots — including Geoffrey de Havilland Jr., the founder's son, in 1946 over the Thames Estuary. We walk through the design, the crashes, and the brutal lessons learned.
