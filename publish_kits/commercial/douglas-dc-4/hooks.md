# Hooks for Douglas DC-4

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

This is the plane that flew over a million tons of supplies into West Berlin during the 1948 airlift — landing every 90 seconds for fifteen months.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

It was supposed to be a pressurized triple-tailed luxury liner called the DC-4E. Douglas scrapped that design, started over, and built the unpressurized DC-4 just in time for World War II to swallow the entire production run. As the C-54, it flew FDR to the Yalta Conference, then it carried the Berlin Airlift on its back. Postwar, civilian DC-4s opened up nonstop transcontinental airline service. Without it, the airline industry as we know it doesn't exist.
