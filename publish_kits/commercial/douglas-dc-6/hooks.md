# Hooks for Douglas DC-6

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

This is the plane that finally let airlines fly across the Atlantic without stopping for fuel — and seventy years later, it's still fighting wildfires.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

1946. The DC-3 was obsolete. The Constellation was selling. Douglas needed a comeback, and they got it: a pressurized four-engine piston liner with a 4,500-mile range, finally giving Pan Am and TWA true nonstop transatlantic service. Over 700 were built. And in 2026, the DC-6 still drops fire retardant on burning forests because no jet does it better at low speed.
