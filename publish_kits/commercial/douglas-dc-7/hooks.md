# Hooks for Douglas DC-7

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It was the fastest piston airliner ever built — and within four years of its debut, jets had made it obsolete.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

1953. Pan Am wanted a piston-engine airliner that could fly New York to London nonstop against winter headwinds. Douglas gave them the DC-7, with four turbo-compound Wright R-3350 engines so over-stressed they were nicknamed engine-eaters. It worked. For about four years. Then de Havilland flew the Comet, Boeing flew the 707, and the DC-7 became the most expensive obsolete airplane in history.
