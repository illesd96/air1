# Hooks for Douglas DC-8

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1961 a Douglas DC-8 broke the sound barrier in a dive, becoming the first civilian airliner to fly supersonic — and almost nobody remembers.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Boeing's 707 got the parades, the presidents, and the history books. Douglas's DC-8 got the slow knife. But here's what they don't tell you: in August 1961, a DC-8 was deliberately pushed into a 45-degree dive over Edwards Air Force Base and hit Mach 1.012 — the first and still only civilian airliner ever to fly supersonic. Why isn't this the most famous airliner in America?
