# Hooks for Douglas DC-9

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Douglas's tiny twin-engine jet outlived its maker by 25 years and spawned a whole family of airliners — including a Boeing.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When Douglas launched the DC-9 in 1965, nobody guessed it would still be flying as a Boeing in 2006. The little T-tail twin with engines bolted to the rear fuselage became the MD-80, the MD-90, and eventually the Boeing 717 — three generations of descendants across forty-one years of production. The original maker died. The plane wouldn't.
