# Hooks for Fairchild Dornier 728

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

A German-American regional jet had 125 firm orders, a flying prototype, and zero deliveries — because the company went bankrupt first.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Early 2002. Fairchild Dornier rolls out the 728 — a 70-seat regional jet meant to compete with the CRJ and ERJ. Lufthansa CityLine has it on order. Crossair has it on order. 125 firm orders on the books. The prototype rolls down the runway. Then the parent company collapses. The 728 is wheeled into storage. Nobody ever takes delivery. It became Germany's most expensive regional aviation failure, and a cautionary tale about regional jet economics.
