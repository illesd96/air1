# Hooks for Fairey Rotodyne

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Britain invented a helicopter-airplane hybrid in 1957 that could fly 190 mph — and noise complaints killed it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

1957. Fairey Aviation flies the Rotodyne — a compound gyroplane with a tip-jet-powered rotor, two wing-mounted turboprops, and the ability to take off vertically then cruise at airplane speeds. It worked. BEA wanted it. Operators across Europe were interested. Then the residents around the test sites complained about the noise — the tip-jets were genuinely ear-splitting — and the whole program died. The Rotodyne is the most spectacular VTOL aircraft you've probably never seen fly.
