The Fairey Rotodyne was a compound gyroplane that combined a tip-jet-powered rotor with two wing-mounted Napier Eland turboprops. It could take off vertically and cruise at 190 mph. British European Airways wanted it. Then the noise complaints from neighbors around the test sites became impossible to ignore. We walk through the engineering, the noise problem, and why this aircraft predicted modern eVTOL by sixty years.
