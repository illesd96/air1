# Hooks for Focke-Wulf A 20

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Focke-Wulf's first airliner was so unloved that almost nobody bought it — and that failure built a dynasty.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Before the Fw 200 Condor, before Heinrich Focke's first practical helicopter, before any of it, there was the A 20 Habicht — a four-seat cantilever monoplane that Focke-Wulf launched in the late 1920s. The airlines didn't want it. Only a handful were built. And yet, this commercial flop became the template Focke-Wulf would stretch, re-engine, and re-sell as the A 32 Bussard — the contract that put the company on the map.
