The Focke-Wulf A 20 Habicht was a beautifully designed four-seat cantilever monoplane that almost nobody wanted. The airlines turned it down. Only a few were built. But the layout — high wing, fixed undercarriage, enclosed cabin — became the DNA that Focke-Wulf would stretch into the A 32 Bussard contract that saved the company.
