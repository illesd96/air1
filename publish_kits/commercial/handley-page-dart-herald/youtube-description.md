The Handley Page HPR.7 Dart Herald was a British turboprop airliner designed in the 1950s as a DC-3 replacement. By the time it reached the market, Fokker's F27 and Avro's 748 had already claimed every customer. Sales were disappointing, and the program contributed directly to Handley Page's 1970 collapse — ending one of Britain's oldest aircraft makers.
