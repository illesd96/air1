# Hooks for Hawker Siddeley Trident

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

British European Airways made Hawker shrink the Trident to fit their routes — and handed Boeing the entire global trijet market on a silver platter.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

1957. De Havilland designed a beautiful 140-seat trijet for the world market. Then their only customer, British European Airways, demanded a smaller plane to fit BEA's specific European route map. Hawker complied. The Trident shrank. Two years later Boeing launched the bigger, faster, longer-range 727 and sold over 1,800 of them. Britain sold 117 Tridents. And the world stopped buying British airliners.
