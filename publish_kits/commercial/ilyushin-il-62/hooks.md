# Hooks for Ilyushin Il-62

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The Soviets built the world's largest jet airliner in 1963 — and gave it a fifth landing gear that drops out of the tail so it wouldn't tip over.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Aeroflot wanted a long-haul jet that could fly Moscow to Havana nonstop. Ilyushin gave them the Il-62 — for a brief moment in 1963 the largest jet airliner in the world, with four Kuznetsovs on the tail and a tiny extra wheel that dropped from the rear fuselage to keep the empty plane from tipping backward on its tail. It was the Soviet VC10. And, like the VC10, almost nobody wanted it.
