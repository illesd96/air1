The Irkut MC-21 — recently rebranded as the Yakovlev MC-21 — is Russia's ambitious bid to challenge Boeing and Airbus in the single-aisle market. First flown in 2017, it originally featured Pratt & Whitney engines and American-supplied composite wing technology. Sanctions forced Russia to rebuild both — switching to the Russian PD-14 engine and reinventing its carbon fiber wing process.
