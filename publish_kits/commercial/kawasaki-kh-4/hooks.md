# Hooks for Kawasaki KH-4

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Japan took a Bell 47, gave it a real cabin, and quietly made the most refined version ever.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Kawasaki had been building Bell 47 helicopters under license since 1952. By the 1960s, they decided to fix the original's biggest flaw — its tiny bubble cockpit — by designing a fully enclosed, four-seat cabin. The result was the KH-4, a Japanese-refined Bell 47 that outsold the American original in Asia and quietly became the standard utility helicopter across Pacific nations.
