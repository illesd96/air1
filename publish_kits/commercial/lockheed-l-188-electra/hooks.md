# Hooks for Lockheed L-188 Electra

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Two Electras broke up mid-air in 1959 and 1960 — and the FBI investigated Lockheed for sabotage before engineers discovered something nobody knew existed.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Lockheed launched the L-188 Electra in 1959 as America's answer to the British Viscount. Within months, two of them shed their wings in flight, killing 97 people. The investigation took two years and uncovered an entirely new aerodynamic phenomenon: propeller whirl mode, a violent harmonic vibration that destroyed the engine mounts and ripped wings off airframes. The fix worked. The reputational damage didn't. Electra production ended at 170 aircraft.
