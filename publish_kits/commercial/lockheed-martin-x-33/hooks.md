# Hooks for Lockheed Martin X-33

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Lockheed almost built a reusable spaceplane in the 1990s — then a single failed fuel tank killed the entire program.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The X-33 was NASA's bet on a next-generation reusable launch vehicle. Lockheed Martin would build a sub-scale demonstrator, prove the tech, and then scale it into VentureStar — a single-stage-to-orbit spaceplane. The aerospike engine worked. The lifting body worked. The composite hydrogen fuel tank ruptured during testing. The program was cancelled in 2001 with the vehicle 85% complete. SpaceX would later prove a different path. The X-33 sits in a hangar as the ghost of a future that didn't happen.
