The Lockheed Martin X-33 was supposed to demonstrate everything needed for VentureStar — a single-stage-to-orbit reusable spaceplane. Aerospike engines. Lifting body aerodynamics. A composite hydrogen fuel tank. Then the tank ruptured. The program was cancelled in 2001. We walk through what worked, what didn't, and why SpaceX would later win the reusability race a different way.
