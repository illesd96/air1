# Hooks for LZ 129 Hindenburg

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It wasn't the lightning. It wasn't the hydrogen alone. The Hindenburg burned because of paint chemistry.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

On May 6, 1937, the LZ 129 Hindenburg — the largest aircraft ever built — touched down at Lakehurst, New Jersey, and erupted in flames. 36 people died, and rigid airships died with them. But the real cause wasn't just hydrogen: investigators have spent decades arguing over the iron-oxide and aluminum paint coating the fabric envelope — essentially solid-rocket fuel.
