LZ 129 Hindenburg was the largest aircraft ever built — a German rigid airship longer than three football fields. Its catastrophic destruction at Lakehurst, New Jersey, on May 6, 1937, killed 36 and ended commercial rigid airship travel. The cause remains debated: hydrogen alone, lightning strike, sabotage, or the iron-oxide and aluminum paint that essentially coated the envelope in rocket fuel.
