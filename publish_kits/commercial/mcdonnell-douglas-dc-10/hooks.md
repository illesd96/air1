# Hooks for McDonnell Douglas DC-10

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

A cargo door that latched but wasn't locked killed 346 people — and gave aviation its most-cited engineering tragedy.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

On June 12, 1972, an American Airlines DC-10 lost its rear cargo door over Windsor, Ontario, and barely landed. Two years later, the same defect on Turkish Airlines Flight 981 over Ermenonville Forest killed all 346 people aboard. McDonnell Douglas had been warned. The DC-10 cargo door became a textbook case taught in every engineering school. The plane survived another 50 years anyway.
