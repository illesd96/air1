# Hooks for McDonnell Douglas MD-80

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Pilots called it the Mad Dog. American Airlines flew 360 of them. And it was so loud in the back row that frequent fliers paid more to sit anywhere else.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Two big engines bolted to the tail, a long thin tube of a fuselage, and a cockpit that still ran on dials and tape — the McDonnell Douglas MD-80 was a 1980 design built on a 1965 airframe, and it shouldn't have worked. Yet American Airlines made it the backbone of its fleet for thirty-six years, retiring the last Mad Dog in 2019. This is the story of the rear-engine workhorse that outlived its maker.
