# Hooks for Saunders-Roe SR.45 Princess

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Britain built the largest all-metal flying boat in history — then mothballed all three before they ever earned a penny.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

On the Isle of Wight in 1952, Saunders-Roe rolled out the Princess — a ten-engine, double-deck behemoth that became the largest all-metal flying boat ever constructed. It was supposed to be Britain's flagship across the Empire. By the time it flew, the world had moved on to land-based jets. All three Princesses ended up cocooned, then scrapped. This is the story of the flying boat that was finished the moment it was finished.
