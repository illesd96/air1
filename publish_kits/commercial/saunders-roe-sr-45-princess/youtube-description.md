The Saunders-Roe SR.45 Princess was Britain's bet that flying boats still had a future after WWII. They were wrong. By 1952 the Comet was flying, the Boeing 707 was on the horizon, and runway infrastructure had caught up to landplanes. The Princess flew anyway — and we walk through exactly why three of them ended up cocooned beside the Solent.
