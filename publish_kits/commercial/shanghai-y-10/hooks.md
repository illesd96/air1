# Hooks for Shanghai Y-10

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

China copied the Boeing 707 in 1980 — flew it, then deliberately killed the program.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

September 26, 1980. A four-engine narrow-body lifts off in Shanghai. It looks like a Boeing 707. That's because it essentially is one — reverse-engineered, redesigned, and built in China at a moment when the country had no commercial jet industry to speak of. The Shanghai Y-10 flew. It worked. Then the leadership pulled the plug. Forty-five years later, China is rolling out the C919 — and the Y-10 is the awkward ghost in the room.
