# Hooks for Sud Aviation Caravelle

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Every modern regional jet has its engines on the tail because a French engineer in 1955 decided that's where they belonged.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Caravelle was the world's first short-haul jet airliner — and the French stuck the engines on the rear fuselage because nobody had ever done it before and the wing was finally free of all that turbine plumbing. The cabin went silent. Boeing copied it on the 727. Douglas copied it on the DC-9. Tupolev copied it on the Tu-134. And then Sud Aviation's brilliant little jet was outsold by everyone it inspired.
