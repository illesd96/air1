# Hooks for Sud-Est SE.200 Amphitrite

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

France built a six-engine wartime flying boat — and the Germans seized it before it could carry a passenger.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Designed in the late 1930s as France's answer to trans-oceanic flying boat travel, the SE.200 Amphitrite was a six-engine, twin-tail monster meant for prestige routes across the Atlantic. Then Germany invaded. The prototype was confiscated and finished under occupation, finally flying in December 1942 with German markings. It carried no fare-paying passengers. It survived the war as a ghost — a French luxury liner with German tail codes.
