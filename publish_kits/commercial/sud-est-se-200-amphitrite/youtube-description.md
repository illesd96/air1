The Sud-Est SE.200 Amphitrite, originally the Lioré et Olivier LeO H-49, was France's pre-war bet on six-engine flying boats for the Atlantic. The German occupation cut it off. The prototype flew in 1942 under German control. It never carried a paying passenger. We trace the design, the seizure, and the strange wartime test flights.
