# Hooks for Tupolev ANT-9

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Stalin's Soviet Union demanded a domestic airliner — Tupolev delivered a three-engine answer to the Dutch.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

By 1929, Soviet pride could no longer tolerate Aeroflot's predecessor flying foreign aircraft. Andrei Tupolev built the ANT-9 — a three-engine, all-metal high-wing airliner clearly inspired by the Fokker Trimotor, but defiantly Soviet. It flew Stalin-era propaganda tours across Europe and stayed in service through 1945.
