The Tupolev ANT-9 was the Soviet Union's first major domestic passenger airliner — a three-engine, all-metal high-wing design that first flew in 1929. It was Stalin's response to Aeroflot's predecessor flying foreign Fokker and Junkers aircraft. The ANT-9 toured Europe as a propaganda showpiece and stayed in service until 1945.
