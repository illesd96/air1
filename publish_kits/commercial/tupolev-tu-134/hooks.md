# Hooks for Tupolev Tu-134

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Look at the nose of a 1970s Soviet airliner and you'll find a glass bubble — because the Tu-134 inherited it from a Cold War bomber.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Tupolev Tu-134 has a glass bombardier nose right out of a Tu-16 strategic bomber, and that's because that's exactly where the design came from. Soviet airliners had to navigate without ILS, GPS, or modern radar over endless tundra, so they kept the glass nose for the navigator's optical sights. The Tu-134 wasn't an airliner that looked weird. It was a bomber that learned to carry passengers.
