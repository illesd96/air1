The Yakovlev SJ-100 — known until recently as the Sukhoi Superjet 100 — was Russia's grand re-entry into the global airliner market. Built with Western suppliers across Europe and the US, it first flew in 2008 from Komsomolsk-on-Amur. Sanctions, a tragic Mount Salak demo crash, and political shifts have forced a complete redesign with domestic parts.
