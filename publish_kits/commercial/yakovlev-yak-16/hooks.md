# Hooks for Yakovlev Yak-16

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Yakovlev built a twin-engine Soviet airliner in 1947 — and the An-2 biplane killed it dead.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

September 1947. Yakovlev — a fighter design bureau — pivots and builds a twin-piston passenger transport called the Yak-16. It's fast, it's modern, it's a clean monoplane. Then Antonov's giant biplane the An-2 enters service: slower, uglier, and infinitely more versatile across Soviet rough fields. The Yak-16 was killed before production. The An-2 became the most-produced single-engine biplane in history. Sometimes the worse aircraft wins.
