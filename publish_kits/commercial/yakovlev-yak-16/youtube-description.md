The Yakovlev Yak-16 was a Soviet twin-piston light transport that first flew in 1947 in both passenger and military cargo versions. It was killed because Antonov's An-2 biplane was deemed more versatile across the rough fields of the USSR. We walk through the design, the competition, and why the biplane won.
