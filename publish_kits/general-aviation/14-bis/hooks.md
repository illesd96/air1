# Hooks for 14-bis

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In Brazil, this is the plane that invented flight. Not the Wright Flyer.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

On 23 October 1906, a wealthy Brazilian dandy in a Panama hat lifted a tail-first canard biplane sixty meters over the Bagatelle field in Paris, in front of a crowd, with the press watching. Alberto Santos-Dumont became the first man to make a publicly witnessed, heavier-than-air powered flight in Europe. To this day in Brazil, he — not the Wrights — is the father of aviation. The 14-bis is at the center of a controversy that's still alive.
