The Santos-Dumont 14-bis, also called Oiseau de proie, was a canard-style biplane designed and built by Brazilian aviation pioneer Alberto Santos-Dumont. On 23 October 1906, near Paris, the 14-bis made what is widely recognized as the first publicly witnessed manned powered flight — 60 meters at roughly 3 meters of altitude, in front of a crowd, photographed, filmed, and officially recorded by the Aéro-Club de France.

In this video: why Santos-Dumont insisted the Wrights' 1903 flights didn't count (the catapult-and-rail argument), what the 14-bis was actually like to fly — tail-first, pushed by a propeller, looking like a box kite going backwards — and why Brazil still teaches Santos-Dumont as the father of aviation today. A replica hangs in the National Congress in Brasília.
