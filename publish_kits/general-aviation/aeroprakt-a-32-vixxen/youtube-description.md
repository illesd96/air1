The Aeroprakt A-32 is a Ukrainian two-seat, high-wing, tricycle-gear ultralight and LSA, designed by Yuri Yakovlev and produced by Aeroprakt in Kyiv. In Australia the same aircraft is marketed as the Vixxen by Foxbat Australia. The structure mixes welded steel tube with aluminum monocoque skin, the cabin is unusually wide for the class, and the slotted flaps allow stall speeds in the low 30 knot range. A Rotax 912 ULS gives a cruise in the 95 to 100 knot range and the kind of STOL manners that have built a loyal owner base from Bundaberg to Belarus. We look at the relationship between the earlier A-22 Foxbat and the A-32, what owners say about flying it in real Australian outback conditions, how the war in Ukraine has affected production and parts support, and why Foxbat Australia's customer base is unusually patient with delays. Smart engineering, modest marketing.
