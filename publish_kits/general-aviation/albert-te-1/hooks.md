The Albert TE.1 was built in France in 1926 — a wooden, cloth-skinned, parasol-wing monoplane that turned out to be genuinely competitive for its class. It set a French altitude record, made several notable long-distance flights, and then mostly faded from memory. In this video, a forgotten interwar French light aircraft that briefly mattered.
