The Albert TE.1 is the kind of airplane that history nearly lost: a single-seat, parasol-wing wooden monoplane built in France in 1926. It set a class altitude record, made notable cross-country flights, and quietly demonstrated that a small French design office could build a practical light aircraft.

In this video: the interwar French light aircraft scene, what the TE.1 actually achieved, why airplanes like this disappeared from collective memory, and what little visual record survives.
