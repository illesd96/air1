# Hooks for Balls 8

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

She launched the X-15. Then the X-43. Forty-nine years on one tail number.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Tail number 52-008. In aviator slang, three zeros are 'balls' — so to NASA's Dryden crew, she was always Balls 8. The B-52B that dropped X-15s, lifting bodies, parachute test articles and the Mach 9.6 X-43A scramjet over a half-century of service. When she finally retired in 2004 at age 49, she was the oldest B-52 still flying. This is the airplane that launched the airplanes.
