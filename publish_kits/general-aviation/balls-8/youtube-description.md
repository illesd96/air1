Balls 8 — NB-52B tail number 52-008 — was a Boeing B-52B Stratofortress modified for use as a launch mothership at the NASA Dryden (now Armstrong) Flight Research Center. Between 1959 and 1968, she carried the X-15 rocket plane on 106 of its 199 flights. After the X-15 program, she spent another 36 years dropping lifting bodies, parachute test articles, scaled hypersonic vehicles and finally, in 2004, the X-43A scramjet that reached Mach 9.6.

In this video: how a 1955 B-52 became NASA's most reliable test platform for half a century, the engineering of the underwing pylon and drop-release system, the famous test pilots who flew her, and her formal retirement at Edwards AFB in December 2004 as the oldest B-52 in the US inventory.
