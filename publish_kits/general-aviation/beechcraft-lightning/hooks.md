# Hooks for Beechcraft Lightning

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Beechcraft built one. It flew once. Then disappeared.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1982, Beechcraft quietly rolled out a single-engine turboprop called the Model 38P Lightning. PT6 power, four seats, retractable gear, a clean composite-friendly airframe — exactly the airplane the high-performance market wanted. It flew. It tested well. Then Beech parked it. The Lightning never reached production, and you've probably never seen one. Today, the story of the turboprop Beechcraft built and then chose not to sell.
