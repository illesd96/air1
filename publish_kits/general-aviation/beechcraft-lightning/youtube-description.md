The Beechcraft Model 38P Lightning was an early-1980s experimental single-engine turboprop — a clean-sheet, four-seat, PT6-powered aircraft that Beech built to explore whether there was a market for an owner-flown turboprop below the King Air. Two prototypes flew. The program was quietly shelved.

In this video: the boardroom calculus at Beech in the Reagan era, how the Lightning's specification predicted the TBM market that SOCATA would own a decade later, and why the type certificate work never finished. We dig into the few surviving photos and engineering notes that document this almost-forgotten airplane.
