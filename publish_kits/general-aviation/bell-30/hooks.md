# Hooks for Bell 30

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

This is the helicopter that became every helicopter.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1943, in a small factory in Gardenville, New York, a self-taught engineer named Arthur M. Young bolted a teetering two-blade rotor and a stabilizer bar onto a steel-tube frame and made it fly. The Bell 30 wasn't pretty — open cockpit, exposed engine, three legs — but the layout it pioneered became the Bell 47, the first civil helicopter ever certified, and the silhouette of nearly every light helicopter that followed.
