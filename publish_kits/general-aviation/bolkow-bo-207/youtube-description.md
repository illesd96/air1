The Bölkow Bo 207 is one of the earliest serious post-war West German civil light aircraft. A four-seat development of the WWII-era Klemm Kl 107, it represented the German light aviation industry getting back on its feet in the late 1950s and early 1960s.

In this video: how the post-war restrictions shaped what Germany could build and when, the direct design lineage from the Klemm trainer, what the Bo 207 actually competed with in Europe, and where the few surviving airframes live today.
