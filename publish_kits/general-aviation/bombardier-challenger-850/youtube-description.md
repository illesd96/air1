The Bombardier Challenger 850 — originally called the Challenger SE, then the Challenger 800 — is a super-midsize business jet derived directly from the 50-seat Bombardier CRJ200 LR regional airliner. Updated and produced as the Challenger 850 from 2006 to 2012, it was the largest super-midsize business jet on the market at the time.

In this video: why Bombardier reused the CRJ200 platform for the bizjet world, what an airline-class fuselage actually gives a corporate operator (cabin volume, baggage, real galleys), the operating cost reality of a former regional-airliner engine plant, and why charter fleets still love these airframes on the used market.
