# Hooks for Bowers Fly Baby

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The Boeing historian's wooden monoplane that beat every homebuilt at Rockford 1962.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Peter Bowers, Boeing engineer, historian and Air Force Reserve officer, drew the Fly Baby in his Seattle basement and won the EAA design contest in 1962. Wood and fabric, open cockpit, folding wings, plans-built for around 1,500 dollars in 1960s money. It is still the prototypical American homebuilt.
