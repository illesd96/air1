Ettore Bugatti commissioned aerodynamicist Louis de Monge to design the Model 100 as a purpose-built racer for the 1939 Deutsch de la Meurthe Cup. The result was a beautifully strange aircraft: forward-swept wing, V-tail, fully retractable gear, twin centerline-thrust Bugatti 50P inline engines driving contra-rotating propellers through a complex gearbox, and automatic flaps that adjusted with airspeed. The September 1939 deadline came and went, German troops crossed into France, and Bugatti hid the airframe in a barn. It survived and is now displayed at the EAA Museum in Oshkosh. American pilot Scotty Wilson built a flying replica in 2015 to prove the design would have worked. It flew. Wilson was lost in a 2016 crash. We cover the engineering choices, the rivalry with Caudron and Heinkel, the Wilson replica program, and what makes the 100P perhaps the most beautiful aircraft never to race.
