# Hooks for Cirrus VK-30

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Before the SR22, Cirrus built this. It was a kit.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1988 the Klapmeier brothers were two homebuilders in Wisconsin selling kits out of a barn. Their first product was the VK-30 — a single-engine, pusher-prop, composite kit plane that looked like a tiny Lear jet. It flew well. It was complicated to build. About 40 were completed. And then Cirrus pivoted to certified aircraft, the SR20 happened, and the rest is history. Today, the kit plane that became a company that changed GA.
