The Cirrus VK-30 was the first product from Cirrus Design Corporation — a single-engine, pusher-propeller, all-composite homebuilt kit aircraft introduced in 1987 and first flown in February 1988. Designed by Alan and Dale Klapmeier in a dairy barn in Baraboo, Wisconsin, the VK-30 carried four to five people and was sold as a quick-build kit until around 1993.

In this video: how the Klapmeier brothers funded a clean-sheet kit plane out of a barn, the engineering choices — composite construction, mid-fuselage engine, retractable gear, pusher prop — that informed everything Cirrus would later certify, why the kit business was abandoned in favor of certified aircraft, and the direct genetic line from the VK-30 to the SR20, SR22 and SF50 Vision Jet.
