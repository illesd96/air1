# Hooks for Coandă-1910

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Romania built a jet-ish aircraft in 1910.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

At the 1910 Paris Air Show, a 24-year-old Romanian engineer named Henri Coandă unveiled the Coandă-1910 — a sesquiplane powered by what he called a 'turbo-propulseur,' a piston engine driving a ducted centrifugal blower instead of a propeller. The history is contested, the test flight is murky, but the airframe is real. Today, the strangest pre-WWI aircraft ever built — and the man whose name we still use for the aerodynamic effect that makes wings work.
