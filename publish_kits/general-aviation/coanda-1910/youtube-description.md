The Coandă-1910 was an unconventional sesquiplane aircraft designed and built by Romanian inventor Henri Coandă and unveiled at the Second International Aeronautical Exhibition in Paris in October 1910. Its propulsion system, which Coandă called the 'turbo-propulseur,' consisted of a 50 hp piston engine driving a multi-bladed centrifugal blower that exhausted through a duct — a ducted-fan arrangement, not a true jet.

In this video: what Coandă actually built (and what he later claimed he flew), the contested December 1910 test flight, why the broader aviation community recognizes Coandă for the Coandă effect rather than for the 1910 aircraft itself, and the strange legacy of an airframe that may have been ahead of its time — or may have been an elaborate later claim.
