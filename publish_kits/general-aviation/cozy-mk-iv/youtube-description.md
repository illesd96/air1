Nat Puffer took Burt Rutan's two-seat Long-EZ, widened it into a side-by-side Cozy III, then stretched and re-engineered it into the four-seat Cozy Mk IV. It is not a kit. It is plans-built from raw materials, with parts and a few prefabricated assemblies available through Aircraft Spruce and Specialty Co. With a Lycoming O-360 or IO-360, a Cozy Mk IV will cruise comfortably in the 160 to 180 knot range with four real seats and a useful load that respects family travel. The community is small, tight, and patient: a Mk IV is a multi-year build, not a weekend project. We cover the lineage from the Vari-Eze through the Long-EZ and Cozy III, what makes a canard fundamentally different to fly, the AeroCanard variant, and what new builders should think about before ordering plans in 2026.
