# Hooks for Dassault Falcon 50

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Three engines. Why? Because in 1976, oceans needed crossing.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When Dassault designed the Falcon 50 in the mid-1970s, the FAA still required three engines to cross long stretches of water. The French response was elegant: a trijet with an area-ruled fuselage, a brand-new wing, and the same Falcon cabin operators already trusted. In this video, the airplane that taught business aviation how to fly intercontinental — and the airplane operators still quietly love.
