The Erla 5 — sometimes catalogued as the DKW Erla Me 5a — is one of the lesser-known 1930s German light sport airplanes. Single-seat, single-engine, built in small numbers, and demonstrated on long-distance flights as late as 1939 before politics and the looming war shut down the market.

In this video: the German sport aviation scene of the early 1930s, what the Erla 5 was designed to do, why a trainer variant didn't find buyers, and what archival material survives.
