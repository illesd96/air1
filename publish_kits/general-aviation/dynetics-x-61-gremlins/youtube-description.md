The Dynetics X-61 Gremlins is an experimental unmanned aerial vehicle developed under DARPA's Gremlins program, aimed at demonstrating air-launch and — crucially — airborne recovery of small, low-cost, reusable UAVs. After several years of testing and a partial-success campaign, in November 2021 a Lockheed C-130 actually caught a returning Gremlin in midair, proving the concept.

In this video: the DARPA brief that started the program, what 'attritable but reusable' actually means in defense planning, the engineering of the docking probe and the tow-plane recovery mechanism, and how the Gremlins lessons are flowing into the larger Collaborative Combat Aircraft and loyal-wingman programs taking shape now.
