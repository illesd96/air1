# Hooks for Epic E1000

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

All-carbon. PT6. 333 knots. Built in Oregon.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Epic Aircraft spent fifteen years trying to certify a kit-plane-turned-production single-engine turboprop. They went through bankruptcy, a Russian ownership change, a relocation to Bend, Oregon — and finally, in 2019, FAA certification of the E1000. Today it's a 333-knot, all-carbon, six-seat turboprop that competes head-on with the TBM. Here's the certification saga the trade press almost never tells in full.
