The Epic E1000 is an American single-engine turboprop with a PT6A-67A out front, an all-carbon airframe, six seats, and a max cruise speed of 333 knots. It received FAA Part 23 certification in 2019 after a development saga that started as the kit-built Epic LT in the early 2000s.

In this video: the bankruptcy and Russian rescue that kept the program alive, the move to Bend, Oregon, the engineering changes that took the airframe from experimental to certified, and why early E1000 GX deliveries are popping up on Controller.com at prices that compete directly with used TBM 940s.
