# Hooks for Hodek HK-101

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

A Czech twin in 1947. Then communism killed it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1947, in newly independent Czechoslovakia, a small design team rolled out the Hodek HK-101: a sleek two-seat twin-engine light aircraft that would have put Czech industry on the map of postwar GA. They built two prototypes. Then the communist coup of February 1948 happened — and the HK-101 vanished into the same nationalized fog that swallowed half of Central European general aviation. Today, the plane history forgot.
