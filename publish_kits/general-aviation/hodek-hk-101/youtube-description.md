The Hodek HK-101 first flew on 3 September 1947 — a two-seat, twin-engined light aircraft designed in postwar Czechoslovakia by a small team led by Bedřich Hodek. Two prototypes were completed before the February 1948 communist coup nationalized Czech aviation industry and the project was abandoned.

In this video: the brief flowering of Czech GA between 1945 and 1948, what the HK-101 was actually trying to be, the political earthquake that ended dozens of small designers' careers overnight, and the surviving photographs and drawings that are almost all we have left.
