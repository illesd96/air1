# Hooks for Hunting H.126

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The British research jet that blew air over its flaps to land at 32 knots.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Hunting Aircraft built the H.126 in 1963 as a flying test bed for jet flap technology. By dumping engine bleed across the trailing edges it could fly stable approaches at thirty-something knots, decades before STOL was fashionable. Only one was built and it lives at Cosford today.
