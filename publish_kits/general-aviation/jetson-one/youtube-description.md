The Jetson One is a single-seat electric octocopter built in Arezzo, Italy by Swedish startup Jetson Aero. 102 horsepower from eight electric motors, a tubular space frame, redundant flight controllers, and a flight time around 20 minutes per battery pack. In the United States it qualifies as a Part 103 ultralight — meaning no pilot's license, no medical, and no aircraft registration required.

In this video: what the Part 103 classification actually buys Jetson legally, the safety architecture (motor-out redundancy, terrain following, geofencing), the realistic mission profile of a 20-minute personal eVTOL, and the manufacturing ramp that's seeing units delivered in Italy and the US. We also cover the limits — because there are real ones.
