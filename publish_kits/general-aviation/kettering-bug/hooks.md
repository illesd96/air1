## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In October 1918, weeks before the Armistice, Charles Kettering and Orville Wright watched a small wooden biplane lift off a launching track in Dayton, fly to a preset distance on a mechanical timer, then drop its wings and dive into the ground. The Kettering Bug was an unmanned aerial torpedo, the first true cruise missile, and a century ahead of its time. The Army built 45. They never went to war. Here's why.
