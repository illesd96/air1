The Kettering Bug — officially the Kettering Aerial Torpedo — was an experimental unmanned aerial torpedo designed by Charles F. Kettering with input from Orville Wright and tested by Dayton-Wright Airplane Company starting in 1918. It carried 180 pounds of explosives up to 75 miles at 50 mph, guided by a preset mechanical timer and pneumatic-electric control system that cut the engine, dropped the wings and let it plunge into the target area.

In this video: how Kettering's mechanical guidance system actually worked, why the Bug was never used in combat despite a successful October 1918 test, the direct technological line from the Bug to the V-1, the Regulus, and modern cruise missiles, and the surviving examples at the National Museum of the US Air Force.
