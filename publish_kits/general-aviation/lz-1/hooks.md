# Hooks for LZ 1

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The first rigid airship that actually flew. Lake Constance, 2 July 1900.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Count Ferdinand von Zeppelin launched LZ 1 from a floating hangar on Lake Constance in July 1900. Aluminum frame, 17 hydrogen gas cells, two 14-horsepower Daimler engines. It flew for 18 minutes, didn't really steer, and broke the ground that every rigid airship that followed stood on.
