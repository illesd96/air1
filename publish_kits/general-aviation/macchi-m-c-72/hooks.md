# Hooks for Macchi M.C.72

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Italy. 1934. Counter-rotating propellers. A piston speed record that still stands.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Macchi M.C.72 is technically a racing floatplane, but it sits firmly in general aviation's heritage as one of the most extraordinary single-engine piston designs ever built. Twin counter-rotating propellers, a coupled engine, and a piston-engine speed record set in 1934 that has never been beaten. In this video, the airplane that closed the Schneider Trophy era — and is still untouched on the record books.
