The Macchi M.C.72 is one of the most extraordinary piston aircraft ever built. Two coupled Fiat AS.6 engines, twin counter-rotating propellers, an airframe optimized for outright speed, and a 1934 world speed record for piston-engine seaplanes that has never been broken.

In this video: the Schneider Trophy context that produced the M.C.72, the engineering choices that made the airframe possible, the pilots who flew it (and the ones who didn't survive the program), and why the speed record still stands almost a century later.
