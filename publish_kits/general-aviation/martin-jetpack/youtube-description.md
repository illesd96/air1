The Martin Jetpack was a single-person aircraft developed by Glenn Martin and Martin Aircraft Company of New Zealand. Despite its name it never used a jet engine; it used two ducted fans, a 2.0 litre two-stroke V4 piston engine, and a frame the pilot strapped into. Unveiled at the EAA's AirVenture in Oshkosh in 2008, the US Federal Aviation Administration classified it as an experimental ultralight airplane. Over the following years Martin demonstrated tethered and free flights, raised capital from Chinese investors, attempted to pivot into emergency-services and surveillance roles, and ultimately wound down operations in the late 2010s without finding a sustainable market. We cover how the ducted-fan layout differs from a true jetpack, what the FAA categorization meant for certification, why the use cases never quite closed, and what modern eVTOL developers may have taken from Martin's three decades of work. A real, flying answer to a Saturday-morning-cartoon question.
