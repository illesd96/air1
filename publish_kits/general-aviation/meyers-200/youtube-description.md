The Meyers 200 is one of those airplanes only the old hands at the airport know about. A four-seat retractable single, designed by Allen Meyers in Michigan in the 1950s, that outperformed almost everything in its segment — and is best known to owners as one of the most rugged airframes ever certified in its class.

In this video: where Meyers came from, what makes the 200 structurally unusual, how the Aero Commander 200 took over the type certificate, and why values today have quietly climbed.
