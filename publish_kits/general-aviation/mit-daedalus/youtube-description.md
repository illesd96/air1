The MIT Aeronautics and Astronautics Department's Daedalus project produced three human-powered aircraft between 1986 and 1988, culminating in the Daedalus 88. With a 34-metre wingspan, a Kevlar and carbon spar, and a structure that weighed only about 32 kilograms empty, it carried Greek cycling champion Kanellos Kanellopoulos 115.11 kilometres in 3 hours 54 minutes on 23 April 1988, from Heraklion on Crete toward Santorini. A gust broke the tail boom meters from the beach. The FAI world records for straight-line distance and duration in human-powered flight still stand. We cover the team led by John Langford and Mark Drela, the physiological program that selected and trained the pilots, the airframe engineering trades, and the failed earlier flights that paved the record. A masterclass in patient engineering.
