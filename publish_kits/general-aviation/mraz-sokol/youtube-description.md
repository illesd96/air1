The Mráz M.1 Sokol is one of those airplanes that almost nobody knows about — and pilots who do know about it tend to get protective. Designed in secret during the German occupation of Czechoslovakia, then produced from 1946 onward by the Beneš-Mráz factory.

In this video: the political and industrial context that shaped Czechoslovak aviation immediately post-war, what made the Sokol's design notable, why it was exported in surprisingly large numbers, and how a handful of survivors are still flying.
