Murphy Aircraft of Chilliwack, British Columbia, has been building Canadian backcountry kit aircraft since the late 1980s. The Murphy Moose is the line's heavy hitter: a high-wing, four to six-seat utility aircraft sold as a kit and a quick-build kit, designed to take Lycoming O-540s, IO-580s, or, famously, the Vedeneyev M14P nine-cylinder radial. Big square doors, a deep cabin floor, and provision for amphibious floats, oversized tundra tires, and skis make it a working aircraft for prospectors, lodge owners, and serious backcountry pilots in Canada and Alaska. We cover the Moose's lineage from the smaller Rebel and Maverick, what the M14P does to a Moose's character versus the Lycomings, how the quick-build kit shortens the build, and what to watch for on a high-time bush airframe. Honest Canadian utility.
