# Hooks for Northrop N-1M

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Northrop's first flying wing. They called it the Jeep.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1940, Jack Northrop wheeled out something that wasn't supposed to fly. The N-1M was a pure flying wing — no tail, no fuselage, just a curved boomerang of wood and metal pushed by two prop engines. The crew called it the Jeep. It taught Northrop almost everything he needed to know to build the YB-35, the YB-49, and eventually — fifty years later — the B-2 Spirit. Today, the airplane where the flying wing began.
