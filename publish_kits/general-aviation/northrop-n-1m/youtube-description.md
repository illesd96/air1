The Northrop N-1M, nicknamed Jeep, was a 1940 American experimental aircraft built by Northrop Aircraft to validate Jack Northrop's flying wing concept. Wooden construction, two pusher props, swept and adjustable wingtips, no tail and no conventional fuselage — just a wing shaped like a flat boomerang.

In this video: how Northrop's obsession with the flying wing started in the 1920s, what the N-1M taught the engineers about stability and control that the larger XB-35 and YB-49 would build on, the dead-ends and crashes, and the direct conceptual thread from this 1940 wooden prototype to Northrop Grumman's B-2 Spirit and B-21 Raider.
