# Hooks for Pilatus PC-24

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It's a business jet. It also lands on dirt.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Pilatus already owned the rough-field turboprop market with the PC-12. Then in 2013, the little Swiss company that builds airplanes between mountain peaks did something nobody else dared: a clean-sheet light business jet certified for unpaved runways. The PC-24 is the only twin-jet on the planet you can legally land on grass, gravel, or a Greenland ice strip. Here's why operators waited eight years for one.
