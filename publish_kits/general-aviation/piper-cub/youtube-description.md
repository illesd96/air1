The Piper J-3 Cub is the picture in your head when someone says general aviation. Cloth-covered fuselage, 65 horses on the nose, one door that opens up and one window that drops down. From 1938 to 1947, Piper built more than 19,000 of them, most for the Civilian Pilot Training Program that fed the WWII pipeline.

In this video: what the Cub actually flies like, why a stick-and-rudder education in one still beats a Cirrus transition, and the unreasonably strong used market for an airplane that cruises at 75 knots.
