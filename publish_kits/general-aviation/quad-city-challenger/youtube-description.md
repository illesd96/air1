Quad City Aircraft Corporation introduced the Challenger in 1983 out of Moline, Illinois. It is a family of one and two-place, pusher-configuration, tricycle-gear ultralight aircraft built around bolted aluminum tube and Dacron sailcloth. The single-seat Challenger I qualifies as a true FAR Part 103 ultralight in the US, while the two-place Challenger II is flown as an experimental or special light sport. Power options have ranged from the Rotax 447 and 503 to the 582 and four-stroke alternatives. The aircraft has been flown on floats and skis, used as a primary trainer at hundreds of small fields, and supported by a tight North American community of dealers and chapters. We look at the Challenger II Light Sport, the differences between Long Wing and Special Edition, what to inspect before buying a 30-year-old airframe, and why the design still ships new kits today. Slow, simple, addictive.
