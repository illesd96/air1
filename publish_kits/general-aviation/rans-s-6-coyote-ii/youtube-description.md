Randy Schlitter introduced the Rans S-6 Coyote II in 1988 from a former bicycle shop in Hays, Kansas. The brief was a side-by-side, high-wing, fabric-covered two-seater that could be ordered as a taildragger or a tricycle, built from a quick-build kit, and certified into the European microlight rules. Power options ran from the two-stroke Rotax 582 up through the modern 100-horsepower Rotax 912, and the airframe scaled cleanly across each fitment. By the mid-2000s the S-6 had outsold most of the homebuilt competition and become a fixture at every European microlight field and American grass strip. Modern Coyote IIs ship as the S-6S light sport with a refined wing and proper instrument panels. We walk through the S-6 family, the differences between European microlight and American light sport configurations, what to look for on a 2,000-hour airframe, and why so many Rans owners eventually finish a second kit.
