The Scaled Composites Model 318 White Knight is a twin-jet, high-altitude carrier aircraft designed and built in Mojave by Burt Rutan's Scaled Composites, with funding from Paul Allen. Its specific purpose was to lift the SpaceShipOne spaceplane to about 47,000 feet for air-launch — and on three flights in 2004, it carried SpaceShipOne to the Kármán line and the Ansari X PRIZE.

In this video: Rutan's design philosophy, the shared cockpit and avionics with SpaceShipOne (so test pilots could train in one to fly the other), the engineering tradeoffs of a purpose-built mothership, and how White Knight retired into a long second career as a high-altitude test platform — and gave its name to the larger WhiteKnightTwo that now carries Virgin Galactic's VSS Unity.
