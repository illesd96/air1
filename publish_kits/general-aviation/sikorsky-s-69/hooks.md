# Hooks for Sikorsky S-69

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Two rotors. No tail rotor. 230 knots. In 1973.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Long before the X2 and Defiant, Sikorsky was building the future. In 1973 they flew the S-69 — a coaxial helicopter with two stiff, counter-rotating rotors stacked on the same mast, no tail rotor at all, and an Advancing Blade Concept that solved the retreating-blade-stall problem that limits every conventional helicopter. It hit 230 knots in level flight. The Pentagon walked away. Fifty years later, Sikorsky is finally selling the idea.
