The Slepcev Storch is a Serbian-built type-certified, kit, and ultralight STOL aircraft designed by Yugoslavian-Australian Nestor Slepcev as a 3/4 scale homage to the wartime Fieseler Fi 156 Storch. Currently produced by Storch Aircraft Serbia in several variants, the design captures the original Storch's iconic high-wing silhouette, full-span leading edge slats, and almost helicopter-like short-field performance, while accepting modern engines from Rotax to Jabiru and Subaru conversions. Ultralight versions are sold as kits for amateur construction; ready-to-fly factory builds are available from the Serbian operation. We cover the build philosophy that keeps the visual fidelity to the original Storch intact, what real-world short-field numbers owners report, the structural and engine differences between the Australian-era Slepcev and the modern Serbian line, and why STOL Drag pilots increasingly look at the Storch when they want personality with their performance.
