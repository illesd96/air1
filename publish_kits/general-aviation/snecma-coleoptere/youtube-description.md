The SNECMA C.450 Coleoptere was a French Cold War tail-sitting VTOL prototype that combined an Atar 101E turbojet with an annular ring wing. Designed by SNECMA and manufactured by Nord Aviation, it first hovered tethered in April 1958 and made its first untethered flight in December 1958. After eight successful tethered hops at Melun-Villaroche, test pilot Auguste Morel attempted the program's ninth flight on 25 July 1959, lost pitch control during transition, ejected at low altitude, and was seriously injured. The prototype was destroyed and the program shut down. We look at the annular wing aerodynamics, why the Coleoptere shared the same fate as Convair's XFY Pogo and Lockheed's XFV, what the German Focke-Wulf Triebflugel proposal contributed to the lineage, and what modern eVTOL designers still take from the experiment. France's bravest failure.
