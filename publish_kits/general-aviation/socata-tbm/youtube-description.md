The TBM was born from a 1987 joint venture between Mooney in Kerrville and SOCATA in Tarbes — a deliberate attempt to build something pressurized, fast, and small enough for an owner-pilot to fly themselves. Mooney bailed in 1991. SOCATA, now folded into Daher, has built every TBM since.

Thirty-plus years and roughly a thousand airframes later, the latest TBM 960 cruises at 330 knots true on a single PT6E-66XT, all the way to FL310, and it does it with two pilots on the front office or one in the left seat.
