The Starck AS-57 is one of André Starck's small but persistent series of post-war French light aircraft. A two-seat, side-by-side, low-wing single — only about ten airframes built, with at least one still active in living memory.

In this video: the Starck design philosophy, what made the AS-57 different from his other small singles, the realities of micro-production in post-war France, and the small community of enthusiasts that has kept the type's memory alive.
