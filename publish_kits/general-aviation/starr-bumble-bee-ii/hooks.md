# Hooks for Starr Bumble Bee II

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The smallest manned airplane ever built. Then it crashed.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1988, Robert Starr finally beat his rival Don Stits for the title of World's Smallest Airplane. The Bumble Bee II was 8 feet 10 inches long, a 5-foot-9 wingspan, and weighed under 400 pounds with Starr in it. The record-setting flight worked. Then, on the same day, the engine quit on climbout and the Bumble Bee II crashed. Starr survived. The wreck is in a museum. Today, the airplane built only to be the smallest.
