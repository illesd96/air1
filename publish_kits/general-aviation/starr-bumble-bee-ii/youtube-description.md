The Starr Bumble Bee II is an experimental aircraft built specifically to capture the title of the World's Smallest Airplane. Designed and flown by Robert H. Starr in Arizona, it set the record on 8 May 1988 — wingspan of about 5.5 feet, length of 8 feet 10 inches, gross weight around 400 pounds. The record flight succeeded. On the same day, the engine failed on climb-out and the aircraft crashed; Starr was seriously injured but survived.

In this video: the decades-long rivalry between Robert Starr and Don Stits over the smallest-airplane record, the engineering compromises of an aircraft this small (control authority, stall behavior, ground handling), the surviving Bumble Bee II at the Pima Air & Space Museum, and what the design teaches about the limits of human-carrying aerodynamics.
