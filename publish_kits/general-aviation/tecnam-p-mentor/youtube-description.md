The Tecnam P-Mentor is an Italian two-seat, low-wing trainer purpose-built in Capua for ab-initio through instrument flight training. It entered service in April 2022 and is EASA CS-23 type certified, then later FAA Part 23 and Transport Canada validated. Power comes from a 100 horsepower Rotax 912iS Sport burning mogas at roughly 17 to 19 litres per hour cruise, a fraction of the operating cost of a legacy 172. The cockpit is Garmin glass with an MPP simulator-of-the-aircraft option, and a constant-speed prop variant brings it into instrument trainer territory. Flight schools from Lufthansa Aviation Training to North American collegiate programs have signed for fleets. We cover the Rotax 912iS in trainer service, the relationship between the P-Mentor and Tecnam's P2008 and P2010, how the P-Mentor fits into MPL-style integrated programs, and what student and instructor reports look like after the first three years of service.
