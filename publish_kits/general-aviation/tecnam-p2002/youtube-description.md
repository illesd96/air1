When the Pascale brothers restarted Tecnam in the 1990s, they bet on the European microlight rules and the Rotax 912. The P2002 Sierra, introduced in the early 2000s, became the proof. A clean low-wing all-metal two-seater with sliding canopy, 100 horsepower of liquid-cooled Austrian-engineered torque, and fuel burns flight schools could actually afford. It quickly grew into seventy percent of Tecnam's available production capacity and became the default trainer at clubs from Scotland to Sicily. We look at the JF club-trainer variant, the differences between the early Sierra and the modern Sierra Mk II, why the airframe scaled up into a family of Tecnams, and what to check on a 2,000-hour school airframe before signing the bill of sale. Comments open for instructors and owners with stick time.
