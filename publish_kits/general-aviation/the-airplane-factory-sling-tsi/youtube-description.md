The Airplane Factory, now Sling Aircraft, was founded by Mike Blyth and James Pitman in Johannesburg South. After the Sling 2 famously circumnavigated the world in 2009, the company kept iterating: the Sling 4, the high-wing Sling High Wing, and the Sling TSi. The TSi mates the four-seat low-wing airframe to a 141 horsepower Rotax 915iS turbo, giving cruise in the high 130s of knots on mogas with a useful load that actually works for a family of four. It is sold as a kit, supported with a builder assist program, and has built a near-cult following on YouTube and at Sun n Fun. We cover the airframe lineage, why the 915 changed the math for kit builders, what a builder assist week in Torrance actually looks like, and what to budget honestly for an avionics-equipped completion in 2026.
