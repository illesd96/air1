In 2009 Mike Blyth and James Pitman flew the second Sling 2 prototype around the world westbound in 40 days, the first time any aircraft of the Sling 2's class had done so. The Sling 2, originally The Airplane Factory Sling 2, is a South African two-seat side-by-side LSA built in Johannesburg South: all-metal, low-wing, Rotax 912 ULS powered, with an unusually generous cabin and useful load for the class. It sells as a kit or factory-built and has spawned a whole family: Sling 4, Sling TSi, Sling High Wing. We trace the founders' decision to fly the prototype around the world rather than market it conventionally, what the trip proved about the airframe and the Rotax 912, how the Sling community organizes itself around YouTube videos and builder assist programs, and what to inspect on a high-time school Sling 2 before committing to a purchase.
