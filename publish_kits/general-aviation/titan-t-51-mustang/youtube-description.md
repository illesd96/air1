The Titan T-51 Mustang is a roughly three-quarter scale replica of the North American P-51 Mustang, designed by Titan Aircraft owner John Williams and sold as a homebuilt kit. Two seats in tandem, dual controls, a bubble canopy, and powertrain options ranging from a converted Suzuki six-cylinder to a Honda V8 producing 200+ horsepower.

In this video: how Williams achieved a credible P-51 silhouette at amateur-builder scale, the powertrain choices and what each does for performance, the EAA workshop culture that turns garages into warbird factories, the realistic build hours and total cost, and the honest answer to 'is it like flying a real Mustang' — from pilots who fly both.
