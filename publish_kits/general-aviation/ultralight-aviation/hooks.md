# Hooks for ultralight aviation

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

No license. No medical. Yes, it's legal.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1982, the FAA looked at the explosion of hang-glider-with-an-engine designs and did something unprecedented: created Part 103, a class of aircraft you can legally fly with zero pilot certificates, no medical, no registration. A 254-pound single-seat sport aircraft on five gallons of fuel. Forty years later, ultralight aviation is the strangest, freest corner of GA — and the most misunderstood. Here's how it actually works.
