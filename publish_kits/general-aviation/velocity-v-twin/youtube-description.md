Velocity Aircraft, based in Sebastian, Florida, evolved its single-engine XL canard into the Velocity V-Twin: a four-seat composite pusher canard with two engines, a single vertical tail, and the kind of unique ramp presence that empties every fly-in coffee shop. Built around the Long-EZ canard tradition Burt Rutan started but engineered for kit-buildable scale, the V-Twin pairs roughly 200 horsepower per side with the canard's stall-resistant characteristics to give a light twin that lands at speeds many singles cannot. We look at the engineering trade between Velocity XL and V-Twin, what Vmc actually means on a canard pusher, what builders say about the build complexity compared to a single-engine Velocity, and how the V-Twin community has grown since first deliveries. Niche, expensive, magnificent.
