# Hooks for Yak-36

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Before the Harrier, the Soviets built this. It barely flew.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1963, Yakovlev wheeled out a thing called the Yak-36 — a stubby, twin-jet experimental VTOL with two engines pointed straight at the ground and a long droop nose that looked like nothing else on the flight line. It hovered, it crashed, it inspired the Yak-38 that actually went to sea, and it gave the Soviet Navy its first taste of jet-borne vertical flight. Today, the awkward prototype that taught the USSR how hard VTOL really is.
