The Yakovlev Yak-36 first lifted off in 1963 as a Soviet technology demonstrator — a way to find out whether the USSR could build a jet that took off straight up. Two Tumansky R27-300 turbojets, a single pilot, a fragile little nose probe, and a flight envelope so narrow that ground crews flagged the rotation off a tether.

In this video: why the Soviet Navy wanted VTOL in the first place, what the Yak-36 actually proved, the crashes that nearly ended the program, and how its hard-earned lessons fed directly into the Yak-38 Forger that finally went to sea on the Kiev-class carriers.
