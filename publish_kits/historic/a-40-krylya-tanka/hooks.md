# Hooks for A-40 Krylya Tanka

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1942 the Soviets bolted glider wings onto a T-60 tank and tried to fly it into combat.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

It sounds like a joke. It isn't. In 1942, Soviet engineer Oleg Antonov designed a flying tank — a T-60 light tank with detachable biplane wings and tail surfaces — that was towed aloft by a heavy bomber, glided to a landing zone, and then shed its glider gear and fought as a regular tank. One prototype flew. It barely flew. The program was cancelled. Here's the story of a Soviet experiment that crossed every line between absurd and prescient.
