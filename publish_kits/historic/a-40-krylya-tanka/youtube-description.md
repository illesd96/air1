The Antonov A-40 Krylya Tanka — Russian for 'tank wings' — was a Soviet attempt to allow a tank to glide onto a battlefield after being towed aloft by an airplane, in support of airborne forces or partisans. The aircraft was designed by Oleg Antonov in 1941 and a single prototype was built in 1942 and tested. The configuration used a T-60 light tank with its turret in a fixed position and detachable biplane wings and twin-boom tail surfaces; the assembly was towed aloft by a Tupolev TB-3 bomber. The single test flight in September 1942 was successful only in the sense that the aircraft did not crash. The TB-3 could barely tow the load, the A-40 had to release early at low altitude, and the tank landed and immediately abandoned the glider components. The program was cancelled.
