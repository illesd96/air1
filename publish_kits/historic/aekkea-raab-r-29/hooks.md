# Hooks for AEKKEA-RAAB R-29

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1936, Greece tried to build its own fighter aircraft. The result had a German designer, a parasol wing, and a top speed that would embarrass a modern Cessna.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Greece in the 1930s had no real aviation industry, so they hired German designer Antonius Raab to bolt together a parasol-wing fighter-trainer in an Athens workshop. The AEKKEA-RAAB R-29 is the obscure Greek aircraft almost no aviation book mentions. Here's why one tiny country tried to compete with Messerschmitt, and how it failed.
