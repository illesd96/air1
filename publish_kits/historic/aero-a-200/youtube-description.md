The Aero A.200 was a Czechoslovak sport plane designed and built specifically to compete in Challenge 1934, the European touring plane championships held that year in Warsaw. It was a four-seater low-wing monoplane optimized around the contest's particular scoring formula — fuel economy, takeoff and landing distance, payload, comfort, and an extended cross-country rally. Aero Vodochody built three examples. The Czechoslovak entry placed respectably but was beaten by the Polish RWD-9, which had been engineered with the same single-purpose specificity. The A.200 nonetheless remains one of the more interesting interwar examples of an aircraft engineered to a competition rule book rather than to operational requirements. In this video we walk through the Challenge 1934 ruleset, how Aero's engineers translated those rules into a design, how the airplane actually performed at the contest, and what happened to the three examples after Czechoslovakia's annexation in 1938-39.
