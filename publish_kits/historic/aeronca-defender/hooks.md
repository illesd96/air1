# Hooks for Aeronca Defender

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

65 horsepower. Cost less than a Ford sedan. Spotted artillery for the US Army in Europe.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1940 the Aeronca Defender cost $1,495 - less than a new Ford. Sixty-five horsepower. Tandem seating. It looked like a tube-and-fabric toy. Then the US Army bought it, called it the L-3 Grasshopper, and sent it to Europe to spot artillery. Slow enough to fly under radar. Quiet enough to creep over German lines. The forgotten cousin of the Piper Cub had a real war.
