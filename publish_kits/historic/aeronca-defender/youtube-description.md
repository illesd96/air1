Most people know the Piper J-3 Cub. Almost nobody outside collectors knows the Aeronca Defender - which is unfair, because it was built in the same era, by a smaller company, for the same mission, and went to war too.

The Defender came out of Aeronca's Middletown, Ohio factory in 1940. Sixty-five horsepower Continental A-65. Tube-and-fabric. Tandem seats. Two doors that swung up like gull wings. Civil Aeronautics Administration certified it as a Civilian Pilot Training Program aircraft - the program that produced over 400,000 wartime pilots before the US even entered WWII. When the Army needed liaison and artillery-spotting aircraft, they ordered Defenders as L-3 Grasshoppers. The L-3 flew over D-Day, Italy, North Africa, and the Pacific - slow enough to creep over enemy lines, quiet enough not to be heard, durable enough to land on roads. Piper got the legend. Aeronca got the forgotten chapter.
