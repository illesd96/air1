# Hooks for Aichi AB-6

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1933, Aichi built a single floatplane reconnaissance prototype for the Imperial Japanese Navy. They picked the Kawanishi design instead. The Aichi AB-6 was scrapped before anyone outside Aichi remembered its name.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Aichi AB-6, also called the Experimental 7-Shi Reconnaissance Seaplane, was Aichi's bid in the 1932 Imperial Japanese Navy 7-Shi competition. Aichi built one. Kawanishi built the E7K, which won. The AB-6 disappeared. This is the obscure Japanese floatplane that lost the contract that shaped the IJN's wartime reconnaissance fleet.
