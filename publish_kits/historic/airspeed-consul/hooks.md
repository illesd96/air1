# Hooks for Airspeed Consul

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It wasn't built to be an airliner. It was a 1946 conversion of a surplus military trainer — and a famous novelist had once part-owned the factory.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When the war ended in 1945, Britain had thousands of surplus Airspeed Oxford trainers, no money, and a desperate need for small airliners to reach a colonial empire that was about to dissolve. The Airspeed Consul was the answer — and the man whose company built it would go on to write A Town Like Alice and On the Beach.
