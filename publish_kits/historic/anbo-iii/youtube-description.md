The ANBO III was a 1929 parasol-wing monoplane training and utility aircraft — and the first Lithuanian design ever produced in series. Two batches of four aircraft were built in 1930 and 1931, for a total of eight. The aircraft also served in reconnaissance duties for the small Lithuanian Air Force.

Its designer, Antanas Gustaitis, is one of the most remarkable figures in interwar Baltic aviation. Trained as an army officer in newly independent Lithuania, he taught himself aircraft design, established a workshop at Kaunas, and produced a series of aircraft known by his ANBO designation (Antanas Nori Būti Ore — 'Antanas Wants to Be in the Air'). He rose to become commander of the Lithuanian Air Force.

In 1940 the Soviet Union occupied Lithuania. Gustaitis was arrested by the NKVD in 1941 and executed at Butovo near Moscow in October 1941. Most of his aircraft were destroyed in the 1941 chaos.
