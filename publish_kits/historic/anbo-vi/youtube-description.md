The ANBO VI was a parasol-wing monoplane designed for the Lithuanian Army as a trainer in 1933, based on the earlier ANBO III. It featured revised landing gear and a more powerful engine — both improvements aimed at fixing operational complaints from instructors and student pilots. Like every ANBO type, it was the personal work of Antanas Gustaitis, an engineer who effectively built Lithuania's national aviation industry himself. The ANBO VI never fought, never broke records, and never sold abroad — but it represented something rare: an interwar trainer designed and constructed entirely inside a state that had only existed as an independent country for about a decade. When the Soviet Union occupied Lithuania in 1940 the surviving ANBO airframes were absorbed, scattered, or destroyed.
