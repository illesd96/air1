The ANBO VIII first flew on September 5, 1939, four days into Germany's invasion of Poland. It was the most ambitious of General Antanas Gustaitis's ANBO series of Lithuanian-designed aircraft: a single-engined low-wing monoplane attack bomber, intended to give the Karo Aviacija a modern striking arm. In June 1940 the USSR annexed Lithuania, the Lithuanian Air Force was disbanded, and Gustaitis was arrested. He was executed by the NKVD on October 16, 1941. The sole ANBO VIII prototype disappeared during the German invasion of 1941. In this video we examine the design itself, a clean and broadly modern airframe for 1939, trace Gustaitis's role as Lithuania's only true aircraft designer through the entire ANBO I through VIII lineage, look at how the small Baltic states maintained independent aviation industries on shoestring budgets, and explain why so little physical evidence of the ANBO VIII survives. We also clarify why the aircraft is sometimes mis-attributed to 'Karo Aviacijos Tiekimo Skyrius,' which was the supply division, not the designer.
