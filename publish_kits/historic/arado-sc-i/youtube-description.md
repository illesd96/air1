The Arado SC I was a 1926 German biplane trainer derived from the earlier S I, but with a more powerful inline engine and considerable structural strengthening. It was intended for the secret German military flying school at Lipetsk in the Soviet Union — a base that operated from 1925 to 1933 under a clandestine Reichswehr-Red Army agreement that allowed Germany to evade Versailles restrictions on military aviation.

In the end, the SC I was not accepted for Lipetsk service. A small number were built instead for the Deutsche Verkehrsfliegerschule, the German civilian airline training school — itself effectively a thinly-disguised pilot pipeline for the future Luftwaffe. Arado Flugzeugwerke would later become one of the most important Luftwaffe contractors of the WWII period, producing aircraft including the Ar 234 jet bomber.

This video covers the SC I design, the extraordinary Lipetsk story, the Deutsche Verkehrsfliegerschule, and how Weimar-era aircraft like this one connect directly to the buildup of the Luftwaffe a decade later.
