The Arado SC II was a 1928 German biplane trainer — a refined successor to the SC I that had been rejected for the secret Lipetsk flying school. The SC II went into modest production, mostly for the Deutsche Verkehrsfliegerschule, the so-called 'commercial pilots' school' that in practice was a thinly-disguised pilot pipeline for what would become the Luftwaffe.

The story here is one of institutional camouflage. Officially, every aircraft built for the DVS was for civil commercial pilot training. In practice, the school's graduates went straight into Reichswehr aviation units that, themselves, were officially something else. The SC II is a small piece of a much larger pattern that ran across roughly a dozen German aircraft companies through the 1920s and into the early 1930s.

This video covers the design changes from SC I to SC II, the DVS, the wider pattern of clandestine military aviation under Weimar, and Arado's later wartime trajectory.
