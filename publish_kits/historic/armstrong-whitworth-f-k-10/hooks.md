# Hooks for Armstrong Whitworth F.K.10

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1916 a British engineer decided four wings were better than two — and built a quadruplane fighter. It didn't end well.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Armstrong Whitworth F.K.10 was one of a handful of operational quadruplane aircraft ever built. Frederik Koolhoven — yes, the same Dutch designer who would later run Koolhoven in the Netherlands — designed it during WWI. The RFC ordered a few, never used them in combat, and quietly moved on.
