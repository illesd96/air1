# Hooks for Avia BH-16

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Czechoslovakia built a wooden ultralight in 1924 with a sixteen-horsepower engine — and it actually flew.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Sixteen horsepower. That is what a Vaslin engine produced when fitted to the Avia BH-16. The Czechoslovak company Avia wanted to see how light a usable monoplane could actually be made — and the BH-16 was the answer. It was a single-seat, low-wing, braced wooden monoplane, and it represents the absolute minimum end of the interwar sport-aircraft envelope. Here's how Czechoslovakia ended up at the leading edge of ultralight design.
