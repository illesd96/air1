The Avia BH-16 was a single-seat very light sport aircraft built in Czechoslovakia in 1924. Like other early Avia designs by Pavel Beneš and Miroslav Hajn, it was a low-wing braced monoplane of wooden construction. The aircraft could be powered by either a 12 kW (16 hp) four-cylinder Vaslin engine or a 19 kW (26 hp) inverted-V twin-Blackburne Tomtit. At those power levels every gram of structural weight mattered, and the BH-16 was effectively a research aircraft testing how usable an absolute-minimum airframe could be. A small number were built and flown, and at least one was demonstrated abroad as part of Avia's effort to find civil export customers. In this video we walk through the BH-16's structure, what flying it would actually have been like, why Avia bothered with such a marginal aircraft in 1924, and how the lessons from the BH-16 fed into Avia's far more famous BH-21 fighter and subsequent designs.
