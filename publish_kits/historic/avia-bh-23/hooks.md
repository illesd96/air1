# Hooks for Avia BH-23

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Czechoslovakia built a dedicated night fighter in 1926 — and the air force didn't want it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Night fighting in 1926 was barely a concept. There were no real radar systems. The aircraft did the work with searchlights, signal flares, and instinct. So when Avia's chief engineers proposed a dedicated night fighter — the BH-23 — based on their successful BH-21 day fighter, the Czechoslovak Air Force shrugged. Here's why a prescient design choice failed because the doctrine for it didn't exist yet.
