The Avia BH-23 was a prototype night fighter aircraft built in Czechoslovakia in 1926. The design was derived from the BH-21 day fighter and incorporated structural changes made for the BH-22 trainer; it was originally designated BH-22N. Searchlights and other night-flying equipment were added — radio-direction-finding gear, instrument lighting, and exhaust shrouding to limit muzzle flash impairment to the pilot's night vision. The Czechoslovak Air Force, like most European air forces in the mid-1920s, had no formal night fighting doctrine and no requirement for the type. No order followed. In this video we walk through what 1926 night fighting actually looked like operationally, why dedicated night fighter design largely had to wait for the radar revolution of the late 1930s to make tactical sense, what the BH-23 inherited from the BH-21, and how Avia's engineering team treated the project as essentially a self-funded technology demonstrator.
