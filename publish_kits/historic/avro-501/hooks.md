# Hooks for Avro 501

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Before the Sopwith Camel, Britain put a single giant float under a biplane and called it a navy.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1913, the Royal Navy didn't have a real naval air arm. What it had was experiments. The Avro Type H, also known as the Avro 501 and 503, was one of those experiments — a biplane originally fitted with one enormous central float and two tiny outriggers, like a duck balanced on a swan. It was clumsy, fragile, and a critical step on the road to British naval aviation. Here's how a forgotten Edwardian seaplane helped invent the carrier age.
