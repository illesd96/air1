Operation Chastise. The Dam Busters Raid. 617 Squadron. The bouncing bomb. You've heard the headlines - here's what the film didn't have time for.

The Lancaster was built around a four-engine error: the twin-engine Manchester had been a disaster. Avro's chief designer Roy Chadwick kept the airframe and bolted on four Merlins. The result became the only Allied heavy bomber that could carry a 22,000 lb Grand Slam. On the Dams raid, Barnes Wallis' Upkeep weapon worked - but the Sorpe dam survived, the Mohne and Eder breached killed 1,600 civilians (most of them slave laborers), and Bomber Command lost 40% of the attacking force. We look at the actual operational results, not the legend.
