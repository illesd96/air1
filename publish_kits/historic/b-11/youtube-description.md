The Bratukhin B-11 was a prototype Soviet transverse twin-rotor liaison helicopter and the last design produced by the Bratukhin aircraft design bureau. Two prototypes were built and flown in 1948. Ivan Bratukhin had been the Soviet Union's leading exponent of the transverse twin-rotor layout — two main rotors on outrigger pylons rather than a single main rotor and tail rotor — since the early 1940s, with the Omega series of designs as predecessors. The B-11 represented his most refined attempt. A fatal accident during testing and the parallel success of Mikhail Mil's single-rotor Mi-1 caused the Soviet leadership to consolidate helicopter development around Mil's bureau and shut Bratukhin's program down.
