The Doolittle Raid did almost no physical damage to Japan. The Japanese military shifted strategy because of it - that's the part that mattered.

On April 18, 1942, sixteen B-25 Mitchells launched from USS Hornet 650 miles east of Japan - 200 miles further than planned, because they were spotted. None of those crews had ever made a carrier launch. None of them would survive the war intact: 11 men captured, 4 executed by Japan, dozens killed in the bailouts and crashes in China. But the raid forced Japan to keep four front-line fighter groups in the home islands, accelerated Yamamoto's push for Midway, and gave America one good news story in the darkest six months of the Pacific War.
