Twelve Boeing 314 Clippers were built. None survive. They were the largest, most luxurious civil aircraft of their time - and within ten years of their first flight they were obsolete, scrapped, sunk, or rotting on Pacific atolls.

The 314 was a flying ocean liner. Berths for 36 sleeping passengers. A dining room with white-jacketed stewards. A bridal suite. They flew the Pacific in five days, San Francisco to Hong Kong, with overnight stops at Honolulu, Midway, Wake, and Guam. In January 1943 the Dixie Clipper carried Franklin Roosevelt to the Casablanca Conference - the first wartime overseas presidential flight in history. We trace what happened to all twelve, and why long-range land planes killed the flying boat in a single decade.
