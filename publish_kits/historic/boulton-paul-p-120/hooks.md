# Hooks for Boulton Paul P.120

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The Boulton Paul P.120 was Britain's last attempt to make delta wings work. It flew for three months in 1952 and then disintegrated mid-air over Salisbury Plain.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In August 1952, Boulton Paul's chief designer rolled out the P.120, a tailless delta research aircraft meant to prove the configuration for British fighters. It flew brilliantly for three months. Then on August 29, the elevon fluttered, the tail came off, and the test pilot ejected. It was the company's last design ever flown. This is the British delta you've never heard of.
