# Hooks for Brunswick LF-1 Zaunkönig

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In the middle of WWII, a German engineering professor designed an aircraft so easy to fly that a complete novice could solo it. They called it the Zaunkönig, the 'wren.' One survives in Britain today, captured at war's end.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Professor Hermann Winter at the TU Braunschweig was asked a simple question in 1939: can you build an aircraft that anyone can fly solo with no training? His answer was the LF-1 Zaunkönig, a STOL light aircraft with such forgiving handling that students could fly it before lessons. It first flew December 1940. The British grabbed it postwar and flew it for decades. This is the foolproof aircraft of the war years.
