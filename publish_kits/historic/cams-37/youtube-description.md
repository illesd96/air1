The CAMS 37 was a French biplane flying boat designed by the Italian-French engineer Maurice Hurel for Chantiers Aero-Maritimes de la Seine. First flown in 1926, it was intended primarily for naval reconnaissance — but in service it ended up doing everything: long-range patrol, mail, photography, training, and finally, in the strange last years of its career, light coastal duties for Vichy France and Free French units.

By the time the type was finally retired around 1941, it had been in front-line French service for fifteen years — longer than almost any biplane flying boat in any other major navy. Around 300 were built across several sub-variants.

This video covers the design, the role of Italian engineers in French interwar aviation, the type's extraordinary career arc through to 1941, and the few surviving artifacts in French naval museums.
