The CANT 18 was a 1920s Italian biplane flying boat trainer built by Cantieri Aeronautici e Navali Triestini in Trieste. Developed from the earlier CANT 7, it featured Warren-truss wing bracing in place of the older strut arrangement and a number of hull refinements aimed at making it easier to handle on the water. It was used by the Italian civil flying schools to prepare pilots for the larger CANT and SIAI-Marchetti flying-boat airliners that operated across the Adriatic.

Trieste's role here is worth noting: the city had been Austro-Hungarian until 1918, and CANT inherited a workforce already familiar with naval and maritime engineering. That cross-disciplinary skill base — boats and aircraft together — made the city one of the most distinctive aviation centres in interwar Europe.
