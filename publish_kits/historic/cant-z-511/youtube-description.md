The CANT Z.511 was a four-engine long-range seaplane designed by Filippo Zappata of Cantieri Riuniti dell'Adriatico. Originally conceived for Central and South Atlantic passenger routes, it became one of the largest single-hull floatplanes ever flown. With Italy's entry into the war the aircraft was reassigned to military transport and special operations roles. The Regia Aeronautica explored using the type for an attack on shipping in New York Harbor using carried small assault craft — and yes, that operation was seriously studied, though it was never executed and the aircraft never crossed the Atlantic. Only two airframes were built; both were lost during the war, one in an Allied air attack at Lake Trasimeno. In this video we separate what is documented from what is rumor, walk through Zappata's design, examine the actual range performance claimed, and look at why the New York plan never had a serious chance of working.
