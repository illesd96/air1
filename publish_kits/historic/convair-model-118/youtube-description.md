The Convair Model 118 ConvAirCar was a prototype flying car developed in 1947 by Theodore Hall and the Convair company, intended for mainstream consumers. The design used a small two-door automobile with a detachable wing-engine pod that the owner would rent at the airport, fly between cities, and detach on landing. Two prototypes were built. The first was lost in November 1947 in an accident attributed to fuel exhaustion — the pilot reportedly checked the car fuel gauge rather than the aircraft fuel gauge before takeoff. The second airframe was rebuilt from salvaged parts and flown, but by the time it was airworthy again Convair's interest, its dealer network's confidence, and the project's price target had all collapsed. In this video we look at what Theodore Hall actually engineered, why Convair backed the program, what realistic operating costs would have been, and why no flying car project since has gotten as close to production as the Model 118 briefly seemed to.
