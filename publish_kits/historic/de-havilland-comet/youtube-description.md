On May 2, 1952, BOAC flew the world's first commercial jet service - London to Johannesburg in a de Havilland Comet 1. Less than two years later, three Comets had broken up in mid-air and the entire fleet was grounded.

The cause was metal fatigue around square window cutouts - stress concentrated at the corners with every pressurization cycle until the fuselage tore. The Royal Aircraft Establishment proved it by pressurizing G-ALYU in a water tank thousands of times until it failed at the corner of a window, exactly as predicted. The investigation defined the modern science of fatigue analysis - and gave Boeing time to perfect the 707. Britain led the jet age for two years and lost it for the rest of the century. Comet 4s eventually flew for BOAC and others until 1981, redesigned and reinforced.
