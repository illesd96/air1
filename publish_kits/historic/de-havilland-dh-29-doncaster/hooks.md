# Hooks for De Havilland DH.29 Doncaster

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1921, de Havilland built the world's first long-range high-wing monoplane airliner — and only two were ever made.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Two aircraft. That is the entire production run of the de Havilland DH.29 Doncaster, a long-range high-wing monoplane built in Britain in 1921. The Air Ministry commissioned it to test whether the cantilever monoplane configuration could outperform the biplane on long routes. The answer turned out to be yes, eventually — but not with the Doncaster. Here's the story of an aircraft that proved a concept and then quietly disappeared.
