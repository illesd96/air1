# Hooks for De Havilland DH.93 Don

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The RAF ordered 250 of these in 1937. They cancelled most of them and used the survivors as ground instructional airframes. Why?

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The de Havilland DH.93 Don was supposed to be the RAF's universal trainer for everything — gunnery, navigation, radio, wireless. Instead, it became one of the most embarrassing procurement misjudgments of pre-war Britain. This is the story of an aircraft cancelled almost as soon as it entered service.
