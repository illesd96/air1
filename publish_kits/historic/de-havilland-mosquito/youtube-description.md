The de Havilland Mosquito should not have worked. Wood, no guns, two pilots, two engines, painted black. In 1941 the Air Ministry only accepted it because de Havilland built it on their own money. By 1942 it was the fastest operational aircraft in the world. The Luftwaffe's interceptors could not catch one.

The Mosquito flew bomber, photo recon, pathfinder, intruder, night fighter, anti-shipping, and high-speed mail roles. Operation Jericho - the February 1944 pinpoint attack on Amiens Prison to free Resistance prisoners before execution - is one of the most precise air strikes ever flown. The wooden airframe meant balsa and plywood sandwich panels could be subcontracted out to furniture makers and piano factories, dramatically scaling production. Postwar, the wooden construction also meant most of them rotted - very few airworthy survivors remain.
