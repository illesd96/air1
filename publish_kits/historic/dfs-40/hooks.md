# Hooks for DFS 40

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Before the Me 163 Komet, Alexander Lippisch built the DFS 40, a tailless research aircraft that proved the delta-wing layout actually worked. It flew once and was nearly forgotten.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Alexander Lippisch had been chasing the tailless ideal for a decade when he built the DFS 40, originally the Delta V, in 1937. It was the direct ancestor of the Me 163. Only one was built, it flew briefly in 1939, and it disappeared in the chaos of WWII. This is the forgotten testbed that made rocket flight possible.
