The Dietrich DP.II Bussard was a 1923 German two-seat training biplane designed by Richard Dietrich and built by the Dietrich-Gobiet Flugzeugwerke at Kassel. It is one of dozens of small 'sport' or 'training' aircraft that emerged in Weimar Germany — and which, taken together, kept the country's aircraft industry alive during a period when the Treaty of Versailles banned military aviation development.

This is not to claim the DP.II was a secret warplane — it was not. But the existence of firms like Dietrich-Gobiet, and pilots like those who flew DP.IIs at the German civil flying schools, meant Germany never actually lost its design culture or its trained aircrew base. When the Luftwaffe was officially re-formed in 1935, the foundation was already there, sitting in dozens of clubhouses and small Kassel-style works.

This video looks at the design, the Dietrich-Gobiet works, the wider Weimar aviation context, and what survives.
