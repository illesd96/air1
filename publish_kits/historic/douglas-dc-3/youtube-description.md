Eisenhower named four pieces of equipment as essential to Allied victory: the jeep, the 2.5-ton truck, the bazooka, and the C-47. Three of those are gone. The C-47 - the DC-3 in military uniform - is still flying paying cargo in 2026.

The DC-3 was built around a 1935 understanding of structural design - and that understanding turned out to be so close to optimal for its mission that 91 years of progress has not actually displaced it from the bush, the Arctic, and the cargo strip. It carried paratroopers on D-Day, kept Berlin alive in 1948, flew the Hump into China, and still serves Buffalo Airways and Basler Turbo Conversions today. Every replacement has been more expensive to operate. We look at why.
