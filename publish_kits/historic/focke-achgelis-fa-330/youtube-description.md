The Focke-Achgelis Fa 330 Bachstelze (Wagtail) is a type of rotary-wing kite — a rotor kite — towed behind German Type IXD2 U-boats during the Second World War to allow a lookout to see significantly further than from the conning tower. About 200 were built by Weser Flugzeugbau. The Fa 330 was unpowered: the rotor was spun up by hand or by airflow as the U-boat moved at surface speed, and lift was generated autorotatively. A 150-metre tether connected the kite to the submarine. From an altitude of roughly 120 metres a sighting horizon of around 40 km could be achieved, compared to perhaps 9 km from the conning tower. The system was used operationally only in the Indian Ocean, where the threat of Allied aircraft was lower; in the Atlantic the rapid-recovery problem made deployment too dangerous.
