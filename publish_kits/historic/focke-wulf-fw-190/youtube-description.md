When the Fw 190 entered service in August 1941, it produced a panic in Fighter Command not seen since the Battle of Britain - and the British were so desperate to study one they planned 'Operation Airthief,' a commando raid to steal one.

They didn't need to. In June 1942 Oberleutnant Armin Faber landed his Fw 190A-3 at RAF Pembrey by mistake - he thought he was in France. That single aircraft let the British engineer the Spitfire Mk IX response. But here's what's quietly remarkable about the Fw 190: Kurt Tank deliberately chose a radial BMW 801 over the Bf 109's inline DB 601, and the radial took battle damage that would have killed an inline. The 190 was the better workhorse - and the late-war 'Dora' D-9 with the inline Jumo finally combined both philosophies.
