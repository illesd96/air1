The Focke-Wulf GL 18 was a 1920s German light transport monoplane designed and built by Focke-Wulf at a time when the company was still a small Bremen firm with a few dozen employees. The aircraft is barely a footnote even in dedicated Focke-Wulf type books. That is exactly why it is interesting. In the late 1920s, Focke-Wulf was trying to find a viable niche in the crowded German civil transport market, competing against Junkers, Dornier, and Rohrbach — companies with deep pockets, government backing, and serious metal-monoplane experience. The GL 18 was Focke-Wulf's attempt at a small operator's airliner. It did not succeed commercially. In this video we walk through what the aircraft was structurally, why the timing was wrong, and how the experience shaped the later, far more famous Focke-Wulf designs of the 1930s and 1940s.
