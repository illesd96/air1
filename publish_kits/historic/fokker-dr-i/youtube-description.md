The Red Baron and the red triplane. The most iconic pairing in aviation history - and one of the most distorted. Manfred von Richthofen scored only 19 of his 80 victories in the Fokker Dr.I. The aircraft was rushed into service to counter the Sopwith Triplane, suffered fatal wing failures, and was withdrawn for six weeks for structural repairs before being reissued.

Richthofen was killed in his Dr.I on April 21, 1918 - and the killing shot, recent forensic analysis shows, almost certainly came from an Australian machine gunner on the ground, not Roy Brown's Camel above him. He bled out within seconds from a single .303 round through the chest. The triplane he was flying went on display in a London shop window within weeks - the war wasn't even over.
