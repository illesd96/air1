# Hooks for Ford Trimotor

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

All metal. Three engines. And Henry Ford may not have actually designed it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1926 Henry Ford bought a struggling aircraft company so he could put his name on what would become the first practical American airliner. The Ford Trimotor - the Tin Goose - flew passengers when most Americans thought aviation was for daredevils. The corrugated skin, the three engines, the Ford badge - and the engineer Ford may have stolen the design from.
