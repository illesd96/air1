Most people think Henry Ford designed the Tin Goose. He didn't. William Bushnell Stout did, and Ford bought his company.

The Ford Trimotor of 1926 came out of Stout Metal Airplane Company - refined as the Ford 4-AT and 5-AT after Ford acquired Stout in 1925. The corrugated all-metal construction owed directly to Junkers, and Junkers' lawyers came calling. But the Ford Trimotor did what no American airliner had done before: it made scheduled passenger air travel routine. By 1929 you could fly New York to Los Angeles by Ford Trimotor and Pullman train in 48 hours - the first transcontinental air service. Two airworthy 5-ATs still fly EAA rides today.
