# Hooks for Giffard dirigible

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1852, a French engineer strapped a 3-horsepower steam engine to a hydrogen balloon and invented powered flight 51 years before the Wright brothers.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Before the airplane, before the Zeppelin, before anyone knew what 'aviation' meant, Henri Giffard climbed into a cigar-shaped hydrogen envelope hanging above a coal-fired steam engine and flew 27 kilometers from Paris to Trappes. This is the story of the first machine that ever flew where its pilot wanted it to go.
