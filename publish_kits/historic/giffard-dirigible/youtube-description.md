On September 24, 1852, Henri Giffard lifted off from the Paris Hippodrome aboard a 44-meter hydrogen-filled envelope, dangling beneath it a coal-fired steam engine producing roughly 3 horsepower. He flew 27 km to Trappes at about 10 km/h, the first human in history to fly a powered, steerable aircraft. In this video we unpack what Giffard actually achieved (and what he didn't), why the steam engine was a death sentence above hydrogen, and how his triangular sail-rudder gave birth to every dirigible that followed. We separate the romantic legend from the engineering, including why he couldn't fly back into the wind, and how his injector pump invention quietly funded the entire aerostat industry. If you think aviation started in 1903 at Kitty Hawk, this footnote rewrites the timeline.
