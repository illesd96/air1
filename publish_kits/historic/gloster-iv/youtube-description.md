The Gloster IV was a single-engined biplane racing floatplane designed and produced by the British aviation manufacturer Gloster Aircraft Company for the 1927 Schneider Trophy contest at Venice. Three were built — the Gloster IVA, IVB, and IVC — each tuned slightly differently around the Napier Lion engine. While the contest was actually won that year by R. J. Mitchell's Supermarine S.5 monoplane, the Gloster IV was an important parallel development that pushed British biplane construction methods to their absolute limit. It is, by reasonable consensus, one of the fastest biplane floatplanes ever built.
