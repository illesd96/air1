The Heinkel HE 10 was a trainer floatplane developed by Heinkel Flugzeugwerke in the late 1920s. Heinkel, founded in 1922 by Ernst Heinkel, spent the Weimar period as a small specialist company largely focused on floatplane and catapult-launched designs — types that fell into the legal civil-aviation exception in the Versailles framework, and that could be quietly exported. The HE 10 fits into that pattern. Surviving records on the type are thin; production was small and most documentation was destroyed during the Second World War. In this video we walk through what the HE 10 was structurally, why Heinkel as a company specialized in floatplanes specifically, how the export market for German civilian-classified trainers actually worked in the 1920s, and how this commercial period set up the much larger Heinkel of the 1930s.
