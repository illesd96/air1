The Spruce Goose isn't made of spruce. It's mostly birch. Hughes hated the name. And it flew - barely, briefly - on purpose, not by accident.

In 1947 Howard Hughes was being torn apart by a Senate hearing investigating wartime contracts. The H-4 had cost taxpayers $23 million and produced nothing usable for the war. The November 2 'taxi test' was a calculated PR move - and it worked. Hughes kept the aircraft fully maintained in a climate-controlled hangar for the next 29 years, ready to fly, paid for out of pocket. This is the story of why one minute of flight saved a man's reputation, and what that tells us about how aviation history actually gets made.
