The IFIL-Reghin RG-4 Pionier was a single-seat primary glider built in the town of Reghin in Transylvania, Romania. Fifty examples were produced from 1954 onward, making it one of the founding aircraft of the postwar Romanian gliding movement and a quiet symbol of communist-era national industry.

Reghin was already known across the Eastern Bloc as a centre of woodworking — the town's furniture and musical-instrument industries supplied the skilled craftsmen that built the RG-4's wooden structure. The Pionier filled the bottom rung of the Romanian pilot training pipeline, feeding cadets toward more advanced two-seat gliders and ultimately powered service in the Romanian air force.

This video looks at how Romania rebuilt its aviation industry under Soviet supervision, the role of the Reghin workshops, the design itself, and what happened to the 50 airframes after the type was retired.
