The corrugated aluminum skin everyone associates with the Ju 52 wasn't decoration - it was the structure. Hugo Junkers patented the corrugated metal wing in 1915 because before stressed-skin design existed, corrugation was how you made a thin aluminum sheet structurally stiff. The aircraft is wearing its own ribs on the outside.

Tante Ju flew everything: Lufthansa scheduled service in the 1930s, Condor Legion supply runs in the Spanish Civil War (where Hitler tested the design), Norway 1940, Crete 1941 - the only major airborne invasion of WWII - and the Stalingrad airlift that destroyed the Luftwaffe's transport fleet. A small number of Swiss Air Force Ju 52s flew until 2018. Some restored examples still fly today out of Germany and Switzerland.
