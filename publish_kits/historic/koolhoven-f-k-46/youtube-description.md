The Koolhoven F.K.46 was a 1933 Dutch training biplane designed and built by Frederik Koolhoven's company in Rotterdam. It was one of a long series of two-seat trainers that Koolhoven produced for the civilian flying schools that fed pilots into the Dutch military and KLM.

Frederik Koolhoven himself is one of the most interesting interwar Dutch engineers. He had worked at Armstrong-Whitworth in Britain during WWI — where he designed several aircraft, including the F.K.10 quadruplane that also appears in this list — before returning to the Netherlands. His company produced a string of training and light military aircraft through the 1930s, all carrying the F.K. (Frederik Koolhoven) designation.

In May 1940, the German invasion of the Netherlands lasted four days. The Koolhoven works in Rotterdam was destroyed in the bombing of the city on 14 May. The F.K.46 and most of its siblings disappeared with it.
