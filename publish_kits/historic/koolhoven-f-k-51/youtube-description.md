First flown May 25, 1935, the Koolhoven F.K.51 was a two-seat biplane basic trainer produced by Frederik 'Frits' Koolhoven's firm at Waalhaven, Rotterdam. The Dutch Army Aviation Group (LVA), the MLD naval air service, and several export customers including the Spanish Republican Air Force operated it. After the German invasion of May 1940, surviving F.K.51 examples were impressed into Luftwaffe service as elementary trainers and continued flying through the war. In this video we examine Frederik Koolhoven's English engineering training at the Société Anonyme des Ateliers d'Aviation, his often-bitter rivalry with Anthony Fokker, the F.K.51's role in the Spanish Civil War (used by both sides at different points), the Rotterdam factory's destruction in May 1940, and what 'captured equipment' really meant logistically for the Luftwaffe in the Low Countries. We also clarify the often-confused F.K.51 vs. F.K.52 lineage.
