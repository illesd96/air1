L'Oiseau Blanc — The White Bird — was a French Levasseur PL.8 biplane that disappeared in 1927 during an attempt to make the first non-stop transatlantic flight from Paris to New York City and claim the Orteig Prize. French First World War aviation heroes Charles Nungesser and François Coli took off from Le Bourget on 8 May 1927. They were last seen over Ireland later that morning. Less than two weeks later, Charles Lindbergh flew the route in the opposite direction in the Spirit of St. Louis and won the prize. The fate of Nungesser, Coli, and L'Oiseau Blanc has never been definitively established. The most credible theories place a crash either at sea well short of North America or on land in Newfoundland or Maine, and several physical searches have been undertaken in eastern Canada and the Atlantic since.
