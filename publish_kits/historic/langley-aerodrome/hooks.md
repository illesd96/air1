# Hooks for Langley Aerodrome

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Nine days before the Wright Brothers, the head of the Smithsonian crashed a tax-funded powered airplane into the Potomac River. Twice.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

October 1903 and December 1903. The Langley Aerodrome — a tandem-wing powered flying machine designed by Smithsonian Secretary Samuel Langley with $50,000 of U.S. Army money — is launched from a houseboat catapult on the Potomac. Both attempts end with the aircraft in the water. Nine days after the second crash, the Wright Brothers fly at Kitty Hawk. This is the story of the most expensive aviation failure in pre-Wright history.
