# Hooks for Lippisch Ente

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In 1928, the world's first rocket-powered aircraft was a canard glider where the pilot lit two solid rocket boosters strapped behind his back and just hoped.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Before the Me 163, before the Bell X-1, Alexander Lippisch bolted two Sander solid-fuel rockets to a tailless canard glider, talked Fritz Stamer into climbing aboard, and lit the fuse. The Lippisch Ente flew for 70 seconds and changed aviation forever. Almost no one remembers the duck that started it all.
