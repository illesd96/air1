In 1951, Lockheed Skunk Works flew the X-7 ramjet testbed, a needle-nosed missile launched from a B-50. By 1958, that platform had been militarized as the AQM-60 Kingfisher, a target drone built to validate American air defense missiles. The problem: the Kingfisher hit Mach 4.3 and 100,000+ feet, and the Nike-Hercules and Bomarc batteries couldn't reliably touch it. Rather than admit the air defense network was inadequate, the Department of Defense terminated the program in 1962. In this video we trace Kelly Johnson's lineage from Kingfisher to A-12 to SR-71, examine the ramjet inlet geometry that made it possible, and show how a humble drone exposed a strategic gap in the U.S. continental defense posture. We also bust the myth that it was 'just' an X-7 with paint.
