# Hooks for Lockheed Constellation

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Three tails. Pressurized cabin. And a hangar so small it had to be designed around it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1939 Howard Hughes and TWA went to Lockheed with one demand: an airliner that could cross America non-stop, above the weather, in comfort. Kelly Johnson - the same engineer who would later design the SR-71 - drew the Constellation. Its triple tail wasn't elegance, it was geometry. TWA's hangar wasn't tall enough for a single tail.
