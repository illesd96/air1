The Constellation's iconic triple tail wasn't an artistic choice. TWA's existing hangars in Kansas City couldn't fit a 24-foot single tail - so Kelly Johnson split the vertical into three smaller tails so the aircraft could be put away at night. Engineering driven by real estate.

The Connie defined the glamour era of air travel. Pressurized cabin. Sleeper berths. Four big Wright Duplex radials. It carried Eisenhower's Air Force One, Pan Am's transatlantic luxury service, and TWA's Hollywood crowd. By 1957 it was already being replaced by the 707 - jet age glamour killed propeller glamour in five short years - but the L-1049 Super Constellation remained in cargo service into the 1990s.
