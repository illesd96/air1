The Lommatzsch FES-530 Lehrmeister is one of those aircraft whose history runs straight down the centre of a divided Europe. Built in the German Democratic Republic from 1954 onward, this two-seat trainer glider was produced in two main span variants — 15-metre and 17-metre — and over 300 examples were completed before production wound down.

In the West, German gliding was rebuilt around Schleicher and Schempp-Hirth. In the East, the Lommatzsch works at Saxony quietly produced the Lehrmeister for the DDR's tightly-controlled gliding clubs, which functioned as both sport organisations and pre-military pilot pipelines for the NVA. After reunification in 1990, most surviving Lehrmeisters were scattered across European clubs — and many are still flying.

This video looks at the design lineage, the political context, the surviving examples, and why East German aviation deserves more attention than it usually gets.
