# Hooks for LZ 76

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

On the night of September 23, 1916, Zeppelin LZ 76 was forced down on an English beach. The British captured her almost intact, and used her construction details to design every airship they built for the next decade.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

LZ 76 was an R-class Imperial German Army Zeppelin, designation L 33, that bombed London on September 23, 1916. Anti-aircraft fire crippled her. She came down on the Essex coast, and the Royal Navy got her hands on the most modern Zeppelin ever built. The R33 and R34 British airships were almost direct copies. This is the captured Zeppelin that gave Britain its airship fleet.
