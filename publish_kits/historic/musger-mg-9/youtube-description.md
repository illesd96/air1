The Musger Mg 9 was a 1936 Austrian tandem two-seat sailplane designed by Erwin Musger and built under the auspices of the Österreichischer Aero-Club. It broke both world and national soaring records in 1936-37 — a remarkable achievement for a country of fewer than seven million people.

Erwin Musger himself is one of the great quietly-brilliant Central European designers. His designs ran from the late 1920s through the early postwar period, and several were produced in significant numbers. The Mg 9's record-breaking 1936-37 season made it briefly famous in the European soaring community — and then in March 1938 the Anschluss absorbed Austria into the Third Reich, and Austrian aviation effectively ceased to exist as a separate entity.

This video covers Musger's career, the Mg 9 design, the 1936-37 records, the impact of the Anschluss on Austrian sport aviation, and what happened to the surviving airframes after 1945.
