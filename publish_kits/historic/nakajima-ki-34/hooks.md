# Hooks for Nakajima Ki-34

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Japan's first modern monoplane airliner was actually a licensed copy of the Douglas DC-2 cousin you've never heard of, the Northrop 5A.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When Imperial Japan needed a modern transport, they didn't design one. They studied the Northrop 5A, an obscure American light transport, and Nakajima reverse-engineered it into the Ki-34. Allied pilots called it 'Thora,' but in 1936 it represented Japan's quiet leap from biplanes to all-metal monoplanes. Here's the airliner history forgot.
