# Hooks for Nord Norécrin

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Postwar France needed a cabin monoplane. They built it from scratch. Nobody outside Europe noticed.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In late 1945 France was still bandaged from occupation - and its aviation industry was being rebuilt from nothing. Nord Aviation's first civil design was a two- or three-seat cabin monoplane called the Norécrin. It first flew in December that year. It would never sell outside Europe, would never make Nord rich, and would remain one of the most underrated little touring aircraft of the postwar era.
