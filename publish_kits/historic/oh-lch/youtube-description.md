OH-LCH is a single specific airframe: a Douglas C-53 Skytrooper, c/n 4903, built at Douglas Aircraft Santa Monica in 1942 and originally delivered to the U.S. Army Air Forces as 41-20095. After WWII she was sold to Finnish carrier Kar-Air in 1949 as OH-LCH (callsign 'Hotel'). She served as a passenger and freight DC-3 through the 1960s, was transferred to the Finnish Air Force, and after retirement was acquired by the Airveteran association in 1989. She is the only Douglas DC-3 currently airworthy in Finland, and one of fewer than 150 still flying worldwide. In this video we trace this individual airframe: her wartime Skytrooper service, transfer to Finland, decades of commercial operation, her near-loss in the 1980s, and the volunteer-funded restoration that keeps her flying out of Helsinki-Malmi. We also clarify why OH-LCH is sometimes confused with the unrelated Finnish DC-2 OH-LDA, and we examine the C-53 vs. C-47 vs. DC-3 distinction that matters for serial number research.
