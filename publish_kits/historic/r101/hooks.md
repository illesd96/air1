# Hooks for R101

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Britain bet its airship future on R101 — then flew it into a French hillside on its maiden long-distance voyage.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

5 October 1930. The British rigid airship R101 crashes into a hillside near Beauvais, France, and burns. Forty-eight of fifty-four people on board die, including the Secretary of State for Air. The British rigid airship program ends, effectively, that night. This is the story of an airship project that was rushed politically, designed by committee, and that killed the British airship industry in a single accident.
