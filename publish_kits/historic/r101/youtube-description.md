R101 was one of a pair of British rigid airships completed in 1929 as part of the Imperial Airship Scheme, a government programme to develop civil airships capable of service on long-distance routes within the British Empire. It was designed and built by an Air Ministry-appointed team and was effectively in competition with the privately designed R100. R101 made its maiden flight on 14 October 1929. After modifications including the insertion of an additional bay to increase gas capacity, it departed for India on 4 October 1930 carrying Air Secretary Lord Thomson among its passengers. In the early hours of 5 October it crashed into a hillside near Beauvais, France, and was destroyed by fire. Forty-eight of the fifty-four people on board died. The disaster effectively ended the British rigid airship programme.
