The Raab-Katzenstein RK.9 Grasmücke (Hedge-Sparrow) was a 1928 German two-seat biplane built by the Raab-Katzenstein Flugzeugwerke at Kassel — the same town that produced the Dietrich DP.II. About twenty were built, and they were used for touring, advertising flying, and basic flight training, often switching roles in a single ownership history.

Raab-Katzenstein itself is one of the most interesting forgotten companies of Weimar aviation. Co-founded by Antonius Raab — a former WWI fighter pilot turned aviation entrepreneur — the firm produced a string of distinctive biplane sport designs through the 1920s before collapsing in the depths of the Great Depression around 1930. The Grasmücke was one of its more successful products, though 'successful' here is relative.

This video covers the company, the RK.9 design, the Weimar club-flying scene, and the type's disappearance in the early 1930s.
