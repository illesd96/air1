# Hooks for RWD 10

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Poland built a tiny aerobatic monoplane in 1933 — and it survived the invasion long enough to be flown by both sides.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The RWD team was a group of Polish engineers who took small light aircraft seriously at a time when most national air forces were focused on bombers. The RWD-10 of 1933 was their aerobatic sport monoplane: single-seat, parasol-wing, light, agile. It was used by Polish pilots until 1939 — and at least a few examples ended up flying for the occupiers after Poland fell. Here's what the RWD-10 actually was, and what happened to it in 1939.
