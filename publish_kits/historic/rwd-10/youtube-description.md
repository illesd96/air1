The RWD-10 was a Polish aerobatic sports plane, a single-seat parasol-wing monoplane, used from 1933 to 1939 and constructed by the RWD team — the design partnership of Stanisław Rogalski, Stanisław Wigura, and Jerzy Drzewiecki, working through Doświadczalne Warsztaty Lotnicze (Experimental Aviation Workshops) in Warsaw. About thirty were built. The type was used by Polish flying clubs and the Aeroklub Rzeczypospolitej Polskiej for competition aerobatics. When Poland was invaded in September 1939, a small number of RWD-10s were evacuated to neighboring countries; others were captured by both German and Soviet forces and used in secondary roles.
