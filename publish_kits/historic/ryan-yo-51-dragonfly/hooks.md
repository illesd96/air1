# Hooks for Ryan YO-51 Dragonfly

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Ryan built a STOL observation plane in 1940 that beat its rivals on paper — and lost the contract anyway.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Three prototypes. Three highly successful test flights. And then the United States Army Air Corps picked someone else. The Ryan YO-51 Dragonfly was a parasol-wing monoplane purpose-built for short-takeoff observation work, and by every operational measure it was the more capable airplane. But in 1940 the Stinson YO-49 won the contract anyway. Here's why — and what the Dragonfly almost was.
