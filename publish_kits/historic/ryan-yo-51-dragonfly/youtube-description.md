The Ryan YO-51 Dragonfly was an observation aircraft designed and built by Ryan Aeronautical for the United States Army Air Corps in 1940. A single-engine parasol-wing monoplane, it was optimized for short takeoff and landing — the kind of work that, by 1944, would be done in Europe by the Stinson L-5. Three prototypes were built. By the testing reports we have, all three flew well. The Army nonetheless judged the competing Stinson YO-49 superior, and no production contract followed. The Dragonfly is a textbook study in how contract awards in 1940 American procurement did not always go to the aircraft that flew best in isolation — supply, parts commonality, manufacturer capacity, and price all came into play.
