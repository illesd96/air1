# Hooks for Savoia-Marchetti S.65

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Italy designed a Schneider Trophy racer with two engines pushing and pulling each other — and it killed its pilot.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

January 1930. Lake Garda. An Italian racing seaplane built with two engines in tandem — one pulling, one pushing — crashes into the water and kills its pilot, Tommaso Dal Molin. The Savoia-Marchetti S.65 had been designed for the 1929 Schneider Trophy, where it never raced. The crash came during preparation for 1931. Here's why Italy bet on the strangest engine layout in Schneider history, and why it didn't work.
