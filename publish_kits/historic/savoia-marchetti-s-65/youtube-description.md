The Savoia-Marchetti S.65 was an Italian racing seaplane built for the 1929 Schneider Trophy race. Its defining feature was the engine arrangement: two Isotta-Fraschini Asso 500 engines mounted in tandem on the centerline above the fuselage, one driving a tractor propeller forward and the other a pusher propeller aft, with the single-seat cockpit between them. The configuration was intended to combine extreme power with minimal frontal area. The S.65 did not actually compete in the 1929 contest at Calshot. It was lost in January 1930 on Lake Garda during preparation for the next Schneider race, killing its pilot Tommaso Dal Molin. The cause was an in-flight failure related to the unusual layout. In this video we walk through the S.65's design rationale, what is documented about the Lake Garda accident, why Italian engineers were drawn to tandem-engine layouts in this period, and how this contributed to the decision to invest in the Macchi M.67 and ultimately the MC.72 instead.
