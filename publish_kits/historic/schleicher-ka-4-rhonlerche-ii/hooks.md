# Hooks for Schleicher Ka-4 Rhönlerche II

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

West Germany was banned from building powered aircraft after WWII — so a generation of German pilots learned to fly silently, in gliders like this one.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1953 West Germany was still under Allied aviation restrictions that banned powered flight by Germans. So when Schleicher rolled out the Ka-4 Rhönlerche II, it wasn't just another two-seat trainer glider — it was a workaround. This is how a single high-wing glider became the unofficial restart button for German aviation.
