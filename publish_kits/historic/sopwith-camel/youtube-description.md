The Sopwith Camel killed 1,294 enemy aircraft. It also killed 385 Allied pilots in non-combat accidents - because the rotary engine's gyroscopic precession made hard right turns devastatingly fast and hard left turns nearly impossible.

New pilots died spinning into the ground trying to climb-and-turn left. Pilots who learned to fight the engine - who turned right three times instead of left once - made themselves into killers. Major aces like Billy Bishop and Roy Brown built their careers on the Camel. Snoopy made it a cultural symbol. We look at why this aircraft was both murderously dangerous to learn and statistically the most successful Allied fighter of the war.
