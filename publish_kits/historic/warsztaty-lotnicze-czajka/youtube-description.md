The Warsztaty Szybowcowe Czajka — Lapwing — was a secondary training glider continuously built in Warsaw from 1931 until the German invasion of Poland in 1939. Designed by Wacław Czerwiński and refined under Antoni Kocjan, over 160 were completed. After 1939, almost all were destroyed.

This aircraft sits in the centre of a much larger story. Interwar Poland was punching far above its weight in glider design — Antoni Kocjan, who refined the Czajka, would go on to lead the underground Home Army intelligence cell that obtained crucial information about the German V-1 and V-2 weapons. He was arrested by the Gestapo and shot in 1944.

This video looks at the design itself, the gliding clubs that used it, the connection to the larger story of Polish wartime resistance, and the few examples that survive in museums today.
