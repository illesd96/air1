On August 14, 1901, the Bridgeport Sunday Herald reported that German immigrant Gustave Whitehead had flown his No. 21 monoplane half a mile at 50 feet altitude over Fairfield, Connecticut. The Wright brothers wouldn't fly powered, controlled flight for another 28 months. In this video we examine the available primary evidence: the Sunday Herald article, the famous (and disputed) photograph allegedly showing the aircraft airborne, the affidavits collected in the 1930s, Stella Randolph's 1937 investigation, John Brown's 2013 photo-forensic claim, and the Smithsonian's contractual position with the Wright estate that arguably biased the historical record. We don't tell you who flew first. We show you the evidence and the gaps and let you decide. Connecticut's 2013 'First in Flight' statute is real. The 1901 photograph is still missing.
