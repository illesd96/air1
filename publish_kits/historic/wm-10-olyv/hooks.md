# Hooks for WM-10 Ölyv

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Hungary in 1931 was a small, landlocked, Treaty-of-Trianon-restricted country — and it built its own military trainer biplane anyway.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Treaty of Trianon had stripped Hungary of two-thirds of its territory and most of its industry. But the Manfred Weiss Works in Csepel, on an island in the Danube, kept building things — including the WM-10 Olyv, a small biplane trainer that Hungary's tiny air force quietly used through the 1930s.
