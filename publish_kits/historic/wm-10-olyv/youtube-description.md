The Weiss WM-10 Olyv (Kite) was a 1931 Hungarian biplane trainer designed and built by the Manfred Weiss Works on Csepel Island in Budapest. After 1920, the Treaty of Trianon had limited Hungary's military aviation, but training aircraft existed in a grey zone — and small biplanes like the WM-10 helped Hungary quietly rebuild a pilot training pipeline.

Manfred Weiss is one of the great forgotten European industrial stories. Founded in the 1880s by a Hungarian-Jewish entrepreneur, by the 1930s the Csepel works was one of the largest industrial concerns in central Europe, producing everything from canned food to aircraft. The story did not end well: the Weiss family was forced to surrender the works to the SS in 1944 in exchange for safe passage out of occupied Hungary.

This video covers the WM-10 design, the Manfred Weiss industrial empire, the Trianon context, and the aircraft's quiet service through the 1930s.
