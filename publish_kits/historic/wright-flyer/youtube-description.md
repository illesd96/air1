Twelve seconds. One hundred and twenty feet. And almost nobody believed it happened.

On December 17, 1903, Orville and Wilbur Wright achieved what Samuel Langley, with $50,000 of US government money, could not. Yet for five years their feat was openly doubted by scientists and the press. This is the real story of the world's first powered flight - the wind-tunnel work, the bitter feud with the Smithsonian, and why the original Flyer spent 20 years exiled in a London museum.
