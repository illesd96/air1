The X-15 was supposed to be a stepping stone. It became the only thing that ever did its job properly.

From 1959 to 1968, three X-15s flew 199 missions. Eight of those pilots earned astronaut wings by crossing 50 miles altitude. One was Neil Armstrong. One was Joe Walker, who reached 354,000 feet - higher than any winged aircraft until SpaceShipOne in 2004. And one was Michael Adams, who died on flight 191 when the aircraft broke up at Mach 5.

This is the X-15 story without the Right Stuff polish: ablative coatings, anhydrous ammonia fuel, B-52 drop launches, and why NASA quietly shelved hypersonic winged flight for forty years after Apollo took the budget.
