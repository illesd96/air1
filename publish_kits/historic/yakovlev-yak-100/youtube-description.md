The Yakovlev Yak-100 was a single-engine transport helicopter developed in the Soviet Union in 1948-1949. It was the Yakovlev Design Bureau's second helicopter design, intended to compete for a Red Army utility helicopter contract that ultimately went to the Mil Mi-1. The Yak-100 used a single main rotor and tail rotor configuration broadly similar to the Sikorsky S-51, which was already a known quantity to Soviet engineers from technical intelligence. It flew successfully but offered no decisive performance advantage over Mil's competing prototype, and the OKB's small helicopter staff was simply outmatched in resources by Mil's dedicated bureau.
