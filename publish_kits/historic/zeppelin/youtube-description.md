The Graf Zeppelin's 1929 round-the-world flight is one of the great forgotten feats of the interwar era. Hugo Eckener commanded. Twenty-one days. Friedrichshafen - Tokyo - Los Angeles - Lakehurst - home. Passengers paid the equivalent of $50,000 a ticket in today's money. The crew included reporters from every major newspaper. It made front pages globally.

LZ 127 flew 590 times. It crossed the Atlantic 144 times. It evacuated stranded explorers from the Arctic. And it never killed a single passenger or crew. Eckener was a thoughtful, anti-Nazi commander who was sidelined by Goebbels precisely because of his political views - and the Hindenburg fire happened on his organization's watch in 1937 while he was in disfavor. We tell the story of the airship that worked, why nobody remembers it, and how its replacement burned an entire industry to the ground in 32 seconds.
