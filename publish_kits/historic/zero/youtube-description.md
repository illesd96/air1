In 1941 the Zero rewrote what a carrier fighter could do. By 1943, the same aircraft was a death warrant for the pilot inside.

The A6M traded armor and self-sealing tanks for range and agility - a choice that worked brilliantly when Japan had the best-trained naval pilots in the world. It stopped working when those pilots started dying and couldn't be replaced. Meanwhile, after Akutan Island gave the US a flyable Zero in 1942, the F6F Hellcat was specifically designed to break it.
