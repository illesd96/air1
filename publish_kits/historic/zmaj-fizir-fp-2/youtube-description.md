The Zmaj Fizir FP-2 was a Yugoslav single-engine two-seat biplane designed by Rudolf Fizir and built at the Zmaj factory in Zemun (now part of Belgrade) starting in 1933. The 'Zmaj' (Dragon) factory was one of the most important interwar aircraft manufacturers in the Kingdom of Yugoslavia.

The FP-2 itself was a conventional trainer biplane in the European interwar mould. What is unusual is its longevity in service. The type was used by the Yugoslav Royal Air Force through the 1930s; some examples survived the 1941 Axis invasion and dismemberment of Yugoslavia; a handful continued to be used during the Yugoslav Partisan air force operations later in the war; and the type was finally retired in 1947, by which time it was being flown by the new socialist Yugoslav government.

This video covers Rudolf Fizir, the Zmaj factory, the FP-2 design, and how the aircraft moved through three completely different political regimes without leaving service.
