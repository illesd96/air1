In 1972, the Air Force needed a tank-killer. Fairchild Republic's answer was insane: take the most ferocious anti-armor cannon ever fitted to an aircraft — the seven-barrel GAU-8 Avenger — and design a plane around it. Add a titanium bathtub to keep the pilot alive, redundant systems everywhere, and an airframe that could fly home on one wing. The A-10 Thunderbolt II. The Warthog. The hog the Air Force keeps trying to kill, and Congress keeps refusing to let them.
