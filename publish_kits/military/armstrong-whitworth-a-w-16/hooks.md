# Hooks for Armstrong Whitworth A.W.16

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Britain wanted a new fighter for the early 1930s. They got a biplane that lost a contest to a Bristol.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Armstrong Whitworth A.W.16 was a single-engine biplane fighter designed in Britain in the early 1930s, when monoplanes were still considered risky and biplanes still ruled fighter squadrons. It flew well enough, but it lost the RAF competition to the Bristol Bulldog. Two were eventually sold to China, where they vanished into the chaos of the Sino-Japanese war, leaving the A.W.16 as one of those Armstrong Whitworth designs that did everything right except win.
