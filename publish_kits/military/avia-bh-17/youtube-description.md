The Avia BH-17 is one of those aircraft whose paper performance and operational reality told two very different stories. Designed by Pavel Benes and Miroslav Hajn at Avia in 1924, the BH-17 was a development of the BH-6 and BH-8 prototype fighters, and work on the BH-8 was cut short in favour of this aircraft. Operational trials in 1924 revealed performance good enough for the Czechoslovak Air Force to place an order for twenty four examples. In actual service, however, the BH-17 proved unreliable. Engine cooling problems, structural concerns, and persistent maintenance demands meant that the type was quickly withdrawn from frontline squadrons and relegated to training duties. In this video we cover the design lineage from the BH-6 and BH-8, the structural and engine choices, the trials process and what went well, the service entry and what went wrong, and the relationship between the BH-17 and the much more successful BH-21 that followed. A textbook study in the gap between evaluation and reality.
