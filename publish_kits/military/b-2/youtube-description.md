The Northrop Grumman B-2 Spirit is the only operational flying-wing bomber on Earth, and the most expensive military aircraft program in history measured per airframe. Twenty-one were built. The unit cost, including R&D, sits around two billion dollars apiece. And it nearly never happened.

In this video we trace the B-2 from Jack Northrop's wartime YB-49 — a flying wing decades ahead of its time — to the Cold War stealth requirement that resurrected the configuration, through the painful production cutbacks that turned a planned 132-aircraft fleet into 21, and into the strikes over Serbia, Iraq, and Libya that the B-2 actually flew. We also look at what made the airframe so expensive: graphite-epoxy skin, radar-absorbent coatings that demanded climate-controlled hangars, and a procurement plan that collapsed alongside the Soviet Union it was built to penetrate.
