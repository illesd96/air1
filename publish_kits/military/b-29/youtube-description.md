The B-29 Superfortress was the first true strategic bomber — pressurized so crews could fly above the weather and the flak, computerized so a single gunner could control multiple turrets, and big enough to carry a 9,000-pound special weapon halfway across the Pacific. In this video we cover Boeing's development gamble, the engine fires that nearly killed the program, the firebombing campaign over Japan, and the two missions in August 1945 that ended the war.
