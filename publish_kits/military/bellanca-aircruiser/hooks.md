# Hooks for Bellanca Aircruiser

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

A Depression-era workhorse so tough it ended up hauling cargo for the US military and bush operators alike.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Built in Delaware in the early 1930s, the Bellanca Aircruiser was a high-wing single-engine workhorse designed to haul passengers, freight, mail, anything you could shove through the door. Powered by Wright Cyclones or Pratt Whitney Hornets, it ended up doing military utility work, bush flying in Canada, and freight runs in places that demanded reliability over glamour. It wasn't a fighter, it wasn't fast, but it kept showing up.
