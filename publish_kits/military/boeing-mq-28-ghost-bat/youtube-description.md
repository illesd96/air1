The Boeing MQ-28 Ghost Bat, previously known as the Boeing Airpower Teaming System, is a stealth multi-role unmanned combat aerial vehicle being developed by Boeing Australia for the Royal Australian Air Force. It is designed as a force multiplier built to fly alongside crewed aircraft as part of an integrated network that can share sensors, electronic warfare capabilities, and offensive payloads. First flown on February 27th 2022, the Ghost Bat is Australia's first domestically developed combat aircraft since the Government Aircraft Factories projects of the mid 20th century. This video covers the program's origins, the technical choices that shape its low observability and modular payload bay, how loyal wingman doctrine differs from autonomous strike drones, the RAAF's broader manned-unmanned teaming strategy, and the political significance of Australia building combat aircraft on its own soil again. The Ghost Bat is one of several loyal wingman programs around the world; understanding its specifics tells you a lot about where Western tactical aviation is heading.
