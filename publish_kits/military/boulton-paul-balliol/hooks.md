# Hooks for Boulton Paul Balliol

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

On May 17th, 1948, it became the first single-engined turboprop aircraft in history to fly. It was an advanced trainer almost no one remembers.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In the late 1940s, the RAF needed a trainer that bridged piston basics and the jet age. Boulton Paul drew up the Balliol — and on May 17, 1948, fitted with an Armstrong Siddeley Mamba, became the first single-engined turboprop aircraft in the world to fly. It was a serious technical achievement, immediately overtaken by RAF policy that decided post-war trainers should use piston engines instead. Production switched to a Merlin-engined version. The Balliol served, trained pilots, and then quietly stepped aside as the de Havilland Vampire jet trainer took over.
