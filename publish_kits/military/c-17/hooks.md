# Hooks for Boeing C-17 Globemaster III

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Tanks. Helicopters. 102 paratroopers. The C-17 Globemaster III lands all of it on a 3,500-foot dirt strip. And then it backs up.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The C-17 Globemaster III is the strategic airlifter that thinks it's a tactical one. Four turbofans powerful enough to haul an M1 Abrams from Charleston to Kuwait, but agile enough to land on a 3,500-foot semi-prepared strip, kneel down, and back itself up under its own power. McDonnell Douglas almost killed the program with cost overruns. Boeing turned it into the most successful Western airlifter since the C-130. This is the C-17 story.
