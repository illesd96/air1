The Caproni Ca.135 was supposed to be Italy's modern medium bomber, designed in 1935 in Bergamo by Cesare Pallavicino. It had retractable landing gear, a glazed nose, two radial engines, and a defensive armament arrangement that looked competitive on paper. Italy was unimpressed, ordering only a small batch for the Regia Aeronautica and putting them to limited use. The real story is what happened abroad. Peru took delivery of Ca.135s in 1937, used them in the Ecuadorian-Peruvian War of 1941. Hungary became the largest operator, flying the Ca.135bis on the Eastern Front against the Soviets through 1942. In this video we cover the design history, the engine variants from Isotta Fraschini to Piaggio, why the Italian engines underperformed, the export configurations for Peru and Hungary, the operational record on the Eastern Front, and the surviving fragments in museums today. A textbook case of an aircraft whose foreign service tells a more complete story than its domestic one.
