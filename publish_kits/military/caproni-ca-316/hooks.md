# Hooks for Caproni Ca.316

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Italy needed a small floatplane to scout from battleship catapults. Caproni adapted an airliner family and called it a day.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Caproni Ca.316 was an Italian reconnaissance seaplane produced during the Second World War, intended for catapult operations from Italian Navy capital ships. It was a member of the broad Ca.306 family, derived more directly from the Ca.310 Idro floatplane. Built in small numbers, it served briefly before the collapse of the Italian fleet rendered catapult scouts pointless.
