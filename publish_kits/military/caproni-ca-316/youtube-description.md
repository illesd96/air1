The Caproni Ca.316 was an Italian reconnaissance seaplane produced during the Second World War, designed for catapult launch from Italian Navy capital ships. It was one of the many derivatives of the Ca.306 airliner family, descending more directly from the Ca.310 Idro, a floatplane version of that twin-engine design. The Ca.316 was a relatively small production run, intended to give Italian cruisers and battleships a modern catapult scout to replace the older biplane floatplanes that had served through the 1930s. In practice, by the time the Ca.316 entered service, Italian fleet operations were under sustained pressure from British carrier air power and the broader strategic shift away from catapult scouting. The aircraft saw limited operational use before being overtaken by events.
