The Sikorsky CH-124 Sea King served the Canadian Armed Forces from 1963 to 2018, fifty five years of frontline operations that included some of the most important experiments in shipboard helicopter aviation. Based on the US Navy SH-3 Sea King, most CH-124s were assembled in Quebec by United Aircraft of Canada, and Canadian engineers were the driving force behind the Beartrap helicopter hauldown and rapid securing device, a system that allowed Sea Kings to operate routinely from small frigates and destroyers in heavy weather. This capability changed how navies around the world thought about anti-submarine helicopter operations from small platforms. In this video we cover the procurement story, the Beartrap development with Fairey Aviation, the operational deployments aboard the Iroquois class destroyers and later the Halifax class frigates, the long delays and political controversies around the replacement programme, and the retirement of the type in 2018. A workhorse helicopter that punched far above its weight.
