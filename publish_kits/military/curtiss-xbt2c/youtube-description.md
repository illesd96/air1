The Curtiss XBT2C was a prototype two-seat, single-engined dive and torpedo bomber developed during the final months of the Second World War for the United States Navy. Curtiss derived it from the SB2C Helldiver and tuned it to meet a 1945 Navy specification calling for a single aircraft that could combine the dive bombing, torpedo attack, and reconnaissance roles previously served by separate aircraft types. Unlike its competitors, the XBT2C retained a two-seat configuration, where the Douglas entries went single-seat. The Navy chose the Douglas BT2D, which became the legendary Skyraider. The Curtiss program was cancelled, and so, in slower motion, was Curtiss as a major aircraft manufacturer. The combined Curtiss-Wright company would survive on engine work and components, but Curtiss as a builder of military aircraft effectively ended here.
