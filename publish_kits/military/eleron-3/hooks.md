# Hooks for Eleron-3

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It fits in a soldier's backpack. It launches from a bungee. And Russia has been flying it over front lines for nearly two decades.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When most people picture a modern drone, they picture a missile-armed Reaper. The reality of frontline reconnaissance UAVs is closer to the Eleron-3 — a small hand-launched Russian reconnaissance drone built by ENICS in Kazan, in service since the mid-2000s, and used heavily in recent conflicts. It is bungee-launched, parachute-recovered, weighs less than five kilograms, and provides a few hours of live video and stills back to a control station. This is the quiet category of warfare the modern era actually runs on.
