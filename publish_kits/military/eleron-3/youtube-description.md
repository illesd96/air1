The ENICS Eleron-3 is a Russian small tactical reconnaissance UAV developed by ENIKS in Kazan and in operational service since the mid-2000s. It is hand- or bungee-launched, parachute-recovered, weighs around 5 kg, and transmits real-time imagery and video to a portable ground control station.

In this video we look at the role of small tactical UAVs in modern reconnaissance, how the Eleron-3 fits into the Russian tactical inventory alongside larger drones like the Orlan-10, the design's evolution through variants such as the Eleron-3SV, the system's wartime use in Ukraine and elsewhere, and the broader shift in modern conflicts toward dispersed, cheap, expendable airborne sensors instead of large manned reconnaissance aircraft.
