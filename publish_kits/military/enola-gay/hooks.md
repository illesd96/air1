# Hooks for Enola Gay

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

A single B-29, a single mission, and a name carried through the rest of aviation history.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Enola Gay is a Boeing B-29 Superfortress named after the mother of its pilot, Colonel Paul Tibbets. On August 6th 1945 it carried out the mission that ended the conventional age of warfare and opened the nuclear one. The aircraft itself survived, was nearly scrapped, and is now preserved at the Smithsonian's Udvar-Hazy Center, where its presence still provokes argument decades later.
