# Hooks for Grumman F-14 Tomcat

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Swing wings. Two seats. The Phoenix missile that could kill from 100 miles. The F-14 Tomcat was a Cold War unicorn — and only one country still flies it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Grumman built the F-14 Tomcat to do something nobody else could: defend an aircraft carrier from waves of Soviet bombers a hundred miles out. Variable-sweep wings, a back-seater running the radar, and the longest-range air-to-air missile ever fielded. Hollywood made it a star. The U.S. Navy retired it in 2006. But the Islamic Republic of Iran is still flying its F-14s — bought by the Shah in the 70s — to this day.
