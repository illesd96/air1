The Boeing F/A-18 Hornet — and its Super Hornet successor — is the multirole strike fighter that built the modern US Navy carrier air wing. It was born as the YF-17, a loser in the Air Force's lightweight fighter competition. The Navy resurrected the design, added a second engine for over-water survivability, beefed up the gear for carrier landings, and produced what would become the most-flown American naval combat aircraft of the past 40 years.

In this video we cover the Cobra-to-Hornet conversion, why two engines mattered to a service that flies over open ocean, how the F/A-18 replaced both the A-7 Corsair II and (eventually) the F-14 Tomcat in the strike-fighter role, the Super Hornet redesign of the 1990s, and why the Hornet became the Blue Angels' demonstrator and the on-screen star of Top Gun: Maverick.
