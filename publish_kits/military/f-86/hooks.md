# Hooks for North American F-86 Sabre

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

MiG Alley, 1951. Swept wings, .50-cals, and the first true jet-vs-jet dogfights in history. The F-86 Sabre is where modern fighter combat begins.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When the Korean War turned hot in 1950, the U.S. discovered that the Soviets had given North Korea something terrifying: the swept-wing MiG-15. The American answer was already in production. North American's F-86 Sabre — itself swept-wing, transonic, with six .50-caliber Browning machine guns and a radar gunsight — entered the fight that December. Over the next three years, the F-86 and MiG-15 met in the skies of MiG Alley and invented modern jet air combat.
