# Hooks for F+W C-3605

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Switzerland needed something to tow targets. Their answer looked like a dinosaur with a propeller stuck on its nose.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Swiss Air Force called it the Alpine Anteater because of its absurdly long nose, but the F+W C-3605 had a serious job: drag aerial targets across the Alps so pilots could practice gunnery without shooting each other. It was built by gutting an old C-3603 piston attacker and bolting a turboprop on the front, and it served from 1971 to 1987 hauling sleeves through valley air few aircraft could handle.
