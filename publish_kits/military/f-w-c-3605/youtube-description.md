The F+W C-3605, nicknamed Schlepp and the Alpine Anteater, is one of the strangest looking aircraft ever to wear Swiss Air Force markings. In the late 1960s the Swiss Federal Construction Works needed a target tug to replace their aging C-3603 piston attackers. Instead of designing something new, engineers ripped out the heavy piston engine, slid the cockpit forward, and grafted a Lycoming turboprop onto the front. The result was a fuselage with a comically extended nose to balance the lighter engine, hence the nickname. From 1971 to 1987 these strange-looking machines hauled aerial targets through Alpine valleys so Swiss fighter pilots could practice gunnery. The C-3605 was never glamorous, never famous, and never fired a shot in anger, but it served quietly for sixteen years doing a job nobody else wanted.
