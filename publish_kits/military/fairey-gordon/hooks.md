# Hooks for Fairey Gordon

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It flew the deserts of Iraq, the skies over Sudan, and the Indian frontier. The Fairey Gordon was the RAF's quiet imperial workhorse.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Between the world wars, the RAF was an imperial air force, and most of what it did had nothing to do with European combat. It patrolled deserts, dropped supplies, transported officials, and fought small actions in the territories Britain claimed. For most of the 1930s, the aircraft doing that work was the Fairey Gordon — a development of the Fairey IIIF, sturdy, easily maintained, and posted everywhere from Iraq to Sudan to India. It is the kind of aircraft popular history skips. It is also, arguably, the most accurate single image of what the interwar RAF actually was.
