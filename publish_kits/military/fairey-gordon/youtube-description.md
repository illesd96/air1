The Fairey Gordon was a British light bomber and general-purpose biplane of the 1930s, developed from the Fairey IIIF. It served extensively with the Royal Air Force in Britain and across the Empire — in Iraq, Egypt, Sudan, the Aden Protectorate, and India — through most of the interwar period.

In this video we look at what the RAF actually did between the wars. Air policing, transport, mapping, supply drops, and yes, the lower-altitude small actions of imperial enforcement — none of it glamorous, almost all of it carried out by aircraft like the Fairey Gordon. We unpack the IIIF design lineage, the Gordon's Armstrong Siddeley Panther radial, the type's role at outposts across the Empire, and the way the Gordon became one of the last RAF aircraft to embody an era that the Second World War would sweep away entirely.
