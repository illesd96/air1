# Hooks for Felixstowe Porte Baby

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

First World War Britain built a huge biplane flying boat to hunt zeppelins. It was named Baby. It was anything but.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Felixstowe Porte Baby was a British reconnaissance flying boat of the First World War, first flown in 1915 and designed by John Cyril Porte at the Seaplane Experimental Station at Felixstowe. It was a three-engined biplane with a hull built for North Sea conditions, and it served as a long-range patrol and zeppelin-hunting aircraft from coastal stations. Despite the diminutive nickname, the Baby was one of the larger aircraft of the war.
