The Fiat R.2 has a quiet claim to fame that no other Italian aircraft can match. When Fiat absorbed SIA in 1918, the R.2, a developed SIA 9 with a 300 horsepower Fiat A.12 engine, became the first aircraft ever sold under the Fiat brand. It was a conventional two-bay biplane with equal-span unstaggered wings, fixed tailskid undercarriage, and pilot and observer seated in tandem open cockpits. The Regia Aeronautica took deliveries in the early 1920s and used the R.2 for reconnaissance and light bombing duties, including operations during the Italian colonial campaigns. In this video we trace the lineage from the wartime SIA designs to the R.2, walk through the structural changes, compare it to its contemporaries like the Ansaldo SVA, and show how the lessons learned fed directly into the more famous Fiat fighters of the 1930s. This is not the most exciting aircraft in Italian history, but it is one of the most important to the company that became one of Europe's biggest aerospace players.
