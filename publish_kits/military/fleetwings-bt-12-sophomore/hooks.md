# Hooks for Fleetwings BT-12 Sophomore

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The Army ordered hundreds. They got 24. Then the contract just vanished.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1942 the United States Army Air Forces wanted basic trainers, and Fleetwings offered the BT-12 Sophomore, an all-metal monoplane built on a stainless steel airframe. It was modern, it was strong, and it was completely unnecessary by the time it was ready. Only 24 production aircraft rolled out before the contract was cancelled, leaving the Sophomore as proof that being good is not the same as being needed.
