# Hooks for General Dynamics-Grumman F-111B Aardvark

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The Navy was forced to buy a fighter version of the F-111. It didn't fit on the carrier. It didn't perform. It died loud.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The F-111B was the carrier-based interceptor version of the F-111 program, jointly developed by General Dynamics and Grumman to satisfy a Pentagon-mandated commonality plan that wanted a single airframe for the Air Force and Navy. It was overweight, ill-suited to carrier operations, and was killed by Congress in 1968. What followed was the F-14 Tomcat, which inherited the F-111B's weapons system and very little else.
