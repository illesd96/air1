The General Dynamics Grumman F-111B was the carrier-based interceptor version of the F-111 program, developed under Secretary of Defense Robert McNamara's TFX commonality initiative which insisted a single airframe could serve both the Air Force and Navy. The Air Force tactical strike version eventually became the F-111 Aardvark; the Navy version was the F-111B, and it was a disaster. It was overweight for carrier operations, the wing layout and visibility were marginal for the demanding work of recovery aboard ship, and the Navy made no secret of its hostility to the program. First flown on May 18th 1965, the F-111B was cancelled by Congress in 1968. The Navy's salvage operation produced the Grumman F-14 Tomcat, which kept the AWG-9 radar and the AIM-54 Phoenix missile system from the F-111B and very little else.
