# Hooks for Hansa-Brandenburg C.I

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It was designed by a 23-year-old engineer named Ernst Heinkel. It flew for Austria-Hungary. Both names would shape aviation for the next thirty years.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1916, a young engineer named Ernst Heinkel was working at Hansa-Brandenburg in northern Germany when he was asked to draw up a reconnaissance biplane for Austria-Hungary. The result was the C.I — a sturdy two-seater that became the most important Austro-Hungarian reconnaissance aircraft of the First World War, built under licence in factories across the empire. Heinkel would go on to found his own company and lead some of the most controversial German aircraft programmes of the 1930s. The C.I is where that career started, and where Austro-Hungarian aviation reached its peak.
