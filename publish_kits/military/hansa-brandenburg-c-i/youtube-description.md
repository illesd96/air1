The Hansa-Brandenburg C.I was a German-designed armed reconnaissance biplane built primarily in Austria-Hungary under licence by Phönix, UFAG, and others. With more than 1,200 airframes produced, it was the most numerous Austro-Hungarian reconnaissance two-seater of the First World War — and the design that launched the career of a young engineer named Ernst Heinkel.

In this video we look at how Hansa-Brandenburg connected German industrial capacity to Austro-Hungarian operational need, the C.I's inward-sloping interplane bracing struts that made it visually unmistakable, its long service over the Italian and Balkan fronts, and how its lineage runs through the Brandenburg D.I fighter and onward into the Heinkel designs of the 1930s.
