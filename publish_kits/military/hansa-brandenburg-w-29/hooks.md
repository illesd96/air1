# Hooks for Hansa-Brandenburg W.29

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The German monoplane floatplane fighter that terrified British coastal patrols in 1918.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Most WWI seaplane fighters were lumbering biplanes that could barely keep up with the boats they patrolled. The Hansa-Brandenburg W.29 broke the rule. A low-wing monoplane on twin floats, designed by Ernst Heinkel and operated by the Imperial German Navy from North Sea bases in 1918, it was fast enough to chase down British flying boats and bring them down.
