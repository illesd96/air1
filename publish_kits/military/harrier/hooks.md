# Hooks for Hawker Siddeley Harrier

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It could take off straight up. It could hover. And in 1982, it won an air war from a tossing aircraft carrier in the South Atlantic.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Through the 1960s, every major air force chased the same dream: a fighter that did not need a runway. Britain was the only country that actually built one. The Hawker Siddeley Harrier was an attack jet with a swivelling exhaust nozzle that could take it straight up, hover in place, and even slow itself mid-dogfight. Most of it should not have worked. All of it did. And in 1982 a handful of Sea Harriers flying off two small carriers retook the Falklands against a numerically superior Argentine air force without losing a single Harrier in air combat.
