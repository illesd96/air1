The Ilyushin Il-28 — NATO reporting name 'Beagle' — was the Soviet Union's first mass-produced jet bomber and one of the most-built jet bombers in history. Over 6,000 airframes left Soviet factories between 1950 and 1955. Versions served well into the 21st century in North Korea.

In this video we cover the design brief that demanded a tactical jet bomber buildable in huge numbers, the Klimov VK-1 engines borrowed from the same Rolls-Royce Nene lineage that powered the MiG-15, the glass-nosed bomb aimer station that kept the Il-28 looking like a wartime design even into the jet age, and its export career — from Egypt at Suez and China over Taiwan to North Vietnam and beyond.
