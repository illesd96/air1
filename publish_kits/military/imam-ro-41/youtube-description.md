The IMAM Ro.41 was an Italian biplane fighter that first flew in 1934, designed by Giovanni Galasso at the Industrie Meccaniche Aeronautiche Meridionali in Naples. After short service as a light fighter, the Regia Aeronautica adopted it as its primary advanced fighter trainer through the late 1930s and into World War II.

In this video we trace the Ro.41's career: the 1934 design brief, the export versions sold to Hungary and Spain, the airframe's surprising agility that gave it a brief and respectable fighter career, and the long second life as the standard pilot trainer of Italian fighter pilots in the years leading into 1940. We also look at why Italy struggled to transition out of biplane fighters during the same window when British and German air services moved to monoplane front-line fighters.
