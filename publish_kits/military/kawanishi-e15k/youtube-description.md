The Kawanishi E15K Shiun was a single-engined Japanese reconnaissance floatplane developed for the Imperial Japanese Navy during the Second World War, known to Allied intelligence by the reporting name Norm. It was designed to outrun enemy fighters by sheer speed, an ambitious goal for a floatplane, and Kawanishi reached for it with a contra-rotating propeller, jettisonable floats for emergency speed escape, and the most powerful engine they could source. The result on paper was impressive. The result in practice was a chronic mess of engine reliability issues, propeller gearbox failures, and disappointing top-end performance. Only a small number were built, the type saw limited operational use, and the program is generally classed as one of the more spectacular failures of late-war Japanese naval aviation. This video covers the E15K's design ambitions, why outrunning fighters was an attractive answer to the floatplane survival problem, the specific engineering choices that produced its problems, and where the program fits in the broader collapse of Japanese aviation industrial capacity.
