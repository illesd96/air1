The Kawanishi E5K was the licensed Kawanishi-built version of the Yokosuka E5Y reconnaissance floatplane, produced for the Imperial Japanese Navy in the early 1930s. Imperial Japan frequently split production of single designs between multiple builders to maintain capacity, distribute industrial workload, and reduce political friction between rival manufacturers. The E5K is a textbook example of that practice, but unlike many split-production aircraft it has almost no English-language documentation. Most of what is known comes from Japanese naval records and a handful of postwar reference works. This video covers what we actually know about the E5K, the practice of parallel production in interwar Japanese naval aviation, why Kawanishi was selected as a second source, and the broader context of catapult floatplane operations in the IJN before the Second World War. It is a short story, and a useful one for understanding how Japanese aviation industrial policy actually worked.
