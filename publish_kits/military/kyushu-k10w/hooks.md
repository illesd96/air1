# Hooks for Kyushu K10W

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Japan licensed an American trainer in 1939. By 1944 it was teaching kids how to fly into the Pacific War. Meet the Kyushu K10W.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1939, the Imperial Japanese Navy bought a licence to build the North American NA-16 — the American training aircraft that would become the legendary T-6 Texan. Watanabe (later Kyushu) made significant changes, and the resulting Kyushu K10W entered service as the Type 2 Land-based Intermediate Trainer. Production numbers were modest, but the K10W's job was enormous: take young pilots straight out of basic flight school and prepare them for the front-line types that were running out of veteran crews to fly them. This is the trainer that taught Japan's last generation of Pacific-War pilots.
