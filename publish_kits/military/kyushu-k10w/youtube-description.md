The Kyūshū K10W — Imperial Japanese Navy Type 2 Land-based Intermediate Trainer — was a single-engined, low-wing monoplane trainer that served in the late stages of World War II. The design was based on a licence-built version of the North American NA-16, the same airframe family that became the T-6 Texan in American service.

In this video we look at the strange transpacific lineage of the K10W, the design changes Watanabe (later Kyushu) made to suit Japanese naval pilot training, the airframe's role in the IJN's intermediate-trainer pipeline alongside the K11W Shiragiku, and the broader collapse of Japanese pilot training as the Pacific War dragged on and instructor cadres ran dry.
