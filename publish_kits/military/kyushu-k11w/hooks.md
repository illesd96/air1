# Hooks for Kyushu K11W

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

By 1944 Japan was running out of trained crews faster than aircraft. This plywood trainer was meant to fix it. Then they strapped bombs to it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1942, the Imperial Japanese Navy commissioned a single airframe that could teach a whole bomber crew at once — pilot, bombardier, navigator, gunner, radio operator. The Kyushu K11W Shiragiku was built mostly from wood to spare strategic materials, and 798 left the factory. Then late in the war, as the Allied noose tightened around the home islands, some were stripped, loaded with explosives, and converted into kamikaze aircraft. This is the story of an aircraft built to train crews, ending the war flown into ships.
