The Kyushu K11W Shiragiku ('white chrysanthemum') was a Japanese World War II crew trainer designed to teach pilot, bombardier, navigator, radio operator, and gunner in a single airframe. Constructed largely from wood to conserve strategic aluminum, 798 were built between 1942 and 1945, making it one of the more numerous late-war Japanese types.

In this video we look at why the Imperial Japanese Navy needed an all-in-one crew trainer by 1942, how strategic material shortages drove the move to wood, the cockpit and observer arrangements that crammed five training roles into one airframe, and the grim late-war fate of some K11Ws that were converted into kamikaze aircraft as the home islands came under direct threat.
