# Hooks for L 68 Alauda

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Germany wasn't supposed to be building military aircraft in 1925. Albatros built one anyway and called it a trainer.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Albatros L 68 Alauda was a two-seat German biplane trainer of the mid 1920s, designed for tandem instruction with the pilot and instructor in open cockpits. It came from a manufacturer best known for its First World War fighters, working in a Germany still bound by the Treaty of Versailles. Officially civilian, practically a stepping stone, the L 68 is one of those quiet pieces of evidence that German military aviation never really stopped.
