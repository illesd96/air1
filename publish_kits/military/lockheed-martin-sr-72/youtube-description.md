The Lockheed Martin SR-72 has been a topic of public discussion since 2013, when Lockheed Martin's Skunk Works division publicly proposed a hypersonic intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance aircraft as a successor to the retired SR-71 Blackbird. The concept describes a Mach 6 capable platform powered by a turbine-based combined cycle propulsion system that transitions from conventional turbine to ramjet to scramjet operation across its flight envelope. As of 2025 no confirmed flight tests have occurred, and the SR-72 remains a design concept with speculation persisting about whether a flight demonstrator exists in classified form. In this video we focus on what is publicly verifiable, the propulsion system challenges, the historical context within the broader hypersonic ISR push that includes Boeing Valkyrie II and various DARPA programs, the policy and budget signals around the U.S. Air Force's interest, and the open questions that anyone watching the program should be careful about answering definitively. Truth over hype, even when the subject is genuinely fascinating.
