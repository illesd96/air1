# Hooks for LVG C.V

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Germany built more of these than Fokker Triplanes, Albatros fighters, and Pfalz scouts combined. Almost no one knows the name.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When the Western Front turned into a grinding artillery war, the aircraft that mattered most were not the scouts — they were the two-seaters that crawled overhead spotting fall of shot, photographing trenches, and bringing back the maps. The LVG C.V was one of the workhorses of that war: a sturdy German reconnaissance biplane built in huge numbers, flown across every front, and almost completely overshadowed in popular memory by the gaudier fighters of the era. This is the aircraft that did the unglamorous work that decided the artillery duels of 1918.
