# Hooks for LVG C.VI

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Britain captured one in 1918 and put it in a museum. A century later it still flies. The LVG C.VI is the last airworthy German WWI two-seater.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

When the British rolled forward in the autumn of 1918 they captured a near-pristine LVG C.VI reconnaissance biplane — a refined, slightly smaller, easier-to-build successor to the C.V. That airframe ended up at the Imperial War Museum, was restored, and as of recent years has been one of the only flyable Imperial German Air Service aircraft on Earth. The C.VI was never meant to be a star. It was meant to be a steady artillery spotter. Today it is one of the most important surviving witnesses to the actual war the German air service fought.
