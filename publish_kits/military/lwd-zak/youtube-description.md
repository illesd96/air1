The LWD Żak was a Polish touring and trainer aircraft of the late 1940s, designed and built by Lotnicze Warsztaty Doświadczalne, the Experimental Aviation Workshops, during the immediate postwar reconstruction period. It was produced in a short series, and although the numbers were small, the Żak mattered because it was one of the first independent Polish aircraft designs to enter production after the country's near-total wartime devastation. Poland's aviation industry was effectively destroyed between 1939 and 1945; the LWD organisation was one of the first serious attempts to put it back together. The Żak was simple, robust, and designed to be built with the resources actually available, which in 1940s Poland meant limited tooling, scavenged materials, and engineers reassembling careers as much as airframes. This video covers the political and industrial backdrop to postwar Polish aviation, the LWD organisation itself, the Żak's design and role as a touring and trainer aircraft, and how the program fit into the longer rebuilding arc that eventually produced the PZL TS-8 Bies and the TS-11 Iskra.
