# Hooks for Macchi M.5

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It took off from water. It dogfought over the Adriatic. In 1917 it was the best flying boat fighter in the world.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Italy fought a different First World War from the rest of Europe — long stretches of mountain and a long, narrow sea where the Austro-Hungarian fleet posed a constant threat. The Italians answered with a unique kind of aircraft: a single-seat flying-boat fighter, agile enough to dogfight land-based opponents, but with a hull that let it operate from any sheltered cove on the Adriatic. The Macchi M.5 was the best of that lineage. It is the reason a small Italian firm called Aermacchi would, eventually, become one of Europe's most important builders of military aircraft.
