The Macchi M.5 was an Italian single-seat fighter flying boat, designed and built by Nieuport-Macchi (the company that would become Aermacchi) at Varese during the First World War. It served from 1917 with the Italian and US Navy units operating in the Adriatic, and it stayed in service into the mid-1920s.

In this video we cover the unusual operating environment of the Adriatic Front, where seaborne aircraft could outflank land battles and patrol against Austro-Hungarian naval forces, the Macchi M.5's surprising agility against land-based fighters, the Nieuport-Macchi design lineage out of the captured Lohner L flying boats, and how this little flying-boat fighter became the foundation stone of one of Italy's longest-lived aviation companies.
