# Hooks for Messerschmitt Me 262

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The first operational jet fighter in history. A hundred miles per hour faster than anything the Allies had. And Germany built it too late.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In April 1944, a Messerschmitt Me 262 took off from a German airfield and changed military aviation forever. It was the world's first operational jet fighter — swept wings, twin Junkers Jumo turbojets, 540 miles per hour at altitude. The Allied propeller fighters chasing it couldn't catch it. But Germany was losing the war on the ground, running out of fuel, running out of pilots, and Hitler kept demanding it be used as a bomber. This is the story of the jet that arrived too late.
