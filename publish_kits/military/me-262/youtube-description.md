The Messerschmitt Me 262 was the world's first operational jet fighter — a generation beyond every piston-engined fighter on either side. Swept wings (almost by accident), Junkers Jumo 004 turbojets, and a speed advantage that should have rewritten the air war. In this video we cover the development saga, the Jumo engine's 25-hour lifespan, Hitler's catastrophic decision to demand a bomber variant, and the dogfights between Me 262s and escorting Allied fighters in 1944 and 1945.
