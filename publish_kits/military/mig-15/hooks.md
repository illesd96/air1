# Hooks for Mikoyan-Gurevich MiG-15

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

In November 1950, an American F-80 pilot saw a swept-wing jet climb past him like he was standing still. The West had just met the MiG-15.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1947, Stalin asked Britain for jet engines. Britain said yes. Within three years, those Rolls-Royce Nenes were powering a swept-wing Soviet fighter that could out-climb, out-turn, and out-ceiling every Western jet in Korea. The MiG-15 was the aircraft that proved the Soviet Union was not catching up — it had caught up. This is the fighter that forced the United States to build the F-86 Sabre, and the jet that No Kum-sok defected with so the Americans could finally figure out how it worked.
