The Mikoyan-Gurevich MiG-15 is the fighter that ended Western complacency about Soviet aerospace. Built around a reverse-engineered Rolls-Royce Nene, given swept wings inspired by captured German research, and produced in numbers no Western program could match, the MiG-15 shocked UN pilots over Korea in 1950 and forced the entire Western jet industry to start over.

In this video we cover the engine gift that should never have happened, how the design team turned a borrowed turbojet into a transonic fighter in under three years, the brutal combat record over MiG Alley, and the day Lieutenant No Kum-sok flew a MiG-15 into American hands so engineers could finally take one apart.
