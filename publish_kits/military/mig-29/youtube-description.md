The Mikoyan MiG-29 Fulcrum was the Soviet Union's light-fighter counter to the F-16 — and in many ways, it was ahead. The R-73 Archer infrared missile combined with the helmet-mounted sight gave Soviet pilots a within-visual-range capability NATO didn't field until the AIM-9X 20 years later. In this video we cover the Soviet TPFI program, the Su-27/MiG-29 split, the East German Fulcrums that gave the Luftwaffe its post-reunification education, and the modern Russian variants still flying.
