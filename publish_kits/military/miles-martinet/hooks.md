# Hooks for Miles Martinet

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Every gunner in the RAF needed something to shoot at. This is the unglamorous plane that towed the targets — and saved thousands of lives.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Miles Martinet does not appear in any famous war film. It shot down nothing. It carried no bombs. It was a target tug — built specifically to drag a sleeve through the sky so air gunners and anti-aircraft crews could practise on something that moved. Britain built nearly 1,800 of them. They flew over 20,000 hours per week at the height of the war. This is the story of the aircraft that did the most boring job in the RAF, and the reason fresh aircrew survived their first real combat sortie.
