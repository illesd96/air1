The Miles M.25 Martinet was the first British aircraft designed from the outset as a target tug — a flying job that does not get monuments, but without which Bomber Command rear gunners and Royal Navy anti-aircraft crews would have entered combat with no live practice at all.

In this video we look at the workhorse side of wartime aviation: how Britain converted Miles Master trainers into Martinets, why a dedicated target-towing design beat the makeshifts that came before, the staggering training tempo of the RAF and Fleet Air Arm in 1942-44, and the sleeve, banner, and winch systems that turned the Martinet into a flying gunnery range. We also cover the Queen Martinet — a remarkable radio-controlled drone derivative that turned the same airframe into the actual target.
