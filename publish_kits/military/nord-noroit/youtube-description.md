The Nord 1400 Noroit was France's attempt to give the Aeronavale a modern reconnaissance and air-sea rescue flying boat after the Second World War. First flown on January 6th 1949, it carried a five-man crew, had a clean twin-engine layout, and was perfectly competent. The problem was timing. By the late 1940s the entire concept of the patrol flying boat was sliding toward obsolescence as long-range landplanes and helicopters took over both maritime patrol and rescue roles. The French Navy ordered only a tiny production run before walking away, and the Noroit slipped into the same quiet grave as dozens of late-era flying boats around the world. This video covers the Noroit's design, why Nord Aviation thought the program had legs, what the French Navy actually wanted instead, and how the broader collapse of the flying boat era killed the project. The Noroit is the kind of aircraft that gets one paragraph in encyclopedias and nothing on YouTube, which is exactly why it's worth twenty minutes of your time.
