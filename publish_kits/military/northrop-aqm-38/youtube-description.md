The Northrop AQM-38 was an American target drone, developed in the 1950s by the Radioplane Division of the Northrop Corporation in Newbury Park, California, and built at Northrop's Ventura Division facility in Van Nuys. Over two thousand were produced. They were used extensively to train US Army and US Navy surface-to-air missile crews — and in the process, most were destroyed.

In this video we look at why expendable target drones became central to Cold War missile training, the AQM-38's design and the variants (MQM-38 and others) that supported different mission profiles, how Radioplane fit into the larger story of early US uncrewed aviation, and how the lessons learned from target drone programmes fed directly into the modern UAV industry.
