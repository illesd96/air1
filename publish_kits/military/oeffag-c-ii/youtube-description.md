The Oeffag C.II is a brief footnote in the history of Austro-Hungarian aviation, but it is a footnote worth reading. Oeffag, the Oesterreichische Flugzeugfabrik AG, was famous primarily for its license production of the Albatros D.III fighter, which by some accounts surpassed the original German aircraft in quality. The company also designed its own aircraft, and the C.II was a two-seat reconnaissance biplane produced in small numbers during World War I for the K.u.K. Luftfahrtruppen. Photographs and surviving documentation are limited, but what we do have tells us about the engine choices, the structural details, and the role the C.II filled alongside more numerous Brandenburg and Lloyd designs. In this video we gather what is known from contemporary Austrian sources, examine the relationship between Oeffag's license work and its own designs, and place the C.II in the broader picture of Austro-Hungarian aviation in 1917 and 1918.
