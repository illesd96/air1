# Hooks for Lockheed P-38 Lightning

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Two engines. Twin booms. The fighter that shot down Admiral Yamamoto. The P-38 Lightning is the strangest fighter that ever worked.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Lockheed's P-38 Lightning looks like nothing else in the Second World War: a central pod between two long engine booms, twin turbocharged Allison V-12s, a 20mm cannon and four .50-cals in the nose. The Luftwaffe called it the 'Fork-Tailed Devil.' In April 1943, sixteen P-38s flew the longest fighter-intercept mission of the war and killed Admiral Isoroku Yamamoto in mid-air over Bougainville. This is the Pacific theater's strangest, longest-legged hunter.
