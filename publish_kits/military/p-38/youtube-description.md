The Lockheed P-38 Lightning is the strangest-looking American fighter of World War II — and one of the most successful. Twin Allison V-12s, turbochargers, a central pod with the cockpit and guns, and twin booms running back to the tail. In this video we cover Kelly Johnson's early design career, why the twin-boom layout solved the turbocharger ducting problem, the Yamamoto interception mission of April 18, 1943, and the top American aces (Richard Bong, Thomas McGuire) who scored their kills in the Lightning.
