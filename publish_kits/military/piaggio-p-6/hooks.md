# Hooks for Piaggio P.6

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Italy needed a floatplane to launch from a battleship catapult. Piaggio's answer never quite caught on.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Piaggio P.6 was an Italian catapult-launched reconnaissance floatplane designed for the Regia Marina in the late 1920s. It was meant to ride on the catapults of Italian capital ships, scouting ahead of the fleet before recovering on calm water. Only a handful were built, and the Italian Navy quickly moved on to other designs, leaving the P.6 as one of the quieter steps in Piaggio's long path from floatplane builder to wartime engine and airframe contractor.
