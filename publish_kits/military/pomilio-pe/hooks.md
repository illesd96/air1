# Hooks for Pomilio PE

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Italy built 1,600 of these reconnaissance biplanes in World War One. Almost no one remembers them. This is the Pomilio PE.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

By 1917 Italy was bleeding men and aircraft on the Isonzo front, and the Pomilio brothers were given a brief: design an armed reconnaissance biplane the Italian air service could actually mass-produce. The Pomilio PE was the result — a steady, two-seat biplane built around an indigenous Fiat engine that flew artillery-spotting and observation missions while Caproni bombers stole the headlines. Over 1,600 PE-series aircraft left Italian factories. Today, almost none survive in any meaningful form. This is the workhorse Italy built when it could not afford to fail.
