The Pomilio PE was a two-seat armed reconnaissance biplane built in Turin during the First World War. Developed by the Pomilio brothers from their earlier PC and PD designs, it became one of the most-produced Italian aircraft of the war, with more than 1,600 airframes delivered.

In this video we look at Italy's often-overlooked WWI air service, the brutal mountain warfare on the Isonzo front that put a premium on artillery observation, why the PE's solid, conventional layout suited Italian industry better than more advanced types, and how the Pomilio brothers' design lineage shaped Italian aviation through to the foundation of the Regia Aeronautica.
