The Potez 540 was a French multi-role aircraft of the 1930s, designed as a long-range reconnaissance bomber for the Armée de l'Air. It first flew in November 1933, was ordered in large numbers, and quickly became one of the most visible aircraft in French service. It was also, almost from the moment of entering service, obsolete.

In this video we look at the design context that produced the Potez 540 (a French doctrine that prioritised long-range observation over modern bomber performance), the Spanish Republican Air Force's use of the type during the Spanish Civil War, the long colonial career of the 540 in French overseas territories, and the painful 1940 record when surviving examples were still in combat squadrons during the Battle of France.
