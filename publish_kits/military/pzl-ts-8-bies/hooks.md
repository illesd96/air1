# Hooks for PZL TS-8 Bies

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

After 1945, Poland could not buy Western jets. So it built its own trainer from scratch. Meet the TS-8 Bies — the Devil.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In the early 1950s, Poland was rebuilding from the war, locked behind the Iron Curtain, and starting to train a generation of pilots for Soviet-pattern jets. It needed a trainer it could build itself. Tadeusz Sołtyk's team at PZL drew up the TS-8 Bies — 'Devil' — an all-metal piston tandem trainer that first flew in 1955. It went on to win the world aerobatic championships, equip the Polish Air Force, and prove that Eastern Bloc industry could produce a first-rate trainer without Soviet help. This is the Polish aircraft that quietly mattered.
