The PZL TS-8 Bies ('Devil') was Poland's first postwar indigenous trainer aircraft. Designed by Tadeusz Sołtyk's team at PZL and first flown in July 1955, the TS-8 became the Polish Air Force's standard basic trainer from 1957 into the 1970s, with around 250 airframes built.

In this video we look at the difficult position Polish aviation found itself in after 1945 — cut off from Western suppliers, rebuilding industry under Soviet political constraint, but with a strong pre-war aviation heritage to draw on. We cover Sołtyk's role, the airframe's performance and aerobatic capability (a TS-8 won the 1957 World Aerobatic Championships), and the type's broader legacy in restoring Polish design confidence that would eventually produce the TS-11 Iskra jet trainer.
