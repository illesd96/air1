The Renard R.31 carries a remarkable distinction. It was the only World War II operational military aircraft entirely designed and built in Belgium. Designed by Alfred Renard and first flown on 16 October 1932, the R.31 was a single-engined parasol monoplane intended for army cooperation, reconnaissance, and light bombing duties. Thirty two examples were built for the Aviation Militaire Belge, and the survivors, although obsolete by 1940, were still in frontline service when Nazi Germany invaded Belgium in May of that year. They flew brave but largely futile sorties against overwhelming Luftwaffe opposition, and most were destroyed within days. In this video we cover the design genesis, the structural and engine choices, the small Belgian aviation industry that produced the R.31, the operational record during the brief campaign of May 1940, and the place the type holds in Belgian national memory. A small aircraft with one of the more poignant distinctions in interwar aviation.
