The Valmet L-90 Redigo is a small piece of national aviation history with a quietly significant distinction. First flown on 12 February 1985, the Redigo is a turboprop-powered military basic trainer and liaison aircraft developed by Valmet as a successor to its own L-70 Vinka piston-engined trainer. It carries a 500 horsepower Allison 250 turboprop, accommodates instructor and student in side-by-side seating, and was designed to be used for both initial pilot training and light liaison work. The Finnish Air Force ordered ten Redigos, and a small number were exported to Mexico and Eritrea. After Valmet's aviation division became part of Patria, no further Finnish military aircraft of indigenous design followed, making the Redigo the last military aircraft designed and produced in Finland. In this video we cover the design history, the comparison with the Vinka, the operational use as both trainer and liaison aircraft, the export campaign and its limited results, and what the end of Finnish military aircraft design says about small-country aviation industries.
