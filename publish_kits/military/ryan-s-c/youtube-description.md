The Ryan S-C, or Sports-Coupe, was a three-seat cabin monoplane designed and built by the Ryan Aeronautical Company in the late 1930s. It was a civilian sport tourer with an enclosed cabin, a strut-braced wing, and a small radial engine in the nose. At least one Ryan S-C was impressed into United States Army Air Forces service during the war as the L-10 and used for light utility and liaison duty. Ryan is far better known today for the NYP Spirit of Saint Louis, the X-13 Vertijet, and the Firebee target drone family, but the late 1930s were a period when the company built small, well-finished civil aircraft for the private market. The S-C is a useful reminder that those programs depended on civilian sales to survive.
