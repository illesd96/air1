# Hooks for Saro Cloud

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The RAF needed to train pilots to fly flying boats. The Saro Cloud was the answer almost nobody remembers.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Saro Cloud started life as the A.19, a small civil passenger amphibian designed by Saunders-Roe on the Isle of Wight. The RAF picked it up as the A.29, redesignated it as a trainer, and used it to teach pilots and navigators how to handle flying boats before they moved on to bigger types. It was unglamorous, slow, and absolutely essential to the pipeline that produced Coastal Command's wartime crews.
