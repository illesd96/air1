The Short Empire flying boat was the aircraft Imperial Airways used to turn the British Empire into a flyable air network. Designed and developed by Short Brothers at Rochester to a 1934 Air Ministry specification, the S.23 Empire class first flew on 3 July 1936, with the prototype Canopus entering service shortly after. Four 920 horsepower Bristol Pegasus engines, accommodation for twenty four passengers in comfort or up to a tonne and a half of mail, and a graceful planing hull made it the gold standard for long-haul flying boats of the late 1930s. The Empire fleet flew Imperial Airways routes from Southampton to South Africa, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Sydney. When war broke out in 1939, many were impressed into RAF service, and the design also fed directly into the Short Sunderland maritime patrol bomber that served throughout the war.
