# Hooks for Supermarine Spitfire

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

The fighter that won the Battle of Britain. The elliptical wing that became a national symbol. The Spitfire isn't just a plane — it's a country.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In the summer of 1940, the Luftwaffe outnumbered the Royal Air Force three to one. Britain had two single-engine fighters worth flying: the Hurricane, and a sleek elliptical-winged interceptor designed by a dying engineer named R.J. Mitchell. The Spitfire was harder to build, slower to produce, and infinitely more beautiful — and it became the symbol of British defiance. By war's end, it had been built in 24 marks across nine years. This is its story.
