The Beechcraft T-6 Texan II is a single-engine turboprop trainer built by what is now Textron Aviation, designed as a license-built and substantially modified derivative of the Pilatus PC-9. It is the primary trainer for the United States Air Force, the United States Navy, and a long list of allied air forces, replacing the Cessna T-37 Tweet jet trainer and the Beechcraft T-34 Mentor turboprop trainer during the 2000s and 2010s under the Joint Primary Aircraft Training System program. The Texan II is heavily instrumented, fully aerobatic, and pressurized, with ejection seats and a glass cockpit, allowing student pilots to transition smoothly toward advanced jet trainers.
