# Hooks for Tachikawa Ki-74

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Imperial Japan wanted to fly across the Pacific to photograph American cities. The Ki-74 was the prototype that almost did it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Tachikawa Ki-74 was a long-range, high-altitude reconnaissance bomber developed for the Imperial Japanese Army Air Service late in the Second World War. Twin-engined, pressurized, and designed for missions that could potentially reach the continental United States, it never saw combat. Only a small number flew before the war ended, leaving the Ki-74 as one of the great what-ifs of late-war Japanese aviation.
