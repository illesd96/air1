# Hooks for TAI Aksungur

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Turkey already had the Bayraktar. Then it built something bigger, longer-legged, and almost nobody talks about it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The TAI Aksungur is Turkish Aerospace Industries' largest drone, a twin-engine unmanned aerial vehicle built on the bones of the Anka family. It first flew in 2019, carries a heavier payload than its more famous siblings, and is intended for long-endurance intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance missions for the Turkish armed forces. Yet it lives in the shadow of the Bayraktar TB2, which absorbs almost all the international attention paid to Turkish drones.
