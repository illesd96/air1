The TAI Aksungur is a high-altitude long-endurance unmanned combat aerial vehicle built by Turkish Aerospace Industries for the Turkish armed forces. It first flew on March 20th 2019 and represents the largest drone in TAI's catalogue, built around technology developed for the earlier Anka series but scaled up with twin engines and a substantially larger payload. The Aksungur is intended for long-duration intelligence, surveillance, target acquisition, and reconnaissance work as well as armed strike missions when fitted with Turkish-developed munitions. While the Bayraktar TB2 has dominated international coverage of Turkish drone capability, the Aksungur quietly occupies the larger and longer-ranged end of the spectrum. This video covers the Aksungur's design lineage from the Anka family, the engineering choices that gave it twin-engine reliability and a heavy payload, how it fits into Turkey's broader unmanned strategy, and why it has remained relatively obscure compared to the Bayraktar. The drone landscape is shifting fast, and the Aksungur is one of the platforms shaping where Turkish aerospace goes next.
