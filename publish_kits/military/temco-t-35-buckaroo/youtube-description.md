The Temco T-35 Buckaroo started life as the GC-1B Swift, a small civilian aircraft that Globe Aircraft had built in modest numbers immediately after World War II. Temco, the Texas Engineering and Manufacturing Company, acquired the rights and redesigned the aircraft as a tandem two-seat military trainer with a Continental O-470 engine and modified airframe. First flown in 1948, the T-35 Buckaroo competed for the US Air Force basic trainer contract but lost to the Beech T-34 Mentor. With no home market customer, Temco turned to exports. Small batches went to Saudi Arabia, where the Royal Saudi Air Force operated three Buckaroos in the early 1950s, and a few other operators took token quantities. In this video we cover the design genesis from the Globe Swift, the comparison with the T-34 Mentor, the USAF evaluation process, the export campaigns and their modest results, and what happened to Temco's aviation business after the Buckaroo. A clear lesson in the importance of the home market for trainer aircraft programs.
