# Hooks for Lockheed U-2

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Seventy thousand feet. Glider wings on a spy camera. The U-2 Dragon Lady is harder to land than to fly — and it's still flying today.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

In 1955, the CIA needed to photograph the Soviet Union from heights no fighter could reach and no missile could touch. Kelly Johnson's Skunk Works delivered the U-2 in eight months. Then Gary Powers got shot down over Sverdlovsk in 1960. Then a U-2 found Soviet missiles in Cuba in 1962. Then, somehow, it kept flying — past the SR-71, past every drone, past every replacement program. This is how a glider with a jet engine outlived the Cold War.
