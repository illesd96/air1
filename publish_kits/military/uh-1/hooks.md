# Hooks for Bell UH-1 Iroquois

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Wop-wop-wop. The two-bladed rotor of the UH-1 Huey is the sound of an entire war — and it changed how the U.S. Army fights forever.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Bell's UH-1 Iroquois — the Huey — was the first turbine-powered helicopter in U.S. military service. In Vietnam, it became airborne cavalry: 7,000 deployed, troops in, casualties out, gunship escort overhead. The signature two-blade rotor wash became the defining sound of the war, immortalized in Apocalypse Now. Decades later, the Huey is still flying in dozens of air forces and Marine Corps service. This is how one helicopter rewrote the doctrine of land warfare.
