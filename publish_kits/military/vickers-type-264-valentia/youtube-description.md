The Vickers Valentia is the aircraft you build when you are running an empire from biplanes and the next monoplane transport is still years away. Derived from the Vickers Victoria, which itself came from the Virginia bomber, the Type 264 Valentia was a converted variant with more powerful Bristol Pegasus engines and a strengthened structure that allowed it to carry twenty two troops or freight loads across the Middle East, India, and Africa. First flown in 1934, the Valentia served with RAF transport squadrons through the Second World War, with the last examples operating in the Western Desert and East African theatres into 1944. In this video we trace the lineage from the Virginia through the Victoria to the Valentia, walk through the structural and engine upgrades, look at the operational role in Empire policing duties on the North West Frontier and during the Anglo-Iraqi War of 1941, and discuss why a 1934 biplane stayed in frontline transport service longer than anyone expected.
