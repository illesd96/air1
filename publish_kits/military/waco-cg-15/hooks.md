# Hooks for Waco CG-15

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

It had no engine. It was towed into combat behind a C-47. And in 1945, a thousand of these gliders were ordered to land troops behind the lines.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

By 1944, the United States Army had a problem with its standard combat glider, the Waco CG-4: it was slow, ungainly, and getting torn apart in operational tows. Waco's answer was the CG-15 — shorter wings, a more streamlined nose, the same payload, more speed under tow. A thousand were ordered. Four hundred and seventy-three were delivered before V-J Day stopped the production line. They never went into a major combat assault. But they are a window into one of the wildest assault concepts of the entire war: silent, engineless gliders crash-landing soldiers behind enemy lines.
