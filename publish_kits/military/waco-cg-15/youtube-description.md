The Waco CG-15 was an American assault glider — engineless, towed by a powered tug like the C-47 Skytrain, and meant to carry troops, light vehicles, and supplies straight into action behind enemy lines. It was developed as a faster, cleaner replacement for the much-better-known CG-4 Hadrian.

In this video we look at how the WWII Allied combat glider concept actually worked, the lessons learned from Sicily, Normandy, and Operation Market Garden that drove the CG-15 redesign, the production cutbacks after Germany's surrender, and the planned use of large glider fleets in Operation Coronet — the never-executed Allied invasion of Honshu, Japan.
