# Hooks for Yokosuka E1Y

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Japan put it on every cruiser in the 1920s. It catapulted off, scouted, then landed in the sea next to the ship. This is the Yokosuka E1Y.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Before radar, Japanese cruisers had floatplanes — small reconnaissance biplanes catapulted off the deck, sent over the horizon to find the enemy fleet, then recovered by crane from the open ocean. The Yokosuka E1Y was the workhorse of that era. Three hundred and twenty were built. They served from 1925 to 1932 across the Imperial Japanese Navy, and they did the unglamorous job that wrote the doctrine the IJN would carry into the Pacific War. This is the floatplane that helped Japan learn to scout at sea.
