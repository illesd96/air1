The Yokosuka E1Y — designated Type 14 Reconnaissance Seaplane — was a single-engined biplane floatplane developed by the Yokosuka Naval Air Technical Arsenal for the Imperial Japanese Navy in the early 1920s. Around 320 airframes were built, and they served as the IJN's standard catapult reconnaissance floatplane from 1925 until being phased out around 1932.

In this video we explain how catapult floatplane reconnaissance actually worked aboard a 1920s cruiser, why pre-radar fleets relied on these slow biplanes as their over-the-horizon eyes, the E1Y's development at Yokosuka, and the gradual evolution from the E1Y through later IJN scout floatplanes like the E7K and E13A that would carry the same doctrine into the Pacific War.
