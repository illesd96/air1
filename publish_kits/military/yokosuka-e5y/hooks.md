# Hooks for Yokosuka E5Y

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Imperial Japan needed a reconnaissance floatplane for its growing fleet. Yokosuka delivered. Almost nobody outside Japan ever heard of it.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Yokosuka E5Y was a single-engine reconnaissance floatplane built for the Imperial Japanese Navy in the late 1920s. It was a workmanlike biplane with twin floats, designed for shipboard catapult operations, and it was also produced by Kawanishi under the designation E5K. It served quietly through the early 1930s, was replaced by faster designs before the war began, and left almost no footprint in English-language aviation history.
