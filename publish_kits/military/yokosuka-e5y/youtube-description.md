The Yokosuka E5Y was a single-engine reconnaissance floatplane designed for the Imperial Japanese Navy in the late 1920s. It first flew in 1928, was a conventional twin-float biplane, and was produced not only by Yokosuka but also by Kawanishi under the parallel designation E5K. The aircraft was intended for shipboard catapult operations, where it would launch from cruisers and battleships to scout ahead of the fleet before recovering on calm water for crane recovery. The E5Y served through the early 1930s and was eventually replaced by faster monoplane designs as Japanese carrier and surface forces modernized. This video covers the E5Y's design, its dual production at Yokosuka and Kawanishi, the broader role of catapult floatplanes in interwar Japanese naval doctrine, and why this particular type has almost no presence in English-language aviation literature. Floatplane reconnaissance was a core part of how the IJN expected to fight a fleet engagement, and the E5Y is one of the early steps in that doctrine.
