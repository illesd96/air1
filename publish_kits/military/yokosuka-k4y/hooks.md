# Hooks for Yokosuka K4Y

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Every Imperial Japanese Navy floatplane pilot in WWII started in one of these. Two-hundred-and-eleven were built. Almost none survived the war.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

Before a pilot could fly an Aichi E13A 'Jake' off a cruiser catapult, they had to learn how to land a floatplane on the water. From 1933 to 1940 the Yokosuka K4Y was the IJN's basic floatplane trainer — a small, forgiving biplane with twin floats, designed at the Yokosuka Naval Air Technical Arsenal and built in modest numbers. It is the kind of aircraft that almost never gets remembered, because it shot down nothing, sank nothing, and survived nothing. But it taught the men who flew the war.
