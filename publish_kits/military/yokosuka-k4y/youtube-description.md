The Yokosuka K4Y was a Japanese single-engined, two-seat biplane floatplane trainer of the 1930s. Designed by the Yokosuka Naval Air Technical Arsenal and built in modest numbers (around 211 airframes from 1933 to 1940), it was the Imperial Japanese Navy's basic floatplane trainer right through the Second World War.

In this video we look at how Japanese naval aviation actually trained its catapult-floatplane pilots before the Pacific War, why floatplane handling was its own distinct skill set, the K4Y's role as a forgiving introductory airframe, and the broader pipeline that took student pilots from the K4Y through more capable types like the E1Y, E7K, and E13A.
