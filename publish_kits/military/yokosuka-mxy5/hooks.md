# Hooks for Yokosuka MXY5

## Shortform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts — 3 sec)

Japan needed a transport glider. Built twelve. Never used one in combat.

## Longform (YouTube — 30 sec cold open)

The Yokosuka MXY5 was a Japanese military transport glider produced for the Imperial Japanese Navy during the Second World War. It was a fabric-wrapped plywood and steel-tube design with a retractable undercarriage, flight tested in 1942. Only twelve were built and none saw operational service, which makes it one of the more invisible glider programs of the war, overshadowed by the German DFS and Gotha programs and the American CG-4.
