The Yokosuka MXY5 was a Japanese military transport glider produced for the Imperial Japanese Navy during the Second World War. Structurally it was a fabric-wrapped plywood skin over a tubular steel frame, fitted with a retractable undercarriage and an emergency skid for off-airfield landings. Flight testing began in 1942. The total production run was twelve aircraft, none of which saw operational service. The story of the MXY5 is also the story of Japan's broader hesitation around airborne assault. The Imperial Japanese Army experimented with paratroop and glider operations early in the war but never developed the doctrine, transport capacity, or logistical infrastructure to use them at scale, and the IJN's even smaller glider effort never produced a meaningful operational role. This video covers the MXY5's design and construction techniques, the broader landscape of Japanese airborne and glider development, why the program produced twelve aircraft and stopped, and how this fits with the much larger Allied and German glider efforts.
