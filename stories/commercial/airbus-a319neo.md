# airbus-a319neo
## Hooks
- The A319neo is the rarest member of the A320neo family — fewer than 100 firm orders versus thousands of A320neos and A321neos, because re-engining a shorter airframe gave diminishing fuel-burn returns.
- The A319neo's biggest customer is the ACJ319neo business jet — billionaires and heads of state ordered more A319neos as private jets than airlines ever did as airliners.
- American Airlines flies one of the largest A319 (ceo) fleets in the world but never converted to the neo — the airline calculated that for short hops, the existing planes would never burn enough fuel to pay back the upgrade.
- easyJet and Air China placed the early A319neo airline orders, but most carriers concluded the A320neo's 30 extra seats were worth more than the A319neo's slightly shorter runway requirement.
- The A319neo's first flight on March 31, 2017 came years after the A320neo, reflecting Airbus's accurate prediction that demand for the shorter variant would be weak — the program existed largely to maintain the family completeness.

## Seed paragraph

Airbus built the A319neo for airlines and ended up selling it to billionaires. The shorter, lighter version of the A320neo turned out to be the wrong shape for commercial flying — every spare seat is profit, and the A320neo simply offered more. But for an oligarch who needs an Atlantic-range jet with a cocktail bar, the A319neo is the perfect platform. The most exclusive airliner Airbus ever built is hiding inside its narrowbody family.
