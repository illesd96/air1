# airbus-a320
## Hooks
- The A320 was the first airliner with full fly-by-wire flight controls and side-stick controllers — pilots literally cannot push the stick into a stall, a software 'envelope protection' philosophy Boeing still rejects today.
- Air France Flight 296 crashed at the Habsheim air show on June 26, 1988 — less than three months after entry into service — during a low-altitude flyby, igniting a 30-year argument over whether fly-by-wire 'alpha protection' helped or hurt the crew.
- Captain Sullenberger ditched US Airways 1549 onto the Hudson on January 15, 2009 after a bird strike took out both engines — his glide to the river was extended by the A320's fly-by-wire flight-envelope protection holding the optimum sink rate automatically.
- Airbus's no-feedback side-stick design — meaning one pilot can move the stick without the other feeling it — has been blamed by some investigators in Air France 447, where the right-seat pilot held back-pressure all the way to the ocean.
- The A320 has a small folding cockpit table that pulls out from under the side-stick — a design touch impossible on Boeing yokes, that pilots use for meals and paperwork in cruise.
- Airbus engineer Bernard Ziegler famously declared the A320 could be flown by 'my concierge' — an arrogant boast that became one of the most-quoted lines in aviation safety history.

## Seed paragraph

In 1988, an Airbus engineer bragged that his new fly-by-wire airliner could be flown by his concierge. Three months later, the same plane crashed into a forest at an air show in France. Twenty years after that, another A320 glided onto the Hudson River and saved 155 lives because the software wouldn't let the pilot stall. The A320 forced aviation to ask: should the computer ever overrule the pilot?
