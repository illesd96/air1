# airbus-a320neo
## Hooks
- The A320neo's two engine choices — the Pratt & Whitney PW1100G geared turbofan and the CFM LEAP-1A — were the result of Airbus deliberately keeping competition alive on the wing, a strategy Boeing abandoned with the MAX's LEAP-only configuration.
- Pratt & Whitney's geared turbofan introduced a literal gearbox between the fan and the low-pressure turbine — letting them spin at different speeds for the first time on a production airliner, and creating years of reliability headaches for early operators.
- The A320neo outsold the 737 MAX in net orders from 2011 onward — and after the MAX groundings of 2019, Airbus's lead became so large that Boeing executives privately admitted the single-aisle race was over.
- Lufthansa was the launch customer in January 2016 — a deliberate choice by Airbus to put the new jet under the strictest possible operational reporting from day one.
- The PW1100G suffered powder-metal contamination issues in 2023 that grounded hundreds of A320neos worldwide — airlines parked aircraft on tarmacs while waiting up to a year for engine inspections.
- Airbus achieved roughly 15% fuel burn improvement vs. the A320ceo not just from engines, but from new Sharklet wingtip devices — bringing the family's range to over 3,500 nautical miles for narrow-body transcontinental flights.

## Seed paragraph

In 2010, Airbus made a quiet decision: re-engine the A320 rather than build a clean-sheet replacement. Boeing thought the move was lazy. Two years later, Airbus's order book was so far ahead that Boeing was forced to rush the 737 MAX into existence. 346 people died because of that rush. The A320neo didn't beat the MAX in the marketplace — it beat the MAX into existence.
