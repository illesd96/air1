# airbus-a330neo
## Hooks
- The A330neo exists almost solely because Airbus needed an A380 replacement strategy after seeing the 787's order book — re-engining a 1990s frame with Rolls-Royce Trent 7000s was cheaper than a clean-sheet design.
- TAP Air Portugal was the launch customer for the A330-900 in 2018, choosing it over the 787 partly because Airbus offered better financing terms during the airline's bailout-driven fleet renewal.
- Rolls-Royce produces the Trent 7000 exclusively for the A330neo — the engine has a slightly larger fan than the Trent 1000 on the 787, but the design is otherwise nearly identical, an unusual sole-source bet by Airbus.
- The A330neo's 'Airspace' cabin was Airbus's quiet response to Boeing's 787 Dreamliner cabin marketing — bigger overhead bins, LED mood lighting, and curved sidewalls that mimic the rival's 'wow factor.'
- Sales were disappointing for years — Airbus had to dramatically discount the A330neo against the 787 — until COVID disrupted long-haul economics and several airlines suddenly preferred the lower-cost regional widebody.

## Seed paragraph

Airbus launched the A330neo in 2014 with one job: blunt the Boeing 787's momentum without spending the billions of a clean-sheet jet. It was a re-engine and a paint job. Sales were terrible — until COVID grounded the wide-body world and airlines suddenly wanted cheap, used-shape twin-jets they already knew how to maintain. The A330neo became a sleeper hit because aviation collapsed around it.
