# airbus-a350
## Hooks
- The A350 only exists because Steven Udvar-Hazy of ILFC — the world's most powerful aircraft lessor — publicly humiliated Airbus's first A330-based 'A350' proposal in 2006, calling it warmed-over leftovers, and forced a complete clean-sheet redesign.
- Qatar Airways grounded 23 of its A350s in a 2021 dispute with Airbus over paint and copper-mesh lightning protection cracks under the surface, leading to a $2 billion lawsuit and Airbus canceling the airline's A321neo order in retaliation.
- The A350's wing is the largest single piece of carbon-fiber composite Airbus has ever made — built in a single autoclave in Broughton, Wales, and shipped on a Beluga to Toulouse.
- The A350-1000 broke the 777-300ER's monopoly on ultra-long-haul, letting Qantas plan 'Project Sunrise' — non-stop Sydney to London, 20 hours, the longest commercial flight ever attempted.
- Airbus had to invent a new black-and-white 'racoon mask' cockpit windshield design to fit the curved composite nose without compromising bird-strike requirements.
- Tom Enders launched the A350 XWB redesign in 2006 over the personal objection of the EADS board, betting Airbus's recovery from the A380 fiasco on a single composite jet to fight the 787.

## Seed paragraph

In 2006, the world's biggest aircraft buyer told Airbus its newest jet was 'a piece of garbage' — to its face, in public. Airbus listened. They tore up the design and started over with carbon fiber, copper mesh, and a windshield shaped like a panda's eye mask. Then Qatar Airways noticed the paint was cracking, and a $2 billion lawsuit threatened to ground the entire program.
