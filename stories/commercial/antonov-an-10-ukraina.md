# antonov-an-10-ukraina
## Hooks
- The An-10 was retired from Aeroflot in 1973 after a horrific fatigue-failure crash at Kharkov killed 122 people — wings literally tore off in cruise due to structural cracks that hadn't been detected.
- The An-10 shared an airframe with the An-12 military transport — its civil version was essentially the cargo plane with passenger windows cut in, a Soviet design philosophy unimaginable in the West.
- Aeroflot used the An-10 on rough provincial fields where no Tu-104 jet could operate — its high wing and rugged landing gear made it the only way to fly into much of the Soviet hinterland.
- Several An-10 prototypes used a five-fin tail experiment before settling on the production twin-fin layout — a layer of empirical fin-area tuning the Soviets did in flight rather than wind tunnels.
- The An-10 was originally named 'Ukraina' as a propaganda nod to Antonov's Kyiv design bureau — a brand that vanished entirely from Soviet aviation after the 1972 crash investigation.

## Seed paragraph

In May 1972, an Aeroflot turboprop coming in to land at Kharkov shed its wings in mid-air. Investigators found that the entire An-10 fleet had been quietly cracking apart for years — a structural-fatigue problem the Soviet system had refused to acknowledge. Within months, every An-10 in commercial service was grounded forever. The 'Ukraina' was the most embarrassing failure in Antonov's history — and the West never knew.
