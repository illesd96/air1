# boeing-787
## Hooks
- The 787 is roughly 50% composite by weight, including a one-piece carbon-fiber barrel fuselage built by Mitsubishi and Kawasaki in Japan — Boeing outsourced more of the 787 than any previous jet, then had to buy back failing suppliers when the program collapsed.
- All 50 Dreamliners worldwide were grounded by the FAA on January 16, 2013 after lithium-ion battery fires on an ANA flight and a JAL aircraft at Boston — the first FAA-ordered grounding of a U.S. type since the DC-10 in 1979.
- Boeing's solution to the battery fires wasn't to fix the batteries — it was to put each one inside a stainless-steel containment box vented overboard, essentially admitting the lithium chemistry could not be made safe.
- Whistleblower John Barnett, a Boeing quality manager at the 787 line in Charleston, was found dead in March 2024 during a deposition over his claims of serious manufacturing defects — the death was ruled a suicide.
- The Dreamliner introduced electronic cabin window 'dimming' (electrochromic glass) instead of mechanical shades — cabin crews can now override the entire plane's windows from a single panel in the galley.
- The 787-9 has 'gust suppression' — the wings actively flex via ailerons in response to turbulence sensors, reducing felt motion in the cabin by roughly 50 percent.

## Seed paragraph

In January 2013, lithium batteries on two brand-new Dreamliners caught fire within nine days of each other, and the FAA grounded the entire fleet. Boeing couldn't figure out why. Their answer wasn't to fix the batteries — it was to put them inside a stainless-steel coffin and vent the smoke overboard. A decade later, the whistleblower who said the planes were built wrong was found dead during his own deposition.
