# a2-cz-ellipse-spirit
## Hooks
- The Ellipse Spirit is a Czech ultralight introduced at the Aero Friedrichshafen show in 2011 — a side-by-side two-seater designed to slip in under the European microlight 472.5-kilogram weight limit.
- Czech Republic has quietly become Europe's ultralight superpower, with manufacturers like A2 CZ, BRM Aero, Aeropro and TL-Ultralight exporting kits worldwide from a handful of small factories.
- The airplane is sold as either a complete factory-built ready-to-fly aircraft or as a kit, letting buyers choose between regulatory simplicity or amateur-build savings.
- Like most modern Czech ultralights, the Spirit uses a Rotax 912 engine — the same 100-horsepower air-and-water-cooled boxer-four that powers thousands of light sport aircraft worldwide.
- The Czech ultralight scene was born out of communist-era restrictions on private aviation — when the Iron Curtain fell, all those underground glider clubs poured their skills into building light airplanes for export.

## Seed paragraph

When the Iron Curtain fell in 1989, the Czech Republic was sitting on decades of suppressed aviation talent — communist-era glider clubs full of engineers with nowhere to go. They started building light airplanes for export. Thirty years later, half the ultralights flying over Europe were built in workshops outside Prague. The A2 CZ Ellipse Spirit is one of them — a Rotax-powered Czech two-seater quietly slipping into hangars from Australia to Argentina.
