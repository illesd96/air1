# aac-seastar-sealoon
## Hooks
- The AAC Seastar Sealoon is a Canadian amateur-built amphibian flying boat designed by Amphibian Airplanes of Canada — a hull-down design that lands directly on water or on retractable wheels.
- Like most homebuilt amphibians, the Seastar Sealoon uses a pusher propeller mounted high above the hull to keep the prop tips out of spray during water operations.
- Canada has more registered floatplanes per capita than any other country in the world, which is why a Canadian designer chose to make an amphibian his first kit aircraft project.
- The Sealoon is currently in low-volume kit production, intended for builders who want a personal floatplane without paying Cessna Caravan amphib prices.
- Bush operations in northern Canada often require an airplane that can take off from a bay in the morning, land on a gravel strip at noon, and splash down on a lake by sunset — exactly the niche the Sealoon was designed to fill.

## Seed paragraph

Northern Canada has more registered floatplanes per capita than anywhere else on earth, which is why a small kit-airplane company in the country decided to build a homebuilt amphibian. The AAC Seastar Sealoon is what they came up with — a boat hull, a high pusher prop, retractable wheels for the gravel strip and a Rotax up top. It is designed for the very specific kind of pilot who wants to take off from a lake, land on a logging road and splash down on another lake before dinner.
