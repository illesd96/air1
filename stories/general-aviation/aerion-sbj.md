# aerion-sbj
## Hooks
- The Aerion SBJ was designed in the 2000s to be the first private supersonic jet, cruising at Mach 1.6 to halve flight times across the Atlantic for executives in a hurry.
- It used a radical 'natural laminar flow' wing design developed in partnership with NASA, intended to reduce drag enough that supersonic flight became fuel-efficient.
- Aerion attracted billionaire backer Robert Bass and aerospace partners including Lockheed Martin and Boeing — but the company spent over a billion dollars and never built a flight article.
- In May 2021 Aerion suddenly shut down, ending two decades of supersonic business jet promises with not a single airframe completed.
- The project was a cautionary tale about how hard it is to bring back civilian supersonic flight after Concorde — every successor concept has either died on the drawing board or is still struggling toward first flight.

## Seed paragraph

For 17 years, Aerion promised executives a supersonic business jet — Mach 1.6, New York to London in four hours, a billion-dollar revival of Concorde for the corporate set. They hired NASA engineers. They signed deals with Lockheed and Boeing. They raised a fortune and built a glittering Florida headquarters. In May 2021 they ran out of money and vanished overnight, never having flown an airplane. The SBJ became the most expensive paper jet in modern aviation history.
