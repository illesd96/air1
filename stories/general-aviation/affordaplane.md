# affordaplane
## Hooks
- The Affordaplane is a plans-built single-seat ultralight designed to be constructed from aluminum tubing and hardware-store parts for under $5,000 — among the cheapest real airplanes a person can build.
- The plans claim a complete build takes 150 to 250 hours, meaning a determined hobbyist could be flying within a single year of weekend garage work.
- It legally qualifies under U.S. FAR Part 103, which means no pilot certificate, no airframe registration, no medical and no annual inspection are required to fly it.
- The Affordaplane is the spiritual descendant of the 1980s 'air bike' movement, when designers like Bill Skiba tried to bring flying down to the price of a motorcycle.
- Critics call Part 103 ultralights 'lawn chairs with engines' — and from some angles, an Affordaplane in flight does indeed resemble a folding chair strapped to a chainsaw motor.

## Seed paragraph

No pilot license. No registration. No medical certificate. No annual inspection. Just a stack of aluminum tubing from the hardware store, a set of plans you printed off the internet, and 200 weekend hours in your garage. The Affordaplane is what happens when American aviation regulation hits its absolute lower bound, and a determined hobbyist decides he is going to fly an airplane this year whether the FAA likes it or not.
