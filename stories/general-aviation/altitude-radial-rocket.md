# altitude-radial-rocket
## Hooks
- The Altitude Radial Rocket is one of the only modern homebuilt kit airplanes designed around a radial engine — typically a Russian Vedeneyev M14P, the same nine-cylinder engine that powered the Yak-52 trainer.
- The radial gives the airplane an almost vintage warbird silhouette, with that distinctive blunt nose and the round engine cowling that makes it look like a P-47 shrunk to homebuilt size.
- The kit is produced in Overland Park, Kansas by the Altitude Group and is aimed at builders who want a fast aerobatic two-seater that sounds and looks like a 1940s fighter.
- Radial engines burn a lot of oil — a typical M14P throws out a quart per hour even in normal operation, which is why every Radial Rocket comes back from a flight with belly streaks.
- Only a handful of Radial Rockets have been completed, but the type has a cult following among ex-military pilots who want a personal Yak-52 they can keep at a grass strip.

## Seed paragraph

Drop a Soviet nine-cylinder radial engine into a homebuilt kit airframe in Overland Park, Kansas, and you get the Altitude Radial Rocket — a private airplane that sounds and smells like a 1940s warbird, leaves oil streaks down its belly after every flight, and turns heads at every fly-in within 500 miles. It is what happens when an American kit builder, an aerobatic pilot and a surplus Russian engine catalog all meet in the same hangar.
