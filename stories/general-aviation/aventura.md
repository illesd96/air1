# aventura
## Hooks
- The Aventura is a kit-built ultralight amphibian that lets you take off from a lake, land on a runway, and pay roughly the price of a used pickup truck for the whole thing.
- Designed by Bob Bailey in 1995, the aircraft uses a pusher prop mounted above the wing so the propeller stays clear of spray during water takeoffs.
- It is one of the few amphibians in the world that legally qualifies under U.S. FAR Part 103 ultralight rules in some configurations — meaning no pilot license required.
- Aero Adventure builds the kits in DeLand, Florida — about 20 miles from a string of Central Florida lakes that serve as the de facto factory test site.
- Aventura builders typically spend 400 to 700 hours assembling the airplane in their garage before flying it off a quiet lake at dawn.

## Seed paragraph

There is a quiet lake somewhere in Central Florida at six in the morning. The pilot — who built his own airplane in his garage out of aluminum tubing and pop rivets — drops the boat hull onto the water, opens the throttle and lifts off in a hundred feet. The Aventura is what flying looks like when you strip away the FAA, the certifications and the bills, and just put a pusher prop on a boat with wings.
