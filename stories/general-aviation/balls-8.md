# balls-8
## Hooks
- Balls 8 got its name from its tail number: 52-008. In military aviator slang, three zeros are 'balls' — so 'balls 8' means '0-0-0-8.'
- The NB-52B was the launch mothership for the X-15 rocket plane, dropping the experimental aircraft on 106 of its 199 flights between 1959 and 1968 and helping put pilots into space.
- After the X-15 program ended, Balls 8 spent the next four decades dropping every other oddball experimental vehicle NASA needed to test — lifting bodies, parachute test articles, the X-43 hypersonic scramjet, you name it.
- When she retired in 2004 at age 49, Balls 8 was the oldest B-52 still flying and the last B-52B model in the U.S. inventory.
- Her last and most famous payload, the X-43A scramjet, hit Mach 9.6 in 2004 after Balls 8 dropped it from 40,000 feet over the Pacific — the fastest air-breathing aircraft ever flown.

## Seed paragraph

She rolled off the Boeing line in 1955 as a brand-new B-52B Stratofortress. Fifty years later she was still flying — older than most NASA engineers, painted black under one wing where rocket exhaust had scorched her aluminum, and carrying the strangest experimental vehicles America could dream up. They called her Balls 8 because of her tail number, and she dropped the X-15, the lifting bodies, and the hypersonic scramjet that hit Mach 9.6. No other airplane has had a stranger career.
