# bombardier-global-7500
## Hooks
- The Global 7500 can fly 7,700 nautical miles nonstop — New York to Hong Kong, or Sydney to Los Angeles — without ever refueling, the longest range of any purpose-built business jet.
- Its four-zone cabin includes a permanent bed, a full galley, and what Bombardier calls 'the Nuage seat,' the only fully reclining executive chair certified to handle a takeoff load.
- In 2019 a Global 7500 set a city-pair record flying Sydney to Detroit, 8,225 nautical miles, in 16 hours and 30 minutes nonstop.
- The airplane is the first business jet with an all-new wing design in a generation, optimized by computational fluid dynamics to cruise at Mach 0.925 — faster than any airliner.
- Each Global 7500 sells for around $75 million empty, and Elon Musk, Jay-Z and assorted Saudi princes are all on the customer list.

## Seed paragraph

It is 1 a.m. somewhere over the Bering Sea. A billionaire is asleep in a real queen-size bed, 41,000 feet above the ice. In the cockpit, two pilots are 14 hours into a single uninterrupted leg from London to Tahiti. The Global 7500 doesn't refuel. It doesn't even slow down. It simply flies further than any business jet has ever flown — and lets the people inside forget they're in an airplane at all.
