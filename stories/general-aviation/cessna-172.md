# cessna-172
## Hooks
- More than 44,000 Cessna 172s have been built since 1955, making it the most-produced aircraft in human history, more than any Boeing, Airbus or Soviet jet combined.
- In 1958 a 172 named Hacienda stayed airborne over Las Vegas for 64 days, 22 hours and 19 minutes straight, refueled mid-air from a Ford truck driving alongside it on the desert road.
- In 1987 a 19-year-old West German named Mathias Rust flew a rented 172 from Helsinki through Soviet air defenses and landed in Red Square next to the Kremlin, triggering one of Gorbachev's biggest political purges.
- Cessna shut down 172 production for a decade in the 1980s due to crushing product liability lawsuits and only resumed after Congress passed the 1994 General Aviation Revitalization Act.
- Roughly two-thirds of all licensed pilots alive today learned to fly in a Skyhawk, including most airline captains flying you across the Atlantic tonight.

## Seed paragraph

May 28, 1987. A teenager from Hamburg lands a tiny red and white airplane in the middle of Red Square. The Soviet air defense network, the most paranoid in the world, somehow let him fly across 500 miles of forbidden airspace. The airplane was a Cessna 172, the same machine that has taught more humans to fly than any other object ever built.
