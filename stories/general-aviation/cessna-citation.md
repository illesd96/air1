# cessna-citation
## Hooks
- When the first Citation flew in 1969, Lear Jet pilots derisively called it the 'Slow-tation' for its leisurely 350-knot cruise — Cessna leaned in and marketed it as the safe, easy-to-fly business jet.
- More than 8,000 Citations have been delivered, making it the largest business jet family in history and out-producing every Gulfstream, Bombardier and Falcon line combined.
- The Citation X, introduced in 1996, cruised at Mach 0.92 and held the title of fastest civilian airplane in the sky after Concorde retired.
- Cessna founder Russ Meyer bet the company on the Citation in 1972, gambling that owner-pilots would graduate from piston twins straight into a personal jet — and they did.
- Country star Jim Reeves's death in a piston single, and the deaths of executives in cumbersome older jets, helped sell the Citation as the safe, sensible business jet for the rest of America.

## Seed paragraph

In 1969 Cessna built a business jet so slow and easy to fly that Lear Jet pilots openly mocked it. Cessna shrugged, pointed at the insurance underwriters, and started selling. Fifty years later, more Citations have been built than every Gulfstream, Bombardier and Falcon combined. The Slow-tation became the most successful family of business jets in human history.
