# cirrus-sr22
## Hooks
- The SR22 is the only certified GA aircraft with a whole-airframe ballistic parachute (CAPS) as standard equipment, fired by a rocket motor through the fuselage skin.
- By the mid-2020s CAPS pulls had saved well over 200 lives across the Cirrus fleet, with every successful deployment logged publicly on Cirrus's website.
- Cirrus founders Alan and Dale Klapmeier built the company in a dairy barn in Wisconsin after Alan survived a mid-air collision in a Cessna that killed his instructor, an experience that drove the parachute design.
- The SR22 has been the world's best-selling single-engine piston airplane every year since 2003, despite costing more than a new luxury car.
- Early SR22s earned a 'doctor killer 2.0' reputation as wealthy low-time owners crashed them at alarming rates, until Cirrus mandated standardized training that dramatically cut the accident rate.
- The airplane's avionics, leather seats and side-stick controller were lifted straight from automotive design language, deliberately making it feel like a BMW instead of a Cessna.

## Seed paragraph

Somewhere over the American Midwest, an engine quits. The pilot doesn't reach for a checklist. He reaches for a red handle in the ceiling, pulls, and a rocket fires through the roof of his airplane. Seconds later the entire Cirrus SR22 is hanging under a parachute, drifting gently toward a soybean field. It is the only certified airplane in the world that lets its pilot do that, and it has saved hundreds of lives doing it.
