# gulfstream-g650
## Hooks
- The G650 flies higher and faster than almost anything in civilian aviation — Mach 0.925 cruise, 51,000 feet ceiling — meaning it routinely climbs above airliners and storm cells.
- When it launched in 2012 the waiting list ran years long and used G650s sold for more than new ones because billionaires didn't want to wait.
- A Gulfstream G650ER set the record for the fastest circumnavigation of the world by a business jet in 2019, completing the trip in 41 hours and 7 minutes.
- The cabin is pressurized to a 4,850-foot equivalent altitude at maximum cruise, low enough that passengers don't suffer the dehydration and jet lag of an airliner cabin.
- Customers include Elon Musk, Steven Spielberg, the Walton family and the official Saudi royal fleet — and the airplane's tail number is among the most-tracked on flight-tracking websites in the world.

## Seed paragraph

Look up at FL510 and you might see a Gulfstream G650 cruising at Mach 0.92 — higher than the airliners, faster than the airliners, with a billionaire watching a movie inside. When it launched, used examples sold for millions more than new ones because the waiting list was years long. The G650 didn't just take the top spot in business aviation. It defined what 'the top' even meant.
