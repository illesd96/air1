# piper-cherokee
## Hooks
- The PA-28 Cherokee was designed by John Thorp specifically to undercut the Cessna 172 on price — Piper's bean counters told the engineers the airplane had to retail for under $9,000 in 1961.
- Cherokee variants have killed more famous people than almost any GA airplane: John Denver died in an experimental Cherokee derivative, and so did singer-songwriter Jim Reeves and racing driver Graham Hill.
- The Cherokee's stub wing — chosen because constant-chord rectangular wings are cheap to build — earned it the affectionate nickname 'the Hershey Bar wing.'
- Over 32,000 PA-28s have been built and the airplane still rolls off the line in Vero Beach, Florida, with essentially the same airframe Piper drew in 1960.
- The Cherokee Six stretched variant became the original air-taxi airplane in the U.S., flying packages, cancelled checks and small-town commuters for thousands of operators in the 1970s.

## Seed paragraph

Piper needed a 172-killer. So in 1960 they handed designer John Thorp a single instruction: make it cheap. He gave them a low-wing, rectangular-winged, single-door airplane with stamped sheet metal everywhere it could possibly substitute for forging. Sixty years later, more than 32,000 Cherokees have been built, and a flight school somewhere in the world is launching one into the pattern every minute of every day.
