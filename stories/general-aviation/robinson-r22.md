# robinson-r22
## Hooks
- Frank Robinson designed the R22 alone in his garage in 1973 after Hughes Helicopters refused to build a small civilian trainer — he wanted to bring helicopters down to the price of a luxury car.
- The R22 became the most-produced civilian helicopter in history and almost every civilian helicopter pilot in the world has at least a few hours in one.
- The teetering two-blade rotor makes the R22 dangerously susceptible to 'mast bumping' in low-G maneuvers, leading the FAA to publish a special airworthiness directive and require additional training — known as the SFAR 73 endorsement.
- The R22 has the fastest control response of any production helicopter, which makes it brilliant for teaching and unforgiving when you fly it badly.
- A new R22 sells for around $400,000 — astonishingly cheap for a helicopter — which is exactly why every flight school still buys them and every news report about a low-time helicopter accident seems to mention it.

## Seed paragraph

A 43-year-old engineer named Frank Robinson quit Hughes Helicopters in 1973 to build a helicopter no one else thought was possible — a brand-new civilian trainer cheap enough for a flight school to afford. He sketched it in his garage. Today the R22 is the most-produced civilian helicopter in history. It is also one of the unforgiving rotorcraft ever built, and you learn that the first time your instructor pushes the cyclic forward to zero-G.
