# bf-109
## Hooks
- Willy Messerschmitt won the 1934 fighter contract over Heinkel, Arado, and Focke-Wulf despite being politically blacklisted by Erhard Milch — the Reichsluftfahrtministerium official whose brother had died in a Messerschmitt airliner crash and who openly wanted Messerschmitt destroyed.
- The Bf 109's terrifying narrow-track inward-retracting landing gear killed an estimated 1,500 Luftwaffe pilots in takeoff and landing accidents — more than were lost to any single Allied fighter type.
- Erich Hartmann shot down 352 aircraft flying Bf 109s on the Eastern Front, more than any pilot in history, by closing to 50 meters before firing because he believed deflection shooting was for amateurs.
- Spain kept building the Bf 109 under license as the Hispano HA-1112 'Buchón' until 1958 — fitted with a Rolls-Royce Merlin engine, the same powerplant that drove its Battle of Britain enemy the Spitfire, making it a Frankenstein 109 with the wrong nose and the wrong heartbeat.
- The fuselage was designed around the lightest, smallest airframe Messerschmitt could wrap around a Daimler-Benz engine, which is why the cockpit was so cramped pilots reported their knees hit the instrument panel — Adolf Galland called it 'a flying coffin if you weren't paying attention.'

## Seed paragraph

Erhard Milch hated Willy Messerschmitt so much that when the Reichsluftfahrtministerium opened the 1934 fighter competition, Milch tried to rig it against him. Messerschmitt won anyway with a tiny aluminum monoplane built around the largest engine he could find, and the Bf 109 would go on to be the most-produced fighter in history. Its narrow landing gear would kill more of its own pilots than the RAF ever did.
