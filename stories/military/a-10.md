# a-10
## Hooks
- The A-10's GAU-8 Avenger cannon is so large the entire aircraft was designed around it — the gun weighs more than a Volkswagen Beetle and fires depleted uranium rounds at 3,900 per minute.
- The recoil from firing the gun is so strong it actually slows the A-10 down in flight — a sustained burst can decelerate the jet by 15 knots.
- Pilots sit in a titanium 'bathtub' of armor that can survive direct hits from 23mm anti-aircraft fire — Captain Kim Campbell flew her Warthog home in 2003 after losing hydraulics over Baghdad, using manual cables.
- During the 1991 Gulf War, A-10s destroyed more Iraqi tanks than any other coalition aircraft — over 900 tanks in 40 days, often killing them from beyond their gun range.
- The Air Force has tried to retire the A-10 at least seven times since the 1990s; Congress has blocked every attempt, often citing letters from infantry soldiers who survived because of it.
- Two A-10s have famously landed safely after one of their engines was completely blown off — the aircraft's high-mounted, separated turbofans were specifically designed to keep flying after catastrophic damage.

## Seed paragraph

In April 2003, Captain Kim Campbell's A-10 took an Iraqi anti-aircraft hit over Baghdad that turned her hydraulics into mist. She had two options: eject into enemy territory, or fly an unflyable plane home using manual cables that hadn't been used in anger since the Korean War. She chose option two. The Warthog she flew was an aircraft the Air Force had been trying to kill for thirty years — and Congress kept resurrecting.
