# c-130
## Hooks
- In 1963 a USMC test pilot landed and took off a C-130 from the deck of the aircraft carrier USS Forrestal — 29 times — without using a tailhook or catapult, proving it could be done.
- The 'Fat Albert' C-130 of the Blue Angels has flown so many airshows that it's become a star in its own right, performing JATO-rocket takeoffs until the bottles were finally exhausted in 2009.
- During the 1976 Entebbe raid, Israeli C-130s flew 2,500 miles, landed at a hostile African airport in the dark, and rescued 102 hostages in 90 minutes — only one Israeli soldier died.
- An AC-130 gunship carries a 105mm howitzer cannon — the same gun used by ground artillery — and fires it sideways out of the fuselage in flight.
- C-130s have served as everything from Antarctic research transports landing on skis to hurricane hunters flying directly into Category 5 storms.
- A C-130 has been in continuous production since 1954, making it the longest continuously produced military aircraft in history.

## Seed paragraph

On the night of July 4, 1976, four Israeli C-130 Hercules transports landed at Entebbe airport in Uganda. They were 2,500 miles from home, in a country whose dictator had personally welcomed the hijackers holding 102 Israeli hostages. Ninety minutes later they were gone, with all but four hostages aboard. Over 70 years, the Herc has done everything from rescue raids to landing on aircraft carriers — the only four-engine transport ever to do it.
