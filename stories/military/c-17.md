# c-17
## Hooks
- A C-17 holds the record for the most people ever carried by a single aircraft: on August 15, 2021, during the fall of Kabul, Reach 871 took off with 823 Afghan evacuees crammed onto its cargo floor.
- The C-17 can land on a 3,500-foot dirt strip with 170,000 pounds of cargo — about a third the length of a normal airliner runway.
- When Boeing tried to shut down C-17 production in 2015, it was the end of a 30-year run — and the last large military airlifter ever built in California.
- C-17s flew the entire NORAD-tracked Santa Tracker hardware to Antarctica, and routinely transport the Presidential motorcade and Marine One when the President travels overseas.
- During the 2010 Haiti earthquake, C-17s established an air bridge from Charleston to Port-au-Prince that delivered more aid in 30 days than ground convoys could move in a year.
- The C-17's reverse thrusters are so powerful it can back up under its own power — useful on tiny airstrips with no ground equipment.

## Seed paragraph

On August 15, 2021, as the Taliban entered Kabul, the crew of Reach 871 made a decision that broke every regulation in the book. They lowered their cargo ramp on the tarmac at Hamid Karzai International Airport and let people aboard. Then more people. Then more. When they finally took off, there were 823 souls sitting on the bare cargo floor of a single C-17 — the most a fixed-wing aircraft has ever carried in human history. The plane is still flying today.
