# f-15
## Hooks
- The F-15 has a confirmed air-to-air combat record of 104 wins and zero losses across five decades of operation by the US, Israel, and Saudi Arabia.
- In 1983 an Israeli F-15 landed safely with only one wing — pilot Zivi Nedivi mid-air-collided with an A-4, lost his right wing entirely, but flew the jet to base on engine thrust and lift from the fuselage alone.
- Test pilots used to demonstrate the F-15's thrust-to-weight ratio by performing a vertical 'Viking funeral' takeoff — straight up from brake release to 30,000 feet.
- An F-15A named 'Celestial Eagle' became the only fighter to ever destroy a satellite, killing the failing Solwind P78-1 spacecraft over the Pacific in 1985 using an ASM-135 missile.
- The F-15EX, delivered in 2021, can carry more air-to-air missiles than any fighter in history — up to 22 — making it a 'missile truck' designed specifically to overwhelm enemy defenses.
- Israeli F-15s flew the 1985 raid on PLO headquarters in Tunisia, a 4,800-mile round trip that required multiple aerial refuelings and was one of the longest fighter strike missions ever conducted.

## Seed paragraph

On May 1, 1983, Israeli pilot Zivi Nedivi collided mid-air with another aircraft and watched his right wing snap clean off. He should have died. Instead, he flew his F-15 home and landed it — engineers later said it was aerodynamically impossible. The Eagle has finished 104 air-to-air engagements without a single loss. This is the story of the fighter that refuses to be killed.
