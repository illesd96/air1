# f-22
## Hooks
- Congress banned the export of the F-22 to anyone — even Japan and Israel — making it the only modern American fighter that has never been sold overseas.
- An F-22 once flew so close to an Iranian F-4 Phantom that the pilot radioed 'You really ought to go home,' before peeling away — the Iranian aircraft hadn't even spotted him until that moment.
- The Raptor's first confirmed air-to-air kill came in 2023 — but it wasn't against another jet, it was a Chinese spy balloon shot down off the coast of South Carolina with a Sidewinder.
- F-22 pilots in 2012 began suffering hypoxia symptoms that nearly grounded the entire fleet — investigators eventually traced it to a faulty vest valve, not the oxygen system itself.
- The production line was deliberately destroyed: Lockheed Martin scrapped the F-22 tooling in 2011, meaning if America wanted more Raptors, they would have to be designed from scratch.
- The F-22 has secret radar-absorbent coatings that have to be reapplied after every flight — maintenance crews spend more time on the skin than on the engines.

## Seed paragraph

In February 2023, an F-22 Raptor took its first-ever combat shot. The target wasn't a Russian Su-57 or a Chinese J-20 — it was a balloon, drifting at 60,000 feet over the Carolinas. After two decades of waiting, the most expensive air-superiority fighter ever built finally drew blood. And here's the part nobody talks about: America can't build any more of them. The factory is gone.
