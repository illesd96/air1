# f-35
## Hooks
- The helmet alone costs over $400,000 and lets the pilot 'look through' the floor of the jet using six external cameras stitched into the visor.
- Lockheed's manufacturing software once couldn't handle the number 100, meaning F-35 part numbers had to roll over and confused the entire supply chain for months.
- An F-35B once shot itself down by firing its own gun pod and ingesting the fragments — the resulting Class A mishap cost over $135 million.
- The Marine Corps' F-35B variant has to hover on a single lift fan driven by a clutch the size of a dinner plate, an engineering trick that took two decades to certify.
- In 2023 a Marine pilot ejected from his F-35B over South Carolina and the jet kept flying on autopilot for 64 miles before crashing — the military had to ask the public for help finding it.
- Israel's F-35I 'Adir' became the first F-35 ever confirmed used in combat, conducting strikes that the IDF only acknowledged years after the fact.

## Seed paragraph

In September 2023, a $100 million stealth fighter went missing over the American South — its pilot had ejected, but the jet refused to crash. For an hour, the most advanced warplane ever built flew itself across two states while the Marine Corps begged civilians on Twitter to help locate it. This is the story of the trillion-dollar machine that nobody, not even its makers, fully understands.
