# f-86
## Hooks
- The F-86's swept wing was reverse-engineered from captured German WWII research — North American found the data in a captured archive and redesigned the Sabre's straight wing in months.
- The MiG-15 vs F-86 dogfights over 'MiG Alley' in Korea pitted American pilots against Soviet pilots flying with Chinese and North Korean markings — both sides knew, neither admitted it for decades.
- Captain Joseph McConnell became America's top Korean War ace with 16 kills before being killed in an F-86 test flight crash three months after returning home.
- Operation Moolah offered $100,000 to any communist pilot who defected with a MiG-15 — North Korean pilot No Kum-Sok finally claimed it in September 1953, flying a MiG to a US base in South Korea.
- F-86s achieved a claimed 10:1 kill ratio against MiG-15s in Korea, though modern analysis suggests the real ratio was closer to 2:1 — still dominant, but not legendary.
- The F-86 was the first fighter to break the sound barrier in a dive during routine combat operations — a Sabre pilot named George Welch may have done it before Yeager's official supersonic flight.

## Seed paragraph

Over a thin sliver of sky in North Korea — 'MiG Alley' — American Sabre pilots fought Soviet pilots flying MiG-15s with North Korean markings. Both sides knew. Neither admitted it. The F-86's swept wing had been reverse-engineered from captured Nazi research, and a single American defection offer of $100,000 finally lured a MiG out of the communist bloc. Captain Joseph McConnell killed 16 MiGs over Korea, came home a hero, then died three months later in a Sabre test crash.
