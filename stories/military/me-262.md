# me-262
## Hooks
- Hitler personally delayed the Me 262 by insisting it be a bomber instead of a fighter — historians estimate this decision cost Germany at least six months and possibly the air war.
- The Me 262's Jumo 004 engines lasted only 25 hours before they had to be replaced — they were essentially disposable, made from substitutes because Germany had no nickel for proper alloys.
- Adolf Galland, Germany's top fighter commander, said after flying the Me 262 that 'it was as if angels were pushing' — and he begged Hitler in writing to mass-produce fighters, not bombers.
- A Walter Nowotny-led squadron of Me 262s shot down approximately 19 American aircraft on March 18, 1945 alone — one of the most successful single-day jet operations in WWII.
- The first Allied pilot to shoot down an Me 262 was Major Joseph Myers in a P-47 Thunderbolt — he caught the jet during its slow landing approach, when its engines couldn't accelerate quickly.
- After the war, the Soviets captured Me 262 tooling and used it to build the Sukhoi Su-9 — and Czechoslovakia continued producing the airframe under the designation Avia S-92 until 1951.

## Seed paragraph

In 1944, Adolf Galland flew the first prototype of a German jet fighter and told his superiors it felt like 'angels were pushing.' For the next year, he begged Hitler in writing to build more of them. Hitler refused — he wanted them as bombers. The decision may have cost Germany the air war. When the Me 262 finally hit the skies in numbers, its engines lasted only 25 hours before disintegrating. But for those 25 hours, nothing in the air could touch it.
