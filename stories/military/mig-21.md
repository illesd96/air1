# mig-21
## Hooks
- Over 11,000 MiG-21s were built — more than any other supersonic jet in history — and they have served in 60+ countries' air forces.
- Romanian MiG-21s were upgraded by Israel in the 1990s with Western avionics in a secret program called the 'LanceR' — turning a Soviet jet into a NATO-compatible fighter.
- In 1966, an Iraqi MiG-21 was famously defected to Israel by pilot Munir Redfa in 'Operation Diamond' — the West got its first complete look at the aircraft that was killing American pilots over Vietnam.
- Indian MiG-21 'Bisons' shot down a Pakistani F-16 in February 2019 during the Balakot crisis, though the loss was never officially acknowledged by Pakistan.
- North Vietnamese MiG-21 pilots claimed dozens of American kills during the Vietnam War — including B-52 bombers shot down over Hanoi using head-on missile passes.
- The MiG-21's tiny cockpit was so cramped that pilots over 5'10" couldn't legally fly it in some air forces — and ejection seats sometimes broke pilots' backs.

## Seed paragraph

In 1966, an Iraqi pilot named Munir Redfa landed his MiG-21 at an Israeli air base. The Mossad had spent two years getting him there. Within weeks the entire West had its hands on the Soviet jet that was killing American pilots over Vietnam. Eleven thousand of these little supersonic darts were built — more than any other supersonic aircraft in history — and they're still fighting in 2026.
