# mig-29
## Hooks
- After German reunification in 1990, the Luftwaffe inherited 24 East German MiG-29s and flew them in NATO exercises until 2003 — revealing serious weaknesses in their radar and avionics to Western pilots.
- An East German defector flew a MiG-29 to West Germany in 1989 in an event so embarrassing to Moscow that the Soviet pilot's family was sent to a gulag.
- Iraqi MiG-29s engaged American F-15s during Desert Storm — five were shot down without scoring a single confirmed kill, partly because Soviet export models had downgraded electronics.
- MiG-29s are still flown by the Ukrainian Air Force in 2024 and have launched American-made AGM-88 HARM missiles using improvised software hacks — a Soviet jet firing NATO weapons.
- The MiG-29's helmet-mounted sight, combined with the high-off-boresight R-73 missile, gave it a major dogfighting advantage that NATO didn't match until the 2000s.
- Slovak MiG-29s were donated to Ukraine in 2023 — the first transfer of supersonic Soviet fighters from a NATO country to the war effort.

## Seed paragraph

In October 1990, the moment East and West Germany reunified, NATO suddenly owned 24 Soviet MiG-29s. For thirteen years, Luftwaffe pilots flew them alongside F-16s and quietly learned everything the West didn't know about the Fulcrum. Today, decades later, Ukrainian pilots are flying the same airframe against Russia — and firing American HARM missiles from Soviet wing pylons through software hacks that shouldn't work, but do.
