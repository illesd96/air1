# mq-9
## Hooks
- An MQ-9 Reaper killed Iranian General Qasem Soleimani in a Baghdad airport strike on January 3, 2020 — the first major-power general killed by a drone strike.
- Reaper pilots fly the aircraft from windowless trailers at Creech Air Force Base in Nevada — often commuting home to suburban Las Vegas after dropping bombs on insurgents 8,000 miles away.
- The MQ-9 can fly for over 27 hours on a single tank of fuel — patrol durations longer than any human-crewed combat aircraft.
- In 2023, a Russian Su-27 dumped fuel on and clipped the propeller of an American MQ-9 over the Black Sea — forcing the US to crash the drone into the water to prevent capture.
- Reapers were used in 2022 to kill Al-Qaeda leader Ayman al-Zawahiri with a 'flying knives' R9X Hellfire variant that deploys six blades instead of explosives to minimize collateral damage.
- An armed Reaper costs roughly $30 million — making it dramatically cheaper than any manned strike aircraft despite being able to loiter for over a day.

## Seed paragraph

On January 3, 2020, a MQ-9 Reaper fired four Hellfire missiles into a convoy leaving Baghdad airport. The target was Qasem Soleimani — the second-most-powerful man in Iran. The drone that killed him was flown from a strip-mall building in Nevada by a pilot who would drive home to dinner that night. Three years later, a Russian fighter would clip a Reaper's propeller over the Black Sea, and America would deliberately crash the drone rather than let the Kremlin have it.
