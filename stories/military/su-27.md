# su-27
## Hooks
- Test pilot Viktor Pugachev debuted the 'Cobra' maneuver at the 1989 Paris Air Show — rearing the Su-27 to 110 degrees nose-up while flying forward, a feat the F-15 could not match.
- An unarmed Su-27 famously shouldered an American P-3 Orion away from Russian airspace over the Sea of Japan in 1987, with both wingtips colliding — neither aircraft was lost.
- Russian Su-27s and their Su-35 derivatives have been intercepting NATO aircraft in the Baltic almost daily since 2014 — sometimes flying within 5 feet of US reconnaissance aircraft.
- The Su-27 was designed with the central thrust line offset slightly to compensate for the natural yaw at high angles of attack — a design trick that made supermaneuverability possible.
- Ukrainian Su-27s engaged in air-to-air combat with Russian jets during the 2022 invasion — the first time the same airframe has fought itself in modern warfare.
- The Su-27 family has produced more variants than any other Russian fighter — including the Su-30, Su-33 (carrier), Su-34 (bomber), Su-35, and Su-37 — all sharing the same basic Flanker shape.

## Seed paragraph

On June 8, 1989, Soviet test pilot Viktor Pugachev pointed his Su-27's nose 110 degrees straight up while still flying forward — a maneuver Western engineers had thought was physically impossible. The crowd at the Paris Air Show didn't know what they were watching. Today, the Flanker's descendants have intercepted American aircraft within five feet over the Baltic, and Ukrainian Su-27s have shot down Russian Su-27s in the first flanker-on-flanker dogfights in history.
