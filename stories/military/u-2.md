# u-2
## Hooks
- U-2 pilots wear partial-pressure suits indistinguishable from spacesuits because at 70,000 feet, their blood would boil if the cockpit lost pressure.
- When Gary Powers was shot down over the Soviet Union in 1960, the CIA's cover story — a 'weather research plane gone off course' — collapsed when Khrushchev produced the pilot alive on television.
- The U-2 is so hard to land that every landing requires a chase car with another U-2 pilot calling out altitude as the plane settles onto its bicycle-style landing gear.
- A U-2 photographed Soviet missile launch sites in Cuba on October 14, 1962 — the photos triggered the Cuban Missile Crisis and brought the world within hours of nuclear war.
- The U-2 is still in active service today, more than 65 years after first flight, and was used as recently as 2023 to track the Chinese spy balloon over the United States.
- Lockheed Skunk Works built the first U-2 in just eight months in 1955, working out of a converted hangar in Burbank under Kelly Johnson — the same team that would later build the SR-71.

## Seed paragraph

On May 1, 1960, an American U-2 was shot down 70,000 feet over Sverdlovsk. The CIA told the world it was a weather plane. Then Khrushchev walked Gary Powers onto Soviet television. Two years later, another U-2 brought home the photographs that started the Cuban Missile Crisis. Sixty-five years after it first flew, the same airframe is still up there — tracking spy balloons over Montana in a partial-pressure suit that doubles as a spacesuit.
