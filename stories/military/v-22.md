# v-22
## Hooks
- The V-22 program killed 30 Marines during testing in the 1990s — including 19 in a single crash at Marana, Arizona in April 2000 — but the Pentagon refused to cancel it.
- An Osprey's prop-rotors are so massive they cannot land in airplane mode like a normal aircraft — if the engines won't tilt, the crew has to ditch.
- Each V-22 costs roughly $90 million and uses a proprietary 'interconnect drive shaft' running between the engines so one engine can power both rotors if the other fails.
- The Osprey was nearly killed by Dick Cheney in 1989 — when he was Secretary of Defense, he tried to cancel it four times, but Congress kept restoring the funding.
- In November 2023, an Air Force CV-22 crashed off Japan due to a gearbox failure, grounding the entire global Osprey fleet for months and reigniting safety concerns.
- Despite its troubled past, an Osprey can fly 250 knots — twice as fast as any helicopter — meaning Marines can be inserted 400 miles from an amphibious ship in under two hours.

## Seed paragraph

In April 2000, a Marine V-22 Osprey killed 19 men in a single crash in the Arizona desert. By then it had already taken 30 lives during testing. The Secretary of Defense had tried to cancel it four times. And yet the tiltrotor — half helicopter, half plane, all controversy — kept getting resurrected by Congress. Today it flies twice as fast as any helicopter, costs $90 million each, and still drops out of the sky often enough to ground the global fleet.
